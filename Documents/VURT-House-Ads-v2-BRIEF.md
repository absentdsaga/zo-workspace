# VURT HOUSE ADS — EXECUTIVE BRIEF
**Wave 1 campaign for Rielli, SinFiltros Nutrition, Trick Daddy Pots**
Prepared: April 24, 2026 · Distribution: Hilmon · Mark · Team

---

## What we're shipping

**Nine ads, three brands, one week.** Every spot is **6 seconds, 1080x1920 vertical, audio-on, non-skippable, with a clickable end card via VAST.** The spec is a captive 6-second window in the VURT app — not a scroll-stop. The viewer sees the whole ad. Every frame must convert.

This is a creative problem, not a video-volume problem. 6 seconds is the YouTube bumper unit and the Meta non-skippable unit — there is a **proven category-by-category playbook** for what wins at this length, and these spots follow it exactly:

- **Luxury swim:** water hook → body in piece → texture macro → end card. No VO. (Skims, Hunza G, Oseree.)
- **Wellness gummy:** founder face direct-to-camera + named problem → bottle → mouth shot → benefit → price/CTA. (Bloom, Goli, Olly.)
- **Celebrity cookware:** celebrity face → hand-off to product by 1.5s → money shot → price anchor. (HexClad x Ramsay, Caraway, Our Place.)

All three categories share the same rule: **one wedge per spot, three spots per brand, A/B-read in week one.**

## Creative direction (why these convert)

### Rielli
**Wedge:** Made in Miami by hand. Most luxury swim is Italy-by-team — Rielli is Arielle, in her atelier, sewing.

Three spots, all built on the category-winning luxury-swim beat structure (water → body → texture → end card):
- **"Worn"** — water hits fabric → Arielle back-turn walking into ocean → texture macro on tie/seam → end card.
- **"By Hand"** — needle through fabric ECU → garment on dress form rotating → tag reveal → end card (`BY HAND · MADE IN MIAMI`).
- **"Out"** — golden-hour body in piece → silhouette against backlit sun → end card (clean wordmark).

No VO across all three. Audio carries the brand (water, ambient, soft synth). Founder appears as silhouette/back-turn/hands — never face-forward speaking. Quiet-confidence register, no % off, no fake urgency.

### SinFiltros
**Wedge:** Latina-coded wellness without the pastel-girly slop. Real coffee, real mornings, named problems.

Three spots, each with a different wedge:
- **"Day One" (Moodmies)** — Valentina archival "PMS day one" face → bottle reveal → mouth shot → b-roll of her day → end card. Direct-response wedge, named-problem hook.
- **"Glow" (Glowmies)** — Valentina archival skin-touch → powder-blue bottle → mouth shot → skin-glow money shot + hair flip + nail tap → end card. Beauty-DR wedge, visible-result hook.
- **"La Rutina" (Moodmies + Glowmies stack)** — both bottles on counter ECU → Valentina tipping each into palm → two-gummy mouth shot → b-roll of her morning → split-color end card driving to home page. **AOV wedge** — bundle creative drives ~2× revenue per converted viewer vs single-SKU spots, sets up future bundle-PDP swap on Shopify.

Founder face stays real (her own archival IG/TikTok — video guy pulls directly from her public feed). Zero shoot day for Valentina — entire campaign builds from archive + AI b-roll + a half-morning product laydown. EN + ES caption/VO variants ship for each spot at the distribution layer (matched-language ad serving).

### Trick Daddy Pots
**Wedge:** The same pots from the show, $169.99, 15 pieces. The show is the asset and price is the hook.

Three spots, all built on the category-winning cookware beat structure (celebrity hand-off to product by 1.5s + money shot + price anchor):
- **"From the Show"** — archival Trick laugh/stir → hand-off to pot → non-stick glide money shot → 15-piece haul + price end card.
- **"The Set"** — pot hits flame → top-down haul → glide money shot → end card with `$169.99 · 4× $42.50 SHOP PAY`. Zero archive, zero talent dependency. Ships first as the control.
- **"Guests"** — archival guest reaction (Mike Epps, Ms Pat, Lil Duval) → Trick mid-stir → product money shot → price end card.

Price is the largest text on every Trick end card. Shop Pay Installments badge prominent (4× $42.50 reads as "less than $50/month" — the cold-traffic price-anchor wedge).

- **6 seconds exact, 1080x1920 vertical, audio-on, non-skippable, clickable end-card via VAST.**

## Production approach

### Founder appearances at 6s
- **Arielle:** on-camera as silhouette / back-turn / hand work. Real body, real Miami, real piece — but no dialogue burden. 60–90 min phone shoot covers all three Rielli spots. If atelier + beach access blocked, all three spots are 100% AI-generatable from real product stills.
- **Valentina:** archival TikTok flashes, sourced by her from her own feed. Optional 60-second phone VO. Zero crew shoot required.
- **Trick:** archive-only. Half-morning product-laydown is the only IRL shoot for the entire brand.

### No paid external creators, no paid models
No seeded creators, no influencer spend, no licensed talent. Founder + archive + product + AI b-roll is the entire stack.

### AI share is high (~60–70% of frames)
At 6s, most screen time is macro + product + environment — exactly where AI video is already 1:1 with live in 2026. Founder beats and archive stay real. **Stack:** Veo 3.1 (primary b-roll) · Runway Gen-4.5 + Act-Two (performance extend) · Kling 2.5 (fallback for physics) · Seedance 2.0 (cheap-at-scale b-roll / prototyping) · Higgsfield Cinema Studio (shot-direction layer) · ElevenLabs v3 (VO cloning, with consent) · Suno v4 (music beds) · Magnific Video + Topaz (finishing) · Descript + Premiere Pro (assembly).

Full AI playbook + prompt patterns in the production brief.

## Why 6s converts for VURT

1. **Captive viewer.** Non-skippable + audio-on = the viewer sees the whole spot. 6s is the optimal length when you don't need to beat the scroll.
2. **End card gets ~17% of screen time.** The CTA surface is generously sized relative to the ad — clicks get a real hold window, not a 1-second flash.
3. **Low fatigue risk.** 6s is 1/5th the screen time of 30s, so the same audience can see a spot 5x before feeling it. Lets us rotate 3 spots per brand without wear-out.
4. **Founder moat still applies.** Arielle in her atelier at 6s still signals what a stock-model 6s never could. Valentina's direct-to-camera "PMS day one" still reads as her, because it is her.
5. **Measurable by format, not just by brand.** 3 variants per brand inside one week = real A/B signal, not just "how did Rielli do."

| Trick cooking-show rights denied | Ship Spot #2 ("The Set" — product-only, zero archive) as control. Spots #1 and #3 hold for rights resolution. |

| AI output quality below bar on a specific shot | At 6s we have only 3–4 AI shots per spot. Easier to regenerate. Budget one re-render day as contingency. |
| End card too tight for offer + price + URL | Trick keeps price + URL only on tightest variant. Rielli/SinFiltros end cards have no price — already lean. |
