## How to Post: Platform-by-Platform Rules

### TikTok Rules

- **Hook in the first 1.5 seconds.** The most dramatic, emotional, or shocking frame goes FIRST. If the viewer doesn't stop scrolling in 1.5 seconds, the clip is dead.
- **Cut at peak tension.** Never resolve the scene. Cut at the slap, the reveal, the betrayal. Leave them wanting more.
- **Text overlay on every clip:** "Watch the full episode on VURT" or "Full series streaming free on VURT"
- **Captions:** Short. Curiosity-gap energy. Examples:
  - "She wasn't supposed to find out like this."
  - "He thought nobody was watching."
  - "This is the moment everything changed."
  - Always end with: "Full episode on VURT. Link in bio."
- **Hashtags (max 5):**
  - Always: #VURT
  - Always: #MicroDrama or #VerticalCinema
  - Add 2-3 culture/genre tags: #ThrillerTok #TrueCrime #DramaTok #BlackCinema #IndieFilm
  - Add the title hashtag: #KillerStepdad #MiamiKingpins etc.
- **Pinned comment on every post:** "Link in bio to watch free"
- **Pin the top 3 performing videos to the profile.**
- **Never post with another platform's watermark.**
- **Posting times to test:** 7-9 AM, 12-1 PM, 7-10 PM ET

### Instagram Rules

- **Reels are NOT copy-pasted TikToks.** Instagram detects cross-posts and buries them (31-44% reduced reach). Re-edit each clip:
  - Adjust text placement for IG's UI (different safe zones than TikTok)
  - Create a cover frame for the grid (keep the grid looking cinematic and cohesive)
  - Remove any TikTok watermark
- **Stagger 24-48 hours behind TikTok.** Never post the same clip on TikTok and IG the same day.
- **Hashtags go in the caption, not the overlay.** Use 10-15 hashtags at the end of the caption.
- **The #1 metric on Instagram is DM shares.** Make content people want to send to friends. "You NEED to see this" energy.
- **Reply to every DM.** Every single one. For the first 6 months, this is how you build 1:1 relationships that compound.
- **Stories (5-10/day):**
  - Polls: "Who's the villain -- A or B?"
  - Countdown stickers to new drops
  - "Tag someone who needs to see this"
  - BTS photos from set
  - Repost any fan content or tags
  - Link sticker to myvurt.com on every 3rd story

### Facebook Rules

- **Same vertical clips, 2-day stagger behind TikTok.**
- **Auto-captions are critical.** Facebook auto-plays muted. If there are no captions, nobody watches.
- **Slightly older audience.** Less meme-y, more narrative hooks. Let the drama speak.
- **Cross-posting from IG is acceptable here** (unlike TikTok-to-IG). Facebook's algorithm is less punishing about this.

### YouTube Shorts Rules (Week 2+)

- **Title card in first 3 seconds.** YouTube is search-driven. The title needs to be readable.
- **Title IS the caption.** Make it keyword-rich: "Killer Stepdad - When She Finds Out The Truth | VURT"
- **Let scenes breathe slightly longer than TikTok cuts.** YouTube rewards watch time more than engagement bait.
- **Subscribe prompt overlay** at the end.

### LinkedIn Rules (Ari / Dioni / Mark -- NOT Intern)

- **This is B2B. Not consumer content.**
- **Do NOT post clip baits on LinkedIn.** LinkedIn users are not there to discover entertainment apps.
- **What to post:** Industry insights, milestones, TechCrunch coverage, the culture-vs-genre thesis, creator economics ("50/50 rev split, here's why"), team updates, new director/creator signings
- **Cadence:** 2-3x/week from founder accounts (Ted, Mark, Dioni). 1-2x from VURT company page.
- **Format:** Native text posts with images or carousels. LinkedIn carousels get 7% avg engagement.

### X/Twitter Rules (@myvurt)

- **Conversation layer.** React to press, cultural moments, industry news.
- **Announce new director/creator signings** (from Ted) -- these are hype-building moments. Frame as "Welcome to VURT" with the creator's handle tagged.
- **Quote-tweet press coverage** with founder perspective.
- **Reply to anyone talking about VURT, microdrama, or vertical cinema.**
- **Do NOT just cross-post clip baits.** X is text-first. Use clips sparingly, lead with commentary.

## Clip Sequencing: The 5-Clip Arc (Per Title)

Each title has 5 clips in Frame.io. These are not random scenes -- they follow a tension arc designed to pull viewers deeper and ultimately drive them to the app.

**When picking which clip to post first from each title, use this framework:**

| Clip | Archetype | What to Pick | Purpose |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Clip 1 (Week 1) | **The Hook** | Pure conflict. The most dramatic, scroll-stopping moment. Cut at peak tension -- the slap, the reveal, the betrayal. | Stop the scroll. "What happens next?" drives app install. |
| Clip 2 (Week 2) | **The Escalation** | Stakes get higher. A new conflict layer or character enters. | Reward returning viewers, deepen investment. |
| Clip 3 (Week 3) | **The Twist** | Unexpected turn. Betrayal, secret revealed, tables turned. | Shareability moment -- "you need to see this." |
| Clip 4 (Week 3-4) | **The Confrontation** | Characters collide. The emotional peak of the story. | Drives comments and debate. "Who's wrong here?" |
| Clip 5 (Week 4+) | **The Edge of Resolution** | Almost resolves. Comes closest to an answer but cuts before the payoff. | Final push to app -- "only way to see how it ends is on VURT." |

**The rule: Never fully resolve the conflict in any clip.** Every clip leaves the viewer needing more. The resolution lives on the app. That is the entire conversion engine.

**How Dioni picks Clip 1 for each title:**
1. Go into the Frame.io link for that title
2. Watch all 5 clips
3. Pick the one with the strongest hook in the first 2 seconds -- the moment that would make you stop scrolling
4. That is Clip 1. The remaining 4 clips get sequenced as Escalation, Twist, Confrontation, Edge of Resolution across Weeks 2-4.

## The Cross-Platform Stagger (Critical)

**Never post the same clip on two platforms the same day.**

When a clip drops on TikTok:
- **Day 1:** TikTok (lead platform)
- **Day 2:** IG Reels (re-edited for IG safe zones + cover frame)
- **Day 3:** FB Reels (auto-captions, narrative hook)
- **Day 4+:** YouTube Shorts (title card, slightly longer cut)

This maximizes each platform's algorithm freshness window and prevents audience fatigue for people following on multiple platforms.

| Metric | Week 1 Target | Month 1 Target |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| TikTok followers | 100+ | 1,000+ |
| IG followers | +50 | +500 |
| Best single clip views | 1,000+ | 10,000+ |
| Shares per clip (avg) | 10+ | 50+ |
| Saves per clip (avg) | 5+ | 25+ |
| App installs from social | Any measurable signal | Consistent trend |

### What Matters Most (In Order)

1. **Shares/sends** -- people sending clips to friends. This is the #1 growth signal.
2. **Saves** -- people bookmarking to watch later. Algorithm loves this.
3. **Completion rate** -- did they watch the whole clip? If not, the hook is weak.
4. **Comments** -- conversation = algorithm fuel.
5. **Likes** -- least important but still a signal.
6. **Follower count** -- vanity metric. Track it but don't obsess.

## The 72-Hour Rule

**Do NOT judge a clip's performance before 72 hours.** The algorithm needs time to find the audience. No panic-posting. No pulling clips early.

After 72 hours, classify each clip:

- **Winner (top 20% by views/shares):** Repost on secondary platforms, create follow-up content, boost if budget allows
- **Solid (middle 60%):** Normal rotation, move to next clip
- **Underperformer (bottom 20%):** Analyze why (weak hook? wrong time? genre mismatch?). Re-cut or retire.
