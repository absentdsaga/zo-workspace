# VURT Social Video Editor Guide

_Version 1.1 — 2026-04-23. Written for the person cutting the clips, not the caption writer. Every rule below is tied to numbers from our full published set: all 41 @myvurt TikToks (scrape), all Meta Graph IG/FB history, all 42 YT uploads, and the full Frame.io inventory (371 videos, 30 Posted / 18 Approved). When the data changes, the rules change._

---

## 0. First principles (read before you open Premiere)

**The job:** turn a long-form VURT show into a 15–90 second vertical cut that makes a stranger save, rewatch, or share it. Not "trailer-style." Not "highlight reel." One emotional beat, one face, one line.

**The KPI that matters (TikTok):** Save rate.
- Save % = saves / views. Over 1.0% = cloneable. Under 0.5% = don't repeat the structure.
- Save > Like. A like costs nothing. A save means the viewer thinks they'll come back to this or send it to someone. That's a retention signal the algorithm rewards.
- Like-rate over 6% is table stakes; if likes are under 6% the hook failed.
- Watch-time ratio (avg watch / duration) is the tiebreaker. Target 0.9+ on clips under 30s, 0.7+ on 30–60s.

**The KPI that matters (IG Reels):** Reach.
- Our best organic Reels reached 5,917 (FS "The rule") and 3,887 (SLAB "party planner"). Our worst brand posts reached 40–200. The delta is 20–140×. Character-rooted clip > brand design post. Every time.

**The KPI that matters (YouTube Shorts / under-3-min catalog):** Sessions that lead to watch-time.
- Our breakouts are sub-3-minute catalog clips (CBD 2:30 = 166K, SLAB 0:56 = 74.7K, SLAB 1:01 = 40.5K). Full episodes do not win on YT. Cut standalone scenes.

**What you can ignore:** Facebook organic reach and "FB algorithm tricks." FB 7-day is **98.2% paid** (439K paid / 8K organic of 447K views). The reach on FB comes from Tarik's ads, not from what you cut. Ship cleanly, don't chase FB virality.

## 1. What the data says wins — and the WHY

### The four organic TikTok winners (save rate ≥ 1%)

| 1 | CBD | EP29 Tatyana Ali "waiting for Spence" | 1,135 | 7.7% | **2.47%** | ~18s |
| 2 | SLAB | "told the judge he was a client" | 569 | 9.5% | **1.41%** | ~22s |
| 3 | FS | "father announces your future at dinner" | 283 | 6.7% | **1.06%** | ~20s |
| 4 | SLAB | "Uncle 3D little league pep talk" | 691 | 9.7% | **1.01%** | ~25s |

**Boosted clips are still part of the organic reference set.** SLAB "He tried to stand up" (380K views) and KIH "You did this" (189.6K views) were TikTok Boosted, but they were boosted *because* they were already outperforming organically — that's why Dioni put spend behind them. Study their cut structure the same way you study the unboosted winners. What you can't do is use their final view count as the organic benchmark: the 380K and 189.6K numbers include paid reach on top of whatever the organic ceiling was. Compare them on save rate and like rate, not raw views.

### The pattern the winners share

1. **Opening punch in the first 1.5 seconds.** Line of dialogue, not a title card. No logo, no "previously on," no slow pan. First frame = face + the hook line's first word already on screen as the clip starts.
2. **Female lead confronting a family figure.** 3 of 4 winners. CBD Tatyana confronts dad-figure. FS daughter confronts father at dinner. SLAB courtroom confrontation. Internal family stakes beat external action. This isn't a demographic choice — it's a stakes choice.
3. **Specific receipts, not general drama.** "Told the judge he was a client" > "there was a courtroom scene." "Waiting for Spence" > "she's heartbroken." Specificity is what gets saved — viewers save the dialogue, not the vibe.
4. **Single-face framing held long.** The winners stay on one face for the core beat. Don't cut to reaction shots mid-line unless the reaction IS the punch. Cutting around kills the save.
5. **Don't close the wound.** End on the cut, the look, the unanswered line — not the resolution. If the viewer gets closure, they don't need to come back. Save = "I need to finish this." If you finish it for them, no save.

### What kills engagement (don't ship these)

- Director/talking-head explainers. Dead every time.
- Carousel "here's 5 reasons to watch." Dead.
- Trailer cuts (multi-scene montage with music bed). Dead as TikTok/IG Reels; fine as a paid-ad creative, not as organic.
- Opening with the VURT logo. Logo belongs in the last 2 seconds, not the first.
- Subtitles that cover the actor's mouth. Covers the face = kills save rate.

## 2. Per-platform editorial specs

### TikTok (@myvurt)

- **Aspect:** 9:16, 1080×1920.
- **Duration sweet spot:** 15–30s for a confrontation beat; up to 60s if the scene needs to breathe. Over 60s → save rate tanks.
- **Safe zones:** top 220px = UI + our profile overlay. Bottom 480px = caption + UI. Keep the face centered vertically within the middle 1000px.
- **Upper-third text overlay:** Burn the clip's hook line (shortened, ~4–6 words max) into the top third. WHY: the TikTok profile grid crops to square and strips captions — without an upper-third overlay, nobody scanning @myvurt's profile knows what the clip is. Winning clips have it, losers don't.
- **Cover frame:** Pick a frame with the actor's face in mid-expression, not closed-mouth neutral. The cover frame = the profile grid thumbnail = the second-chance hook.
- **Subtitles:** Burned in, sans-serif, white with black stroke, max 2 lines at a time, positioned middle-bottom (above UI, below face). Auto-subtitles from the VURT captions workflow, then hand-tuned for line breaks.
- **Audio:** Keep the original dialogue as the dominant track. Don't layer trending audio — VURT's edge is the dialogue IS the hook. Trending-audio lip-syncs underperform on our shows.
- **End card:** Last 1–2 seconds. VURT logo bottom-right + "Streaming free on VURT" small text. Not a full-screen logo card — that's where viewers drop.

### TikTok Stories (visual-only)

- Same 9:16, 1080×1920, no sound assumption (Stories often play muted).
- **No dialogue-driven cuts** — pick the most visually striking frame-sequence from the scene (tears, slow turn, slap).
- Burn the key line as a full-screen title card.
- 5–10 seconds. Stories are a glance format.

### Instagram Reels

- Same 9:16 master as TikTok, but:
- **Remove the TikTok watermark** if the source was exported from TikTok. IG downranks it.
- **Reshoot the cover frame** — IG grid is a different crop (4:5 preview). Test the cover in both grid and feed preview.
- IG Reels duration sweet spot: 15–60s. Same rules as TikTok.
- No upper-third overlay needed on IG — IG captions show above the grid preview, so the profile-grid problem doesn't exist.

### Instagram Stories (3-frame funnel)

- Frame 1 (hook): The opening punch of the clip, 5s, with a bold line of text and the sticker-swipe pointing to Frame 2.
- Frame 2 (payoff): The emotional beat, 10s.
- Frame 3 (CTA): "Watch the full scene free on VURT" with the link sticker → myvurt.com (web, NOT app store — per VURT rule).
- WHY 3 frames: IG Story retention drops ~25% per frame. Three is the max before falloff kills the CTA.

### Facebook

- **Aspect:** 9:16 upload — FB now plays vertical in feed. Do NOT pad to 16:9 black bars.
- **Duration:** Same as TikTok master (15–60s).
- **Caption with clickable link:** myvurt.com/[show]. FB is the only major platform where the link in the caption is clickable from feed without friction. Use it.
- **Reality check:** FB organic reach is noise (8K views / week across all posts). The reach comes from paid ads. Cut cleanly, don't obsess. Your FB job = make sure the clip exists on FB for paid-media retargeting, not chase organic virality.

### YouTube Shorts

- 9:16, under 60 seconds.
- **Title is searchable** (unlike TikTok/IG). Put the show name and the emotional beat in the title: "Tatyana Ali's breakdown in Come Back Dad | VURT"
- **Description should include the full show title, cast handles, and a myvurt.com link.** YT discovery is search + related, not FYP. Treat the description like SEO copy, not a caption.
- **End screen:** Last 3 seconds can host a subscribe + link overlay. Use it.

### YouTube long-form (catalog clips, 1–3 min)

- Separate product from Shorts. This is where our actual YT breakouts live (166K, 74.7K, 40.5K on single-scene clips).
- 16:9, 1920×1080.
- Scene-level cuts: one continuous scene, no trailer-style montage.
- Title format: "[Scene description] | [Show name] | VURT"
- Thumbnail: single face, high contrast, ~2-3 words of overlay text, bright color accent. YT thumbnails are a separate craft — if you're not confident, flag it and someone else cuts thumbs.

## 3. Cover frame / thumbnail discipline

A 40% save-rate delta has been observed between "good cover" and "lazy first-frame default." WHY: on TikTok profile grid and IG Explore, the cover IS the ad. If the cover is a closed-mouth neutral or a logo, nobody clicks in.

**Rules:**
- Pick a frame 2–4 seconds in, not the first frame.
- Actor's face, mid-expression (tear, laugh mid-snap, jaw-drop, glare).
- Eyes visible. Looking at someone or into camera.
- No text, no logo on the cover frame itself — text goes in the upper-third overlay (which is frame 1 of the clip, separate from the cover thumbnail).
- For shows with multiple episodes cut to social: vary the covers. Same-show same-cover = viewers think it's the same clip and scroll past.

## 4. Frame.io tag workflow (ground truth)

Every finished clip gets a Status tag by Dioni in Frame.io. Those tags are authoritative — not filenames, not folder location.

| Status | What it means | Editor action |
|---|---|---|
| **Posted** | Live on at least one platform | Don't re-cut. Archive in your project folder. |
| **Approved** | Cleared review, ready to go out | This is the posting queue. |
| **Needs Review** | Cut is done, waiting on feedback | Fix what's flagged and re-upload. |
| **Feedback Given** | Notes left, blocked on your revision | Read the notes, cut v2, resubmit. |
| **Denied** | Rejected. Don't resurrect. | Move on. Don't try to rescue. |
| _Untagged_ | Mostly episode masters, some forgotten cuts | Ignore unless Dioni flags it. |

**Important:** Social cuts can come from episode masters in the show root, not just `Social Media Clips/`. Example: the #1 organic TikTok winner (CBD EP29 Tatyana Ali) was cut from `VURT_COMEBACKDAD_EP29.mp4` in the CBD show root. Scan the full show folder, not just the social subfolder.

**Posted Frame.io duration ≠ posted social duration.** Dioni may trim/re-edit the cut after pulling it. If you see a mismatch between Frame.io length and what actually went up, that's expected, not a bug.

## 6. The paid / organic distinction — don't conflate them

The distinction is about **what number you trust**, not whether to ignore the clip. A boosted clip is still a clip — its structure is still worth studying. What changes is which metrics are trustworthy:

- **View count on a boosted TikTok** — inflated by paid reach on top of organic. Don't benchmark against it.
- **Save rate and like rate on a boosted TikTok** — still meaningful, because they're ratios against total views (paid + organic). A 2% save rate on a boosted clip = viewers who were served it (paid or not) liked it enough to save.
- **FB video views** — ~98% of FB reach is paid (Simple Social / Tarik's ad spend). Don't infer "FB loves this clip structure" from FB view totals. Use save / comment / share rates instead, or ignore FB as a signal entirely.
- **Meta-ads managed creative** — same principle. The cut may be strong; the view count is bought.

The two boosted TikToks that matter:
- **SLAB "He tried to stand up" (380K views)** — boosted *because* it was trending organically first. Study the cut.
- **KIH "You did this" (189.6K views)** — boosted for the same reason. Study the cut.

Ask Dioni for the pre-boost performance window if you want the pure organic signal. He'll know the shape of what earned the boost decision.

Rule of thumb: when you're choosing which clips to clone the structure of, sort by **save rate + like rate**, not views. That works across boosted and unboosted clips equally.

## 7. Cut checklist (run before you submit to Frame.io)

- [ ] First 1.5 seconds: face + hook word. No logo, no title card, no pan-in.
- [ ] Upper-third overlay text on TikTok cut (hook line, ≤6 words).
- [ ] Cover frame selected (face mid-expression, eyes visible, 2–4s in, not frame 1).
- [ ] Subtitles burned in, don't cover mouth, ≤2 lines at a time.
- [ ] Ends on the cut/question, not the resolution.
- [ ] Runtime: under 60s for TikTok/IG/Shorts; under 3:00 for YT catalog.
- [ ] Audio: original dialogue dominant, no trending audio overlay.
- [ ] VURT logo bottom-right last 1–2s only.
- [ ] 9:16 exports for TikTok / IG Reels / FB / YT Shorts; separate 16:9 for YT long-form.
- [ ] Filename follows the existing convention: `VURT_[SHOW]_[Descriptor]-[Edit##]-v[n].mp4`
- [ ] Uploaded to the right Frame.io folder (`Social Media Clips/` for new social cuts, or show root if it's an episode master pull).
- [ ] No TikTok watermark on the IG/FB/YT exports (if the source was rendered out of TikTok).
