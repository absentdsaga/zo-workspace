## Read this first (3 min)

You're producing **9 ad spots across 3 brands** for VURT's in-app ad slot. Five-day turnaround.

**Format specs for every spot (locked):**
- **6 seconds exact.** Not 5.8. Not 6.2. **6.0.**
- **1080x1920 vertical.**
- **Audio-on, non-skippable.**
- **Clickable end-card via VAST** — end card gets the last ~1 second, full-frame click target.

**The creative philosophy at 6s — copy the category winners, not the 30s shrink-down:**

6 seconds is the YouTube bumper unit and the Meta non-skippable unit. There is a proven, category-by-category playbook for what converts at this length. Every spot in this campaign follows the playbook for its category:

| Brand category | Winning beat structure | Sound design |
|---|---|---|
| **Luxury swim** (Rielli) | water hook (0–1s) → body in piece (1–3s) → texture money shot (3–5s) → end card (5–6s) | Ambient water + soft synth. **No VO.** |
| **Wellness gummy** (SinFiltros) | founder face direct-to-camera + named problem (0–0.5s) → bottle reveal (0.5–2s) → mouth shot (2–3.5s) → benefit b-roll (3.5–5.5s) → price/CTA (5.5–6s) | Founder voice (≤5 words) + warm beat |
| **Celebrity cookware** (Trick) | celebrity face + signature gesture (0–1s) → hand-off to product (1–2.5s) → money shot (2.5–4.5s) → set + price anchor (4.5–6s) | Sizzle hits hard at the cut + Miami bass bed. Voiced price stamp optional |

**Three things that matter most:**
1. **Founder beats stay real** (not AI-generated likeness). Silhouette / back-turn / archival flash is the entire founder exposure at 6s — never AI-generate a known face.
2. **Price / offer / installments are on-screen, never voiced** — except Trick, where price is optionally voiced as a 2-word stamp.
3. **No slop copy.** No "no filters, no extremes." No "unlock your glow." If you write it in a lower third, burn it.

## 5. AI generation playbook for 6s

At 6s, AI is doing **most** of the frames. Updated playbook:

### Which model for what

| Shot type | Primary | Fallback | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product in motion (fabric, gummy, pot on flame) | **Seedance 2.0** | Veo 3.1 | Seedance is dirt cheap (~$0.05/5s) for iteration; Veo for hero render. |
| Environmental b-roll (atelier, counter, Miami skyline) | **Veo 3.1** | Kling 2.5 | Veo wins on environmental fidelity and natural light. |
| Anonymous-body silhouette (Arielle back-turn if blocked) | **Veo 3.1** | Kling 2.5 | Body physics — Veo and Kling both solid; Veo reads slightly cleaner. |
| Macro textures (fabric, oil, steam, liquid) | **Seedance 2.0** | Kling 2.5 | Cheap-at-scale iteration; macros are AI-native. |
| Texture money shot (Rielli — fabric tie/seam) | **Veo 3.1** | Seedance 2.0 | Hero conversion frame — use the higher-quality model, finish in Magnific. |
| Skin glow money shot (SinFiltros — Glowmies) | **Veo 3.1** with real face reference | — | Face reference must be Valentina's. Never generate face cold. |
| Non-stick glide money shot (Trick — pan release) | **Live phone shoot** | Veo 3.1 with real food reference | Live wins for food physics. AI fallback only if shoot blocked. |
| Archival face (Trick, Valentina) | **NONE — real archive only** | — | Never generate a known face. Right-of-publicity trap. |
| Shot with complex camera move | **Higgsfield Cinema Studio** (wraps Veo/Kling/Sora/Seedance) | Direct Veo | Higgsfield stacks cinematic camera moves Veo/Kling struggle with directly. |

### Prompt patterns that work at 6s

Every 6s spot uses 2–4 AI clips of 1–2s each. Keep prompts short and specific:

1. **Feed a real product still as image-to-video reference** when available (Veo 3.1, Kling 2.5, Seedance 2.0 all support this). Quality jump is night and day.
2. **Constrain the camera move.** "Static locked-off" or "slow dolly in 6 inches per second." Never "cinematic" or "dynamic."
3. **Reference the grade + lens.** "Shot on A7S III, 50mm, natural window light, slight film grain, warm tungsten accent." Never say "cinematic" — that triggers the generic-stock-footage look.
4. **Generate in 2–4s clips, not 6s.** Longer clips drift on physics. Stitch 2–3 short clips in the timeline.
5. **Finish with Magnific Video** for AI source; Topaz for live source. Never skip the finish — untreated AI output reads as generated.

## 6. What to AI vs what to shoot real (6s version)

When in doubt: shoot real for founder beats and food physics, AI everything else.

### AI handles at 6s (generate these, skip the shoot day)
- All product-in-motion macros (fabric, gummy, pot on flame, oil shimmer, steam)
- All atelier/kitchen environmental b-roll (wide shots, counters, doors opening, racks)
- All "anonymous body" lifestyle cuts where face is never forward (hand reaching, bare feet, back turns, silhouettes)
- All text-on-black opens — render as plain frames in After Effects, not AI-generated
- Miami skyline, ocean, golden-hour light — zero tells

### AI is close but not there yet — use with caution at 6s
- Hands doing skilled work longer than 2s (stitch-through shots can drift — keep AI macro work under 2s; go real above that)
- Food physics longer than 2s (sizzle, glide, slide — phone-shoot beats AI every time, even at 6s)
- Known-face cameos — **never AI**, even at 6s. Archive only for Trick, Arielle, Valentina.

### Shoot real — AI can't fake this
- Arielle's silhouette / back-turn / hands across all 3 Rielli spots (~8 seconds total live footage). 60–90 min phone shoot.
- Trick product laydown — half-morning with actual 15-piece set, induction burner, food for the glide beat, phone camera.
- Any archival flash (Valentina TikTok direct-to-camera, Trick show + guests) — pulled, not generated.

### Campaign-by-campaign AI/live split (6s)

**Rielli — ~60% AI, 40% live:**
- AI: fabric macros, atelier wide shots, dress-form rotation, golden-hour environment, silhouette body if no atelier access.
- Live: Arielle's silhouette/back-turn/hand beats across 3 spots (~8 seconds total live footage needed).
- Shoot cost: **one 60–90 min phone shoot.** If atelier blocked: **100% AI**, zero shoot.

**SinFiltros — ~95% AI, 5% live:**
- AI: all product beats, gummy drop, bottle macros, morning counter, mouth-shot from face reference, benefit b-roll (skin glow ring-light, hair, nails).
- Live: zero Valentina shoot — she delivers archival TikTok clips and (optional) phone VO. Archival is live footage by definition.
- Shoot cost: **zero shoot days for Valentina** + half-morning product laydown for bottle/counter beats. Valentina's time ask: **under 10 minutes** (consent + optional 60-sec VO). Video guy pulls all archival founder beats from her public IG/TikTok feed directly.

**Trick Daddy Pots — ~30% AI, 70% live:**
- AI: transitional b-roll (steam, environmental inserts, lid lift if not shot live).
- Live: half-morning product laydown + pot-on-flame + glide money shot. Archival cooking-show footage is live (not AI).
- Shoot cost: **half-day product laydown.** Zero Trick on-camera time except optional 60-sec phone VO.

## 7. Anti-slop checklist (run this before final delivery)

Before exporting any final cut, check each spot against:

- [ ] No "no filters, no extremes" or symmetric-corporate copy anywhere
- [ ] No "empower," "unlock," "finally," "reimagine," "redefine," "elevate" in any text
- [ ] No stock-music bed (use Suno v4 custom generation or licensed track)
- [ ] No fake percent-off, no fake urgency, no fake scarcity copy
- [ ] End card is readable at arm's length on a phone
- [ ] **Trick end cards: `$169.99` is the largest text on the frame**
- [ ] URL is clickable — tested in VAST preview, not just internal editor
- [ ] No VO mentions price math (e.g., "forty-two-fifty times four")
- [ ] No voiced tagline that sounds like a TV ad — Trick stamps stay ≤5 words and specific
- [ ] Archival clips have consent + rights on file
- [ ] Voice clones have written consent + ElevenLabs in-product attestation
- [ ] **AI-generated faces only used with real face reference + consent** — never cold-generated
- [ ] Spot runs **exactly 6.0 seconds** (not 5.8, not 6.2)
- [ ] **Texture/money shot beat is present** (Rielli 3.0–5.0s, SinFiltros 3.5–5.5s, Trick 2.5–4.5s) — if missing, the spot is a slideshow, not an ad
