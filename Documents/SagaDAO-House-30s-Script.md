## EDIT NOTES FOR SKYRISE

### The Balance

This video has to do two things at once: prove SagaDAO House throws the best party you weren't at, and prove it's a real content production house. The viewer should walk away wanting the vibes AND understanding that this is where creators make content with brands. Not a conference. Not a meetup. A house full of creators, cameras, and culture, where sponsors get dedicated product-centered content baked into the experience.

### Picking Shots

Source footage is all Abu Dhabi SagaDAO House content from Breakpoint Dec 2025. You have a lot. Here's how to choose:

**Crowd density shots** are your most important asset. A packed room is proof. It tells the viewer this wasn't a 20-person side event in a hotel suite. 521 people came through. Show that. These shots anchor the party energy and establish scale before anything else.

**Production setup and cameras rolling** are the second most important. This is the product. Lights, rigs, the confessional room, Skyrise gear visible in frame. When a viewer sees a full production setup inside a house party, they understand what SagaDAO House actually is: a content studio disguised as the best party at the conference. This is where creators sit down with a sponsor's app, demo a product on camera, film a review, record a segment. The production infrastructure is the proof that this house creates real content for brands, not just Instagram stories.

**Candid creator moments** sell the talent. Creators laughing on camera, mid-interview in the confessional, reacting to something, being themselves. These shots tell the viewer: the house is full of people who make content for a living, and the cameras are always rolling. A sponsor watching this should immediately picture their product in one of those creator's hands during a filmed segment.

**Party energy and nightlife** is the hook. This is what stops the scroll. Red cups, crowd going off, someone pouring shots, the energy peaking. But don't over-index on this. If the whole video is party footage, it reads as just a party. The party footage hits hardest when it's sandwiched between the production shots and the creator moments, because now the viewer understands: these creators are having the best night of their lives AND they're making content all week.

**Group photos and crew shots** are your bookends. The rooftop terrace group shot at 0:04 sets the tone: this is a crew, not a crowd. The callback at 0:24 closes the loop. These shots create the feeling of a tight community that you want to be part of, or want your brand in front of.

### Color Grade

Miami Vice palette. Teal, magenta, purple, warm skin tones. Match the sponsor deck: deep purple, neon pink, cyan. Push the halation on highlights. The color grade is the entire retro reference. Baker Street handles the 80s nostalgia through the music. The grade handles it through the visuals. No 80s stock footage, meme inserts, or retro cross-cuts needed. The real footage with this grade does more than any stock clip could.

### Typography

SagaDAO brand font throughout. No retro typeface swaps. White or cyan text on dark. Quote attributions in magenta or green to match the deck. Every text overlay should be readable in under one second. If you have to slow the edit to let someone read, the text is too long.

### Pacing

Version A rides the sax. The cuts are confident, not frantic. Let the rhythm of Baker Street dictate the edit points. The sax swells should line up with the biggest visual moments: the group shot reveal at 0:04, the party peak at 0:14, the skyline beauty shot at 0:17. Version B is the opposite. TikTok-native. Choppy, meme-speed, sensory overload. Both should feel like 30 seconds went by in 10.

### What This Video Does NOT Do

This video does not pitch sponsors. It does not mention sponsorship. It shows what the house is: a creator content house with world-class vibes. The product-centered content that sponsors get (creators using their app, demoing their device, filming dedicated segments) is a separate deliverable. This video is the culture reel. Its only job is to make the viewer think: "I need to be involved with this." The website link at the end does the rest.
