# VURT LinkedIn Intern Brief

**For:** LinkedIn content creator (graphics + copy)
**Last Updated:** April 3, 2026

---

## What LinkedIn Is For VURT

LinkedIn is **B2B only**. It is not a consumer channel. Nobody on LinkedIn is looking for their next show to binge. They ARE looking for:

- The next company to invest in
- The next partnership opportunity
- Industry trends that make them look smart when they share them
- Creators and talent worth knowing

**Your audience on LinkedIn:** investors, brand partners, potential distribution deals, filmmakers considering submitting to VURT, entertainment industry professionals, media journalists.

**Your audience is NOT:** viewers, fans, people who want to watch clips.

This means: **never post clip baits on LinkedIn.** No drama scenes. No "watch this" energy. That's for IG, TikTok, FB, and YouTube. LinkedIn gets the business story behind the content.

---

## The Brand Voice on LinkedIn

VURT's voice is **confident, not loud. Intentional, not stiff.**

### What That Sounds Like

- Short sentences. Clear intent. No fluff.
- Direct. Not corporate. Not salesy.
- You're talking to smart people in the entertainment and tech industry. Respect their intelligence.
- Culture is present but never performed. Don't try to sound "urban" or "edgy." Just be clear and real.
- **No hype words.** Never use: "excited to announce," "thrilled," "game-changing," "disrupting," "snackable," "viral," "influencer."
- **No em dashes (—).** Use periods or commas instead.

### What It Doesn't Sound Like

- "We're SO excited to announce..." (corporate cringe)
- "This is going to CHANGE THE GAME" (empty hype)
- "Check out this amazing clip!" (wrong platform, wrong audience)
- Long paragraphs that bury the point (LinkedIn rewards scannable posts)

### Example of the Right Tone

> The micro-drama market hit $11B last year. By 2030, it's projected at $26B.
>
> Every major streamer is now scrambling to figure out vertical. Disney launched Verts. Peacock is testing vertical NBA. Netflix is redesigning for mobile.
>
> VURT has been vertical-native since day one. We don't retrofit. We don't pivot. This is what we built.
>
> 100+ titles. Free to watch. Filmmakers keep their IP.
>
> myvurt.com

Notice: no hype, no "excited," no "game-changing." Just facts, positioning, and a clear CTA.

## Content Pillars (What to Post About)

Every LinkedIn post should fit one of these categories:

### 1. Market Data / Industry Trends
Why the micro-drama space matters. Numbers. Competitor moves. Format shifts.

**Example angles:**
- "$11B global micro-drama market. Here's who's winning and why."
- "Disney, Peacock, Netflix all went vertical this month. Here's what that means for indie creators."
- ReelShort revenue milestones, DramaBox growth, new entrants

**Where to find data:** `Documents/VURT-master.md` has all the market research, competitor data, and source citations.

### 2. Creator Economics / Platform Differentiation
What makes VURT different. The 50/50 split. No paywall. No aggregator. 48-72 hour turnaround. IP ownership.

**Example angles:**
- "Most platforms take 70%. We split 50/50 and don't require exclusivity."
- "48-72 hours from submission to streaming. No aggregator. No distributor middleman."
- "Filmmakers keep their IP. That's not a tagline. That's how the deal works."

### 3. Director / Creator Spotlights
Introducing the filmmakers behind VURT originals. Their background, their craft, their project.

**Example angles:**
- "VURT Filmmakers Spotlight: [Name]. Director of [Title]. Premiering [Date]."
- Focus on the filmmaker's credentials and vision, not the plot of the show.

### 4. Press Coverage / Milestones
Amplifying TechCrunch, REVOLT, and any other coverage. New title counts. Platform growth.

**Example angles:**
- Share press links with a founder perspective on what the coverage means
- Milestone markers: "100+ titles at launch," new partnership announcements

### 5. Team / Culture
Behind-the-scenes of building VURT. Not BTS of filming. BTS of the company.

**Example angles:**
- Team at events (99 Jamz, industry panels)
- Hiring announcements
- Strategic partnerships

## Graphic Design Guidelines

### Colors
| Name | Hex | Use |
|------|-----|-----|
| VURT Black | #0B0B0B | Primary backgrounds |
| Core Gold | #D4A63A | Logo gradient center, premium accents |
| Gold Highlight | #F5D27A | Subtle reflections, top-edge gradients |
| Deep Gold | #9C6B12 | Shadow areas, cinematic weight |
| Warm White | #F2F2F2 | Headlines, titles, body text on dark |
| Soft Gray | #9A9A9A | Metadata, captions, secondary text |

### Typography
| Use | Font | Fallback |
|-----|------|----------|
| Headlines | Neue Haas Grotesk | Inter |
| Body / UI | Inter | — |
| Accent (episode numbers, stats, metadata) | IBM Plex Mono | — |

**Rule:** If typography ever feels decorative, playful, or attention-seeking, it's not VURT. Clean and restrained always.

### Logo
- Full VURT wordmark + iconic "V" mark
- Always on high-contrast backgrounds
- Never stretch, skew, add shadows, outlines, or effects
- Never place on busy backgrounds without a solid/gradient overlay behind it

### Graphic Style
- **Cinematic, not flashy.** Think premium streaming brand, not startup promo.
- Dark backgrounds (VURT Black) with gold accents.
- Clean layouts. Lots of breathing room. Don't crowd the frame.
- Data visualizations should be simple and readable. No 3D charts, no gradients on bar graphs. Clean lines, VURT colors.
- Text on graphics should be minimal. If you need a paragraph, put it in the caption, not the image.

### What NOT to Do in Graphics
- No neon colors, bright gradients, or "tech startup" aesthetics
- No stock photo vibes
- No clip art or generic icons
- No busy patterns or textures competing with the content
- No rounded bubbly fonts
- No emojis on the graphic itself

## Post Formats That Work on LinkedIn

### 1. Single Image + Text Post (Default)
One strong graphic. Caption underneath. This is your bread and butter.

### 2. Carousel / Document Post
Multiple slides. LinkedIn carousels average ~7% engagement, which is high. Good for:
- Market data breakdowns (slide per stat)
- "How VURT works" explainers
- Creator spotlight series (slide 1: name/photo, slide 2: credits, slide 3: the project, slide 4: CTA)

### 3. Native Text Post (No Image)
Pure text. Works for thought leadership, founder perspectives, hot takes on industry news. Keep it scannable: short paragraphs, line breaks between ideas.

### 4. Article Link Post
Sharing press coverage or blog posts. Add your own caption framing why it matters. Don't just paste the link with no context.

## Caption Structure (LinkedIn)

```
[Opening line that earns the click to "see more"]

[2-3 short paragraphs of substance. Data, insight, or story.]

[Clear CTA or closing statement]

myvurt.com

#VURT #MicroDrama #IndieFilm #VerticalCinema
```

**The first line is everything.** LinkedIn truncates after ~150 characters with a "see more" link. That first line must make someone want to expand. Lead with a number, a bold claim, or a question.

**Good first lines:**
- "The micro-drama market hit $11B last year."
- "Every major streamer went vertical this month. Here's what nobody's talking about."
- "We split revenue 50/50 with filmmakers. Here's why."

**Bad first lines:**
- "We're excited to share some news about VURT!"
- "Check out our latest update."
- "Big things are happening."

### Hashtags
Keep to 3-5 max. Always include `#VURT`. Other options:
- `#MicroDrama` `#IndieFilm` `#VerticalCinema` `#StreamingPlatform`
- `#FilmmakerSpotlight` (for director posts)
- `#EntertainmentIndustry` `#MediaTech` (for industry trend posts)

Do NOT use consumer hashtags like `#DramaTok` `#WhatToWatch` on LinkedIn. Wrong audience.

## Quick Gut Check Before Posting

1. Would an investor read this and think "these people know what they're doing"?
2. Would a filmmaker read this and want to submit their project?
3. Does this sound like a real person or a marketing team?
4. Is the first line strong enough to earn the "see more" click?
5. Does the graphic look like it belongs on a premium streaming brand's page?

If any answer is no, revise before submitting.
