# PART 1 — SPONSOR-FACING

## Sponsorship Products

### 1. Title Sponsor — Show

You own a show. Name on it, integrated across every episode for the term.

- **Term:** `[N]`-week minimum, `[N]`-week renewal options
- **Includes:**
  - Brand integration in show open/close
  - `[X]` minutes of dedicated content per episode (product mention, demo, host read)
  - Logo/lockup in all clips, thumbnails, social cutdowns
  - Cross-promo mentions across the slate during sponsor weeks
  - On-chain performance dashboard (wallet connects, claims, attributed mints)
- **Best for:** Major launches, infra/protocol projects, ongoing brand alignment

### 2. Native Product Placement *(validated format)*

Your product appears organically in the content. Hosts use it on-camera. **This is the format SagaDAO House sponsors are repeatedly asking for more of.**

- **Term:** Per-episode, per-week, or season pass
- **Includes:**
  - Product physically/digitally present and used in the content
  - Host walkthrough or demonstration (scripted or unscripted)
  - On-screen call-out and link in description/community
  - Approved talking points (sponsor reviews, doesn't write)
- **Best for:** dApps, hardware, consumer products, anything that benefits from "shown, not told"

### 3. Run-of-Slate Ad

Single creative runs across the active slate that week.

- **Term:** Weekly buy
- **Includes:**
  - `[X]`-second pre-roll or mid-roll across all shows that week
  - Standardized creative spec
  - Aggregated reporting across the slate
- **Best for:** Awareness pushes, recurring brand presence, lower-commit testing

### 4. Activation / Launch Package

Synced coverage across the slate for a specific drop, mint, or event.

- **Term:** Single-event (typically `[N]`-day window)
- **Includes:**
  - Coordinated mention in every show airing during the window
  - Live coverage if applicable (stream, IRL event tie-in)
  - SagaDAO comms amplification (X, Discord, community channels)
  - On-chain attribution for the drop
- **Best for:** Token launches, NFT mints, dApp store debuts, IRL events

### 5. Treasury / Brand Alignment Deal

Multi-month brand partnership, DAO-level. Sponsor's project becomes a recurring presence; in return, deeper integration and ecosystem alignment.

- **Term:** `[N]`-month minimum
- **Includes:** Custom — usually a mix of products 1–4 plus DAO co-marketing, governance touchpoints, community channels, IRL event presence
- **Best for:** Solana ecosystem partners, infra projects, long-term brand bets

## Performance Kickers (priced separately, on top of base)

Crypto-native pricing the rest of the creator economy can't offer:

- Wallet connects from show traffic — `$[X]` per connect above `[threshold]`
- Claims/mints attributed to show — `[X]%` of mint revenue or flat `$[X]` per mint
- New Discord/community joins — `$[X]` per join above `[threshold]`
- Sponsor token kicker (sponsor-side cost only — see Payment Policy)

---

## Payment Policy

**SOL and USDC only.** No sponsor tokens accepted by SagaDAO or passed through to creators.

If a sponsor wants to pay in their own token, they swap to SOL or USDC at deal close on their side.

**Optional sponsor-token kicker:** A sponsor can elect to drop a token allocation to top-performing creators and/or top community testers as a bonus on top of the SOL/USDC deal. The DAO doesn't custody it — sponsor airdrops directly to nominated wallets at sponsor's cost. Skips DAO treasury risk entirely.

## The Deal Structure

**One model for everyone.** Creators own their gear and overhead is low — no production capital recoup needed.

### The Waterfall (every sponsor dollar, in order)

1. **Sales commission** — `[X]%` off the top, only if a broker (internal or external) sourced the deal
2. **Prize fund** — if SagaDAO is covering prizes for this specific deal, that amount is set aside before split
3. **Treasury cut** — **10% of gross** flows to SagaDAO DAO treasury (on-chain wallet, public)
4. **Net pool** — what's left, split between creator and DAO ops

### Net Pool Split (default)

- **Creator:** `[75]`**%**
- **DAO ops:** `[25]`**%**

Creator share is high because creators bring the gear, talent, and audience. DAO ops covers BD, distribution, infra, comms — the team making the slate possible. Treasury (the 10% above) is separate from ops.

## Worked Example — All Numbers Are Placeholders

| Line | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Sponsor deal | $10,000 |
| Sales commission (assume 15%) | \-$1,500 |
| Prize fund (assume DAO covers $1,000) | \-$1,000 |
| Treasury cut (10% of gross) | \-$1,000 |
| **Net pool** | **$6,500** |
| → Creator share (75% of net) | **$4,875** |
| → DAO ops share (25% of net) | **$1,625** |

Creator walks with $4,875 of a $10K deal. Treasury compounds $1,000. Ops gets $1,625. Sales got $1,500. Audience got a $1,000 prize fund.

If no broker and DAO doesn't cover prizes, creator share gets bigger:

| Line | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Sponsor deal | $10,000 |
| Sales commission | $0 |
| Prize fund | $0 |
| Treasury cut (10%) | \-$1,000 |
| **Net pool** | **$9,000** |
| → Creator share (75%) | **$6,750** |
| → DAO ops share (25%) | **$2,250** |

## Treasury Use — How the 10% Works

To make treasury cut land as a DAO signal, not just a fee:

- **Public on-chain wallet** — every deal flows visible. Address: `[WALLET]`
- **Quarterly community votes** on treasury use:
  - Community grants
  - IRL events (SagaDAO House, hackathon presence)
  - Dev work (validator ops, U$ER, future builds)
  - Emergency / opportunity fund
- Treasury never touches creator pay or ops budget. Strictly community-aligned spend.

This is what makes 10% feel like alignment rather than a tax.
