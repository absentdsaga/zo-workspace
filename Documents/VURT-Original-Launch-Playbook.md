### 4. Posting Schedule (1-Week Sprint)

**Platforms:** Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, YouTube Shorts (all active as of March 28, 2026)
**Posting model:** Each clip goes to ALL platforms the same day. No stagger.

| Day | All Platforms (IG + TT + FB + YT) | Stories (IG) |
|-----|-----------------------------------|--------------|
| Day 1 | Clip 1 | 3-5: polls, text teasers, clip repost |
| Day 2 | Clip 2 | Push Clip 1 + tease Clip 2 |
| Day 3 | Clip 3 | Push Clip 2 + tease Clip 3 |
| Day 4 | Clip 4 | Push Clip 3 + tease Clip 4 |
| Day 5 | Clip 5 | Push Clip 4 + tease Clip 5 |
| Day 6-7 | Stories only (rest before next title) | Wrap-up: best moments, polls, trailer drop |

**Clip cadence:** 1 clip per day across all platforms. Finish the core arc (5 clips) in 5 days.
**Remaining clips (6-10):** Become backfill. Drop 1 per day in gaps between the next title's clips, or on days when the next title doesn't have a drop scheduled.
**72-Hour Rule (runs in parallel):** After 72 hours, classify each clip: Winner / Solid / Underperformer. This tells you whether to SCALE (boost, remix, create follow-up) -- not whether to keep posting. Keep posting regardless.

### 6. Weekend / Between-Drops Push

Keep the energy up between clip drops:
- Stories: 3-5/day -- even without BTS, you have plenty of ammo:
  - Repost the clip with a poll sticker ("would you have done that?")
  - Screenshot your view count as social proof ("👀")
  - Text-on-black teaser: just a quote from the next clip, white text, no video
  - "Tag someone who needs to see this" sticker
  - Screenshot interesting comments/DMs and repost
  - Countdown sticker to next clip drop
- Reply to every comment and DM
- If collab invites were accepted, ride that wave
- Drop the trailer as a separate piece (it's not a clip, doesn't compete)
- Drop Clip 2 when it's ready (every 2-3 days). Don't hold content waiting for 72-hr data.
