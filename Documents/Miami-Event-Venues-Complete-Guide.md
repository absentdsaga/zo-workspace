# 7. SKILLS REQUIRED FOR MAXIMUM EVENT SALES

To sell across ALL of these venue types and maximize revenue, you need the following skill stack:

---

## A. VENUE KNOWLEDGE & MATCHMAKING
- **Spatial intelligence**: Ability to walk a client's vision into the right square footage, ceiling height, and flow
- **Portfolio fluency**: Know every venue's sweet spots, limitations, and hidden costs by heart
- **Competitive positioning**: Understand which venues compete for the same client segment and why yours wins

## B. CONSULTATIVE SALES & DISCOVERY
- **Needs assessment**: Ask the right questions — headcount, format, brand tone, AV needs, F&B style, load-in requirements
- **Solution architecture**: Don't just sell a room — sell the full production concept (layout, flow, tech, catering, parking logistics)
- **Budget engineering**: Work backwards from client ROI, not just rental fees. Frame venue cost as % of total event spend

## C. RELATIONSHIP CAPITAL
- **Hotel/venue GM relationships**: Direct lines to decision-makers for holds, pricing flexibility, and priority booking
- **Preferred vendor networks**: Curated AV, catering, décor, and production partners at each venue
- **CVB/DMO partnerships**: Greater Miami Convention & Visitors Bureau relationships for citywide bids and incentive packages

## D. TECHNICAL PRODUCTION LITERACY
- **AV & staging fluency**: Understand rigging points, power requirements, LED wall specs, sound system types
- **CAD/floor plan reading**: Interpret and create event layouts that maximize capacity and flow
- **Permitting knowledge**: Fire marshal capacity rules, noise ordinances, outdoor event permits, liquor licensing

## E. REVENUE OPTIMIZATION
- **Yield management**: Dynamic pricing based on seasonality (Art Basel, Super Bowl, F1, boat shows vs. summer)
- **Upsell architecture**: Package ancillary revenue (AV upgrades, premium catering, branding opportunities, parking passes)
