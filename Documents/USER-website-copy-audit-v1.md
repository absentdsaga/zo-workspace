# U$ER Website Copy Audit & Rewrite Guide
## From "cool landing page" to "money-printing conversion machine"

## PART 1: THE DIAGNOSIS

### What the site does well
- **Visual design is strong.** Dark theme, neon accents, robot mascots — it looks like a premium Web3 product. Don't touch the design language.
- **The 5-step pipeline is a genuine differentiator.** No competitor has this sequential filtering concept.
- **Sentinel AI section is impressive.** Smart bug triage, screenshot analyzer, conversational QA — this is real value.
- **SBT-gating is a moat.** 120K+ verified device holders is something UserTesting and BetaTesting literally cannot replicate.

### What's killing conversions

**Problem 1: The site talks to itself, not the buyer.**
The copy describes features in builder-speak ("Sequential Filtering", "SBT-Gated Entry", "Proven Tester Cohort") instead of outcomes the buyer cares about. A project founder scanning this page in 8 seconds needs to see: "We get real people to test your app and tell you what's broken, and they'll also leave reviews on the dApp Store."

**Problem 2: Too many words in the wrong places, not enough in the right places.**
The "Less NOISE. More SIGNAL" section has 2 paragraphs saying the same thing. Meanwhile, the most important thing — what the buyer actually gets delivered — is buried in a 6-card grid with tiny text.

**Problem 3: Zero tester-facing content.**
The site is 100% aimed at projects. But this is a two-sided marketplace. You need testers too. UserTesting has a whole "Get Paid to Test" toggle in the nav. BetaTesting has "Join as Beta Tester" prominently in the header. Your site has "Tester Login" — that's it. A Saga/Seeker holder landing on this page has no reason to sign up.

**Problem 4: Missing services = missing money.**
No mention of focus groups, KOL amplification, or consultation. These are your upsells — the difference between a $5K deal and a $15K deal. They don't exist on the page.

**Problem 5: The pricing form is buried and passive.**
The "Submit Your App" form at the bottom asks the buyer to pick from a dropdown of amounts ($5K–$30K). This is backwards. You should be telling them what things cost, not asking them to name their budget. It signals "we don't know what to charge" and anchors low.

## PART 4: PRICING & SCOPING STRATEGY

### Your question: "What way should I approach taking their money and appreciating scope?"

Here's the optimal model based on what works in B2B services:

### The "Anchor High, Close Fast" Framework

**Step 1: Show the tiers on the website (as above).** This pre-qualifies buyers. Anyone who fills out the form has already mentally accepted "$5K minimum." No more $500 tire-kickers.

**Step 2: The intake call is a scoping session, not a sales pitch.**
When a project fills out the form, you (or Daemon) get on a 20-min call. The script:
- "What are you launching?" (understand their app)
- "What do you need to know before launch?" (understand their pain)
- "Have you ever had a bad dApp Store review that hurt you?" (create urgency)
- "Based on what you're describing, the Growth package at $10K is the right fit. If you also want KOL amplification to drive more testers, that's an additional $2,500."

**Step 3: Invoice immediately after the call.**
Don't send a proposal doc. Don't wait for them to "think about it." Say: "I'll send the invoice now, and once payment hits we start the campaign within 48 hours." Speed is the key. The longer between call and payment, the lower your close rate.

**Step 4: The upsell happens AFTER the first campaign.**
Once you deliver the first report, that's when you pitch focus groups: "Your testers flagged 3 issues with onboarding. Want us to set up a focus group with your top 5 testers so you can dig deeper? That's $3K and we can have it running this week."

This is way more effective than trying to sell everything upfront. The first campaign builds trust. The upsell sells itself.

### Pricing psychology notes:
- **$5K minimum is your floor.** You already charged $1,500 when this was manual/community-driven. With the app, AI reports, and professional packaging, $5K is justified and probably still underpriced.
- **Don't show prices below $5K anywhere.** This anchors your value. If someone says "I only have $2K," you say "We can scope a smaller test round — let's talk about what makes sense." But the website never shows less than $5K.
- **USDC is smart.** Crypto-native projects expect to pay in USDC. It's faster than invoicing, no payment processor fees, and it signals you're in-ecosystem.
- **The jump from $5K to $10K should feel like a no-brainer.** The Growth tier includes consultation + 3x the testers. That's where most buyers should land. Design the tier so $10K feels like the obvious choice (this is the "decoy effect" — the Launch tier exists to make Growth look like a bargain).

## PART 5: SKILLS & SUBAGENTS THAT WOULD HELP

You asked what skills and subagents would make this better. Here's what I'd want:

### For Copy
1. **A/B testing copy agent** — Once the new copy is live, run split tests on headlines, CTAs, and pricing tier labels. Even small headline changes can 2x conversion rates.
2. **Competitor monitoring agent** — Scheduled agent that checks UserTesting, BetaTesting, Testlio, and any new Solana-native testing services monthly. Alerts you to pricing changes, new features, new copy angles.

### For Conversion
3. **Analytics/heatmap setup** — Install PostHog or Hotjar to see where visitors actually click, scroll, and drop off. Without data, all copy optimization is guessing.
4. **Testimonial collection agent** — After each campaign, auto-send a feedback request to the project team. Collect quotes. The single highest-leverage thing you can add to this site is 3-5 client testimonials.

### For Revenue
5. **Pricing optimization agent** — Track close rates by tier. If 80% of buyers pick Launch ($5K), the tiers need rebalancing. If most pick Growth ($10K), you can raise prices.
6. **Outbound sales copy agent** — Generate personalized outreach for dApp Store projects that don't have many reviews yet. "We noticed [App] has 3 reviews on the Solana dApp Store. Our testers can give you 50+ verified reviews in 2 weeks."

### For the Two-Sided Marketplace
7. **Tester engagement agent** — Send campaign notifications to Genesis Token holders. "New test round: [App Name]. $X in prizes. Connect your wallet to participate." This keeps the supply side active.
8. **Content/SEO agent** — Write blog posts and X threads about testing results (anonymized). "We tested 11 Solana dApps. Here's what the best ones have in common." This builds authority and drives organic traffic to both sides of the marketplace.
