Executive Summary

At 5% inflation / 10% MEV, Saga DAO is underperforming its revenue potential. We currently receive delegation from 6 stake pools totaling ~30K SOL in pool stake (plus ~40K SFDP and ~10K native). By dropping to 0/0, we unlock or increase delegation from every commission-sensitive pool on Solana, growing total stake from 80K to ~166K SOL.

The proposal:

  • Increase annual validator income from ~525 SOL to ~1,228 SOL (+134%)
  • Grow total stake from ~80K to ~166K SOL
  • Expand active pool delegations from 6 to 9
  • Break even within 18 days of the commission change

The core math: at 0/0 with 166K stake, block rewards + priority fees generate far more than the commission income we give up. Every 10K SOL of additional delegation produces ~97 SOL/year in per-slot revenue — revenue that goes 100% to the validator regardless of commission setting.

How Validator Revenue Actually Works

Validator revenue has four components:

Revenue Stream Who Gets It Scales With
Inflation commission Validator (commission %) Total stake × commission rate
Block rewards Validator (100%) Number of leader slots
Priority fees Validator (100%) Number of leader slots
MEV commission Validator (commission %) Number of leader slots × tip volume

Block rewards and priority fees go 100% to the validator regardless of commission setting. They only depend on how many leader slots you get, which scales linearly with stake.

At our current 80K SOL: - Inflation commission (5%): 1.27 SOL/epoch — this is what we’d give up - Block rewards + priority fees: 4.26 SOL/epoch — this scales with more stake - MEV commission (10%): 0.06 SOL/epoch — negligible

The entire inflation commission at 5% is worth 231 SOL/year. Meanwhile, every 10K SOL of additional pool delegation generates 97 SOL/year in block rewards + priority fees. We only need 24K more stake to break even — and we’d gain ~95K.
