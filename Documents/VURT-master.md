## COMPANY OVERVIEW

**What VURT is:** Micro-drama platform with a cultural identity moat — "Black creators, Black stories" in a $14B global microdrama market dominated by Chinese-backed platforms producing generic romance.

**Business model:** Ad-supported + product placement (vs. ReelShort/DramaBox pay-per-episode paywall model). Hypothesis: lower acquisition costs, longer user lifetime, less churn.

**Current audience:** Female, 40s (core). 18-30 demographic is the growth target — they expect participation, not just consumption.

**Competitive position:** No microdrama platform has interactive features. Zero community infrastructure across the entire category. None using clippable moments or content creators as distribution strategy. All running paid acquisition. VURT can be first on multiple fronts.

## STRATEGIC THESIS

### The System
These aren't separate initiatives — they're a connected flywheel:

1. **Conflict-resolution format** creates content that hooks people
2. **Distribution machine** turns that content into reach
3. **Community** turns reach into retention
4. **Interactivity** turns passive viewers into participants
5. **IRL activations** create organic content that feeds back into distribution
6. **Ad-supported model** makes economics sustainable without churn treadmill

Each revolution makes the next one bigger.

### IRL Amplification Theory (Dioni's Working Theory)
IRL experiences where creators from different communities collaborate with each other — and where superfans, fans, and community members are part of it — amplifies content and attention spread. The event is simultaneously:
- A production engine
- A creator collaboration engine (cross-pollinating audiences)
- A distribution event
- A community builder (fan-fan bonding, parasocial → real social)
- A conversion tool
- Digital participation layer means remote audience is co-creating the narrative

Each layer amplifies the others. IRL content is inherently more shareable because it's unscripted, multi-POV, and emotionally resonant.
