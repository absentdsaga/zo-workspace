## COMPANY OVERVIEW

**What VURT is:** Micro-drama platform with a cultural identity moat — "Black creators, Black stories" in a $14B global microdrama market dominated by Chinese-backed platforms producing generic romance. Name origin: "Vertical Urban."

**Soft launch:** February 23, 2026 (App Store + Google Play)
**Official launch:** March 17, 2026 (TechCrunch coverage)

**Tagline:** "Stories told OUR Way. Built for the Culture."
**Subtitle:** "Home Of The Vertical Filmmakers And Creators"

**Business model:** Ad-supported + product placement (vs. ReelShort/DramaBox pay-per-episode paywall model). Hypothesis: lower acquisition costs, longer user lifetime, less churn.

**Current audience:** Female, 40s (core). 18-30 demographic is the growth target — they expect participation, not just consumption.

**Primary audience profile:** Diverse audiences ages 18-50. Millennials and older Gen Z. Mobile-first, streaming native. Culturally fluent and visually literate. Value authenticity, nuance, smart storytelling.

**Viewing behavior:** Watch primarily on phones. Short-form friendly but story-driven. Engage during late nights, commutes, breaks, downtime. Stay for stories that feel intentional, not algorithmic.

**Competitive position:** No microdrama platform has interactive features. Zero community infrastructure across the entire category. None using clippable moments or content creators as distribution strategy. All running paid acquisition. VURT can be first on multiple fronts.

**Platform:** myvurt.com (web) + iOS App Store + Google Play. Built on enveu white-label streaming infrastructure (Angular SPA).

## STRATEGIC THESIS

### The System
These aren't separate initiatives — they're a connected flywheel:

1. **Conflict-resolution format** creates content that hooks people
2. **Distribution machine** turns that content into reach
3. **Community** turns reach into retention
4. **Interactivity** turns passive viewers into participants
5. **IRL activations** create organic content that feeds back into distribution
6. **Ad-supported model** makes economics sustainable without churn treadmill

Each revolution makes the next one bigger.

### IRL Amplification Theory (Dioni's Working Theory)
IRL experiences where creators from different communities collaborate with each other — and where superfans, fans, and community members are part of it — amplifies content and attention spread. The event is simultaneously:
- A production engine
- A creator collaboration engine (cross-pollinating audiences)
- A distribution event
- A community builder (fan-fan bonding, parasocial → real social)
- A conversion tool
- Digital participation layer means remote audience is co-creating the narrative

Each layer amplifies the others. IRL content is inherently more shareable because it's unscripted, multi-POV, and emotionally resonant.

## BRAND IDENTITY

### Personality
- Confident, not loud. Intentional, not stiff.
- Culturally fluent as lived experience, not costume.
- Cinematic by design — crafted with care and restraint.
- "VURT doesn't ask for permission. VURT knows exactly who it's for and why it exists."

### Voice & Tone
- Direct and clean. Short sentences. Clear intent. No fluff.
- No hype words, exaggerated promises, or influencer-style excitement.
- "If it reads like it's trying too hard, it's not VURT."
- Culture is present but never performed.

### Words We Use
Stories, Culture, Ownership, Episodes, Series, Vertical Cinema, Creators, Originals, Community

### Words We Avoid
Content, Influencer, Snackable, Low Budget, User Generated, Viral, AI

### Colors
- VURT Black: #0B0B0B (primary backgrounds)
- Gold Highlight: #F5D27A (subtle reflections, top edge gradients)
- Core Gold: #D4A63A (logo gradient center, premium accents)
- Deep Gold: #9C6B12 (shadow areas, cinematic weight)
- Warm White: #F2F2F2 (headlines, titles, body text on dark)
- Soft Gray: #9A9A9A (metadata, captions, secondary text)

### Caption Style
- One strong idea per post
- Minimal punctuation
- No excessive hashtags
- Emojis rare and purposeful
- White space is okay
- "A good VURT caption feels like a statement, not an announcement."

### Gut Check Before Posting
1. Does this feel confident or loud?
2. Are we spotlighting the story or ourselves?
3. Would this still make sense a year from now?
4. Does this feel true to the culture or forced?

### Typography
- Headline: Neue Haas Grotesk (fallback: Inter)
- Body/UI: Inter
- Accent: IBM Plex Mono (sparingly — episode numbers, timestamps, metadata)
- Rule: "If typography ever feels decorative, playful, or attention-seeking, it's not VURT."

### Logo Usage
- Full VURT wordmark + Iconic "V" mark
- Always high-contrast backgrounds
- Never stretch, skew, add shadows/outlines/effects
- Never on busy backgrounds without solid/gradient overlay

### Social Media Philosophy
- "Front porch, not a billboard" — invite conversation, not announcements
- "Curated feed, not a repost dump" — quality and pacing matter
- "Creator amplifier, not a brand ego page" — the work comes first
- Posting priorities: story clips, creator spotlights, cultural commentary, platform updates (sparingly), community wins
- Rule: "If a post doesn't serve the story, the creator, or the community, it doesn't belong on the feed."

### North Star Message
"VURT was the only platform that TRULY understood authentic cinema and culturally rich storytelling in the vertical era."

## TECHNICAL STATE OF THE PLATFORM

*As of March 19, 2026 — from manual testing + crawler audits*

### URL Structure (Verified)
- **Series DO have unique URLs:** `https://www.myvurt.com/detail/micro_series/{show-slug}` (e.g., `/detail/micro_series/the-love-letter`)
- **Episodes do NOT have unique URLs:** URL stays at the series level when switching episodes. No episode-level deep linking possible on web.
- **Share links go to enveu's domain:** Share button generates `https://vurt.enveu.link/{random-hash}` — all link equity goes to enveu, not myvurt.com. Hash is random (e.g., `dmqdikktzy`), carries zero keyword signal.
- **Deep links are broken at episode level:** Shared link → browser → "open in app?" → app opens to show page, NOT the specific episode shared.

### SEO Grade: D+ (from technical crawl)
- **Sitemap.xml is empty** — search engines can't discover content
- **Zero schema markup (JSON-LD)** on any page
- **No per-page meta tags** — series pages have no title, description, OG tags, Twitter cards, or canonical URLs
- **Angular SPA** — crawlers and AI search engines may see blank JS shell instead of content
- **Broken internal URLs:** `myvurt.com/www.myvurt.com/privacy-policy` (doubled domain bug)
- **Internal settings exposed:** `/settings/terms-and-conditions` publicly accessible
- **12 HTML pages** = entire indexable footprint for 100+ title platform

- **iOS:** 4.7 stars, 11 ratings
