2. **Platform Health Score: 35/100 (D)**

   *For a 4-week-old platform, focus on Engagement and Retention — Growth can be bought, stickiness can't.*

3. **Acquisition efficiency problem:** Paid Social is 83% of traffic but 85% of those sessions don't engage (avg 43s). That's ~10.0K wasted sessions this week. **Action:** Test deep-linking ads to specific series pages (Favorite Son, Girl In The Closet) instead of the homepage. Test video-first ad creative showing actual show clips. Tighten targeting to 18-50 interest-based audiences.

4. **Organic signal:** 17% of traffic is organic/direct, but it drives 65% engagement — 4x more valuable per session than paid. Best channel: Organic Search at 71% engagement, 78m 38s avg. **Action:** Prioritize the SEO fixes from the dev call — auto-populated sitemap, SSR/pre-rendering for Angular, unique episode URLs, and per-page meta tags. Each fix directly grows this high-value channel.

5. **The app is the product, the web is the funnel:** App engagement is 87% with 72m 2s sessions. Web is 21% with 1m 30s. That's a 4x gap — 11.8K web users vs 308 on app. **Action:** Measure web-to-app conversion rate. Ensure app download CTAs are prominent on mobile web (above the fold, post-episode). Given the 4x engagement difference, every percentage point of web→app conversion meaningfully grows engaged users.

6. **Android users are power viewers:** 132m 31s avg sessions vs 11m 33s on iOS. Only 142 Android users vs 166 iOS — but they watch longer. Store ratings: iOS: 5.0★ (13 ratings), Android: 0 ratings.

7. **Retention data gap:** GA4 isn't splitting new vs returning users cleanly. This may be a data stream configuration issue — worth flagging to enveu's dev team.

8. **Low session depth:** 1.2 sessions per user means almost nobody comes back within the same week. For a content platform, target is 2.0+. **This is the leaky bucket:** paid ads bring people in, they watch once (or bounce), and don't return. Solve this before scaling ad spend further.

9. **Tracking gap:** 314 users spent avg 10m 57s on content GA4 labels '(not set)'. This means screen names aren't being passed in GA4 screen_view events for these views. **Action for dev team:** Ensure every screen_view event includes the series/episode title so this engagement can be properly attributed.

10. **Homepage funneling:** 58% of sessions land on '/' with 67% engagement. **Action:** The homepage needs to surface content immediately — hero clip auto-playing, trending series, 'Start watching' CTA above the fold. Every second a user spends figuring out what to watch is a second closer to bouncing.

11. **Best completion rate (7d) — 'Episode 2 - Killer Stepdad':** 37.1% watch-through (163 plays). This is your highest-quality content signal. **Use it in retargeting ads** — viewers who finish content are your best conversion target.

12. **Strong series funnel — 'The Parking Lot':** 60% of Ep1 viewers went to Ep2 (232 → 140 plays). High episode retention. Prioritize this series in paid ads — viewers who start are likely to continue.

13. **DAU cooling:** 3-day avg dropped to 1.4K (down 33%). Check: did ad spend decrease? Content gap? Or is this natural daily variance?

14. **Evening prime-time audience:** GA4 reports in UTC. Peak: 7PM, 4PM, 11PM UTC = **3PM, 12PM, 7PM ET** (2.2K users). Classic wind-down viewing window. **Actions:** Schedule content drops for 7-8 PM ET. Social posts 1-2 hours before peak to prime the pipeline.
