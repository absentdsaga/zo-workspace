2. Platform Health Score: 31/100 (D) Growth: 11/25 | Engagement: 10/25 | Retention: 3/25 | Traffic Quality: 7/25 How it's scored: Growth (11/25): WoW user growth 230% — 500%+ = 25/25 | Engagement (10/25): 10.9% engagement rate — 25%+ = 25/25 | Retention (3/25): GA4 not reporting new vs returning — scored 3 (data gap) | Traffic Quality (7/25): 16% organic/direct traffic — 50%+ = 25/25 For a 4-week-old platform, focus on Engagement and Retention — Growth can be bought, stickiness can't.
3. Acquisition efficiency problem: Paid Social is 84% of traffic but 91% of those sessions don't engage (avg 20s). That's ~33.0K wasted sessions this week. Action: Paid social already deep-links to show detail pages (99%+ of paid sessions). Focus on reducing detail page bounce — age gate friction and 27MB page weight are primary causes. Test video-first ad creative showing actual show clips. Tighten targeting to 18-50 interest-based audiences.
4. Organic signal: 16% of traffic is organic/direct, but it drives 24% engagement — 3x more valuable per session than paid. Best channel: Organic Search at 76% engagement, 70m 31s avg. Action: SSR is live — focus remaining SEO wins: auto-populated sitemap, unique episode URLs, and per-page meta tags. Each fix directly grows this high-value organic channel.
5. Web vs app engagement gap: App engagement is 87% with 64m 34s sessions. Web is 10% with 0m 29s. That's a 9x gap — 39.2K web users vs 344 on app. Note: Web is the primary AVOD revenue channel — improving web engagement directly increases ad revenue. Focus on reducing detail page bounce and improving time-to-video on web, rather than funneling users to app.
6. Android users are power viewers: 109m 31s avg sessions vs 19m 37s on iOS. Only 157 Android users vs 187 iOS — but they watch longer. Store ratings: iOS: 5.0★ (14 ratings), Android: 0 ratings.
7. Retention data gap: GA4 isn't splitting new vs returning users cleanly. This may be a data stream configuration issue — worth flagging to enveu's dev team.
8. Low session depth: 1.1 sessions per user means almost nobody comes back within the same week. For a content platform, target is 2.0+. This is the leaky bucket: paid ads bring people in, they watch once (or bounce), and don't return. Solve this before scaling ad spend further.
9. Cohort retention red flag — Organic Social: 613 users acquired this week, only 0.2% returned the next day. At this volume + this return rate, the channel is delivering bouncers, not viewers. Actions: (1) Audit the creative — does it set the right expectation? (2) Check landing page bounce isolated to this channel. (3) Compare D1 across creatives to find the few that actually retain.
10. Tracking gap: 349 users spent avg 14m 49s on content GA4 labels '(not set)'. This means screen names aren't being passed in GA4 screen_view events for these views. Action for dev team: Ensure every screen_view event includes the series/episode title so this engagement can be properly attributed.
11. Drop-off alert — 'Episode 1 - Come Back Dad': 2,283 plays, only 7.3% completion (~167 people finished). Something is breaking in the first 30-60 seconds — hook, loading issue, or quality gap. Watch the first minute on mobile and check NPAW error rate for this title.
12. Quality dilution pattern: Engagement rate fell from 32% (pre-scale) to 11% (current). This is the classic paid acquisition tradeoff — more users, lower quality per user. Not alarming yet, but if engaged sessions plateau while DAU grows, you're buying vanity metrics. The target: grow engaged sessions 20%+ WoW.
13. DAU volatility: 1.3K to 12.2K range in 7 days (9x swing). Traffic is campaign-dependent. Organic baseline (pre-paid days) appears to be ~1.3K. Goal: raise that organic floor through SEO, social, and retention.
14. Engaged sessions (the real metric): 4.7K (+28% WoW). That's 11% of total sessions. These are people who actually watched content. Growing but monitor closely.
15. Evening prime-time audience: GA4 reports in UTC. Peak: 5AM, 6AM, 11PM UTC = 1AM, 2AM, 7PM ET (7.3K users). Classic wind-down viewing window. Actions: Schedule content drops for 7-8 PM ET. Social posts 1-2 hours before peak to prime the pipeline.

Using AVOD benchmark range of $15-25 CPM (Adwave, verified for Pluto TV/Tubi tier) with ~5 ads/hr (industry standard ad load), est. $15-$25/week at current scale. VURT is not yet running ads. This is a scaling model, not a forecast. The direct lever is growing engaged watch-hours.
