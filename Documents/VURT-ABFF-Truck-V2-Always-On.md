# VURT ABFF Truck — V2: Always-On
## Vertical Cinema. Built for the Culture.

**Campaign:** VURT video-truck buy, ABFF week (Miami Beach)
**Spot type:** OOH video panels on a moving LED truck — silent, ≤15s

---

## The idea

ABFF is the densest gathering of film/TV buyers, producers, directors, press, and agents in the country. V1 converts the industry room. V2 does the harder job: get the same industry audience — plus Miami foot traffic — onto `myvurt.com` and hold them long enough to see what VURT actually is.

This isn't an explainer ad. It's a **statement of category.** Every Chinese-backed micro-drama app is selling "bite-sized drama, anywhere." VURT is selling **Vertical Cinema, Built for the Culture** — a claim those apps won't make and can't defend.

## Why this hero line is correct

- **Category wedge.** Ten competitors studied (ReelShort, DramaBox, ShortMax, FlexTV, Goodshort, MoboReels, WeTV, Kalos, PineDrama, FlareFlow) all say "bite-sized / snack-size / addictive / quick fix." None say "cinema." None say "culture." VURT can own both unopposed.
- **"The Culture" holds many communities, never blends them.** VURT isn't claiming one culture, and it isn't mixing cultures into a single sound. "The Culture" is the full set of communities VURT hosts — gaming, horror, drama, hip-hop, telenovela, faith, creator, etc. — each celebrated for what it values and how it gathers. People belong to several at once, and VURT is where they find their people, their shows, and each other.
- **OOH rule: ≤7 words.** Two declarative beats, no CTA.
- **Quotable.** Can be repeated verbatim at a panel or dinner — that's how festival audiences carry a brand after the truck drives off.

- Background: VURT Black `#0B0B0B`. Solid — no gradients.
- Type: **Inter Bold** throughout. IBM Plex Mono for domain.
- QR → `myvurt.com?utm_source=truck&utm_campaign=miami-2026&utm_content=v2-general`.

**Why this hero/sub combo:**

- **"Vertical Cinema"** — VURT's own brand word. Claims a category the micro-drama apps won't touch.
- **"Built for the Culture"** — VURT's tagline. "The Culture" = every community VURT hosts, celebrated distinctly, never blended.

**Why "ALWAYS FREE TO WATCH" as the primary support line:**

- **"Always"** promises permanence — kills the "free trial" assumption a passerby will default to.
- **"To watch"** = behavior verb, not abstract claim. Tells the viewer exactly what they're getting and what they can do.
- **Cuts against both competitor categories at once.** SVOD = subscriptions. Micro-drama = per-episode paywalls. "Always free to watch" rules out both in five words.
- **Reads cleanly at 40 ft on LED.** All caps, short words, single line.

**Alt support lines (for A/B inside V2 rear rotation — pick 2–3 per night):**

| Alt | When to ship | Why |
|-----|--------------|-----|
| **"FREE TO STREAM"** | Industry-leaning days / routes | Cleanest, least defensive. Reads as a category statement, not a friction-removal pitch. Best when truck is parked near venues or industry hotels where the audience already understands streaming categories. |
| **"STREAM FREE · NO SIGN-UP"** | Consumer-leaning days / routes | Removes the second-biggest friction objection after price. True (10-video sign-up rule means first 10 are walk-in). Strongest on consumer foot traffic where the "I'll have to make an account" reflex kills conversions. |
| **"NO PAYWALL · NO SUBSCRIPTION"** | Late-week, when retargeting fatigue sets in | Most explicit competitive positioning. Names what the rest of the category does and says VURT doesn't. Use sparingly — defensive copy is weaker than declarative copy, but it converts the price-sensitive holdout. |

**Default rotation:** 60% "Always Free to Watch" / 25% "Stream Free · No Sign-Up" / 15% "Free to Stream." Pull "No Paywall · No Subscription" only if the dashboard shows scan rate dipping by Day 3.

## Stickiness loop (after the scan)

1. QR → `myvurt.com?utm_source=truck&utm_content=v2-general`.
2. Landing page auto-plays a 90-second highlight reel of VURT originals (muted, captions on — matches phone-default behavior).
3. 30s dwell → overlay: *"Keep watching → [next episode prompt]."* One tap continues.
4. 4th video → modal: *"Get notified when new episodes drop."* Captures phone for SMS re-engagement (live once 10DLC is registered; skip for round 1).
5. 10th video → existing VURT sign-up prompt (per AVOD product rule). Sign-up is deferred to the point where behavior has already converted.

**B2B layer (industry path):**

Secondary CTA on landing page header: small-caps link *"For directors, producers, and partners → [myvurt.com/partners]"*. Routes industry visitors to a partners page that lists distribution deals, submission for originals, and a contact form. Keeps consumer flow clean while giving ABFF attendees a door.

## Why this matches VURT's KPI

Per AVOD rule — **primary KPI is video views, not sign-ups.** V2 is built for that:

- Every scan lands on autoplay.
- Every frame on the landing page points at "watch more."
- Sign-up deferred to 10th-video behavioral conversion.
- Meta pixel on `myvurt.com` auto-builds a retarget Custom Audience at near-zero cost.
- B2B path lives underneath, doesn't clutter the consumer flow.

## Variants (for A/B inside V2 rotation)

On truck buys, the screens cycle through multiple ad creatives instead of looping one spot. Making 4–6 short spots instead of one long one means different viewers see different content as the truck drives — you sample the whole VURT slate across a night without cramming it into one edit. Variants below are optional add-ons to the main spot above; each is a separate 15s side version that enters the rotation.

- **"One show" cuts** — six separate 15s side spots, one per VURT original. Higher production cost, higher per-pass relevance. Recommend 70 / 30 weighting (main spot / one-show mix).

- **"Creator shout" cut** — a VURT creator on-camera, silent + captioned: *"My show is on VURT."* Humanizes the brand. Needs talent release (single face = hero frame = consent required).

- **"Premiere" cut** — a variant for festival weeks (ABFF, Tribeca, AFI, etc.). Opens with the word **"PREMIERING THIS WEEK"** then plays a frame from a show actually dropping on VURT during the truck buy window. Ends on the hero line + QR. **Why this works:** festival audiences are wired to respond to "premiere" — it's literally why they're at the festival. Industry-relevant: buyers and agents track new content windows. Requires only one confirmed premiere title during truck week. Re-usable for any festival truck buy going forward.

- **"Range" cut** — 15s of 6 cuts, each a different genre/tone from the VURT slate (drama, comedy, documentary, action, telenovela, music-culture). One word per cut: "DRAMA · COMEDY · DOC · ACTION · MUSIC · CULTURE." Ends on the hero line. **Why this works:** directly proves VURT is a platform for many cultures, not one. Answers the ABFF industry question — *"what does this platform actually make?"* — with six answers in four seconds. Text-only is optional; ideal is one real clip per genre.
