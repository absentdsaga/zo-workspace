# FLARE DAY PROTOCOL — What's Actually Safe

**Use this when:** active dyshidrotic eczema flare, headache, hand bumps, post-trigger meal, or after NSAID/Advil.

This is the intersection of FOUR constraint sets:
1. **Low nickel** (SNAS)
2. **Low histamine** (MCAS/HIT)
3. **Non-NSAID-aggravating** (gastritis-protective)
4. **No mast cell liberators** (citrus, tomato, strawberries, chocolate, etc.)

Anything that fails ANY axis = no during a flare. Source-cross-checked against Jensen 2006, Mislankar 2013, Antico 2011, Maintz/Novak 2007 (histamine), and standard NSAID gastritis dietary protocols.

---

## ✅ SAFE DURING ACTIVE FLARE

### Tier 1 — Eat freely (safe across all 4 axes)

**Proteins (must be fresh-cooked, eaten within 4 hours, never reheated):**
- Eggs (any style — scrambled, boiled, poached) — gold standard during flare
- Fresh chicken breast (raw → cooked same day)
- Fresh turkey breast (raw → cooked same day)
- Fresh-cooked beef (lean cuts: sirloin, top round, ground 90/10) — eat within 4hrs of cooking
- Fresh-cooked pork loin
- Fresh-frozen cod, tilapia, halibut (low-histamine fish)
- Fresh-frozen salmon (skin-on, cook same day) — wild Alaskan best

**Carbs:**
- White rice (jasmine, basmati, plain) — most safe carb you have
- White potato (peeled, boiled, baked plain — not fried)
- Sweet potato (peeled, baked) — small portion during flare
- Plain white bread (no seeds, no whole grain) — sourdough best
- Saltine crackers (Premium brand confirmed safe)
- Plain rice cakes (Lundberg or Quaker — check ingredients, no flavors)
- Rice noodles
- Plain bagel (white, no seeds)
- Cream of rice cereal

**Vegetables:**
- Cucumber (peeled if not organic)
- Zucchini (cooked)
- Carrot (cooked or raw)
- Bok choy
- Cabbage (small amounts, cooked)
- Cauliflower (cooked)
- Butternut squash
- Iceberg or butter lettuce
- Celery
- Bell peppers (skip during severe flare — solanine can mildly liberate)

**Fruits (peeled):**
- Apple (peel — pesticides; even organic, peel during flare)
- Pear (peeled)
- Blueberries (fresh, NOT frozen — freezing concentrates oxalates/histamine for some)
- Cantaloupe, honeydew, watermelon
- Mango (small portion)
- Cherries (fresh)

**Fats:**
- Butter (real butter from cows, not margarine)
- Olive oil (extra virgin)
- Coconut oil
- Ghee

**Sweeteners:**
- Honey (raw, local if possible)
- Maple syrup (real, not "pancake syrup" — small amount)

**Beverages:**
- Filtered water
- Coconut water (fresh, not from concentrate)
- Plain whole milk OR oat milk (oat milk has trace nickel — limit to 8oz/day)
- Chamomile tea
- Ginger tea (fresh ginger steeped in hot water)
- Rooibos tea (caffeine-free)
- Small black coffee (4oz max during flare — caffeine can help vascular headache)

### Tier 2 — OK in small amounts during flare

- **Plain Greek yogurt** (unsweetened, full-fat) — limit to 1/2 cup; dairy is a wildcard
- **Plain oatmeal** — 1/2 cup max during flare-day stomach protection only (oats are mid-nickel but coat stomach for NSAID protection); long-term avoid
- **Banana** — 1 banana max; mild histamine liberator but BRAT-protocol staple for gastritis
- **Plain mozzarella** (NOT aged, fresh ball type) — small amount; aged cheese is a no
- **White flour pasta** (plain, no sauce — butter + parmesan-free salt only) — moderate nickel

## ❌ HARD NO DURING FLARE

### NSAIDs / Pain meds
- **Ibuprofen / Advil / Motrin / Aleve / Naproxen** — mast cell trigger + gastritis cause + blocks resolution. NEVER during flare.
- **Aspirin** — same family
- ✅ **Tylenol (acetaminophen)** is the only OTC pain med safe for you — max 3000mg/24hr

### Foods that are HIGH on multiple axes
**Dairy aged/fermented:**
- All cheeses except plain fresh mozzarella in tiny amount
- Sour cream, cream cheese (during flare)
- Buttermilk
- Cottage cheese (often listed as low-histamine but unreliable)

**Meats:**
- ALL leftover meat >4 hours (rotisserie chicken from fridge = no)
- ALL cured/smoked: bacon, ham, sausage, pepperoni, salami, prosciutto, hot dogs, deli meat
- Canned meats/fish (tuna, sardines, chicken)
- Ground beef left out or sitting in fridge >4hrs after cooking

**Fish:**
- Tuna (fresh OR canned) — high histamine even fresh
- Mackerel, sardines, anchovies — same
- Smoked salmon, lox

**Vegetables:**
- Spinach (highest nickel, also histamine liberator)
- Kale, chard, collard greens
- Tomato (fresh, sauce, paste, ketchup, sun-dried)
- Eggplant
- Mushrooms (especially canned)
- Pickled anything

**Fruits:**
- All citrus (lemon, lime, orange, grapefruit) — histamine liberators
- Strawberries — liberator
- Pineapple — high liberator
- Avocado (high histamine)
- Dried fruits

**Nuts/seeds (ALL during flare):**
- Peanuts, peanut butter (high nickel)
- Almonds, almond butter
- Cashews, walnuts, pecans, pistachios, hazelnuts
- Sunflower, sesame, pumpkin, chia, flax
- Tahini, Nutella

**Legumes (ALL):**
- Beans, lentils, chickpeas, soy, tofu, edamame, hummus

**Grains:**
- Whole wheat, whole grain bread/pasta
- Rye, multigrain
- Granola, muesli

**Sweets:**
- Chocolate, cocoa
- Licorice
- Anything with high-fructose corn syrup, artificial colors, or sulfites

**Beverages:**
- All alcohol (mast cell trigger + DAO blocker + dehydrating)
- Soda (caffeine + sulfites + colors)
- Energy drinks
- Citrus juices (orange, lemonade, grapefruit)
- Tomato juice
- Black/green tea in large amounts (mild DAO blocker)
- Kombucha, kefir (fermented)

**Sauces/condiments:**
- Vinegar of any kind (apple cider vinegar = exception in tiny amounts, NOT during flare)
- Soy sauce, fish sauce, oyster sauce
- Mustard, ketchup, mayo, BBQ sauce
- Salad dressings (most contain vinegar + tomato + sulfites)

## 🆘 NSAID GASTRITIS RESCUE FOODS (post-Advil window)

If ibuprofen was just taken on empty stomach, these are the foods that physically coat the stomach. Eat within 5–15 min of NSAID exposure:

**Fastest stomach coaters:**
1. **Whole milk** (8–12oz) — instant lining coat
2. **Oat milk** (8–12oz) — second best
3. **Plain oatmeal** (small portion, even though oats are mid-nickel — stomach trumps this short window)
4. **Saltines + butter** — proven gastric soother
5. **Banana** — pectin coats lining
6. **White rice (cooked)** — bulks and absorbs acid
7. **Plain bread + butter**
8. **Bone broth** (homemade or low-sodium boxed Pacific brand) — collagen + glycine repair gut lining

**Then within 30 min, real meal:**
- Eggs scrambled in butter + white rice
- Fresh chicken breast + white rice
- Plain congee/rice porridge

## 📋 RESCUE STACK (during flare, on top of daily supplements)

### OTC additions
- **Allegra (fexofenadine) 180mg** — 1x in morning during flare days
- **Pepcid (famotidine) 20mg** — 1x AM, 1x PM during flare
- **Tylenol 500mg** — for headache; max 6 tabs/24hr
- **Hydrocortisone 1% cream** — thin layer on hand bumps 2x/day, max 5 days
- **Vitamin C 1000mg** — natural antihistamine, 1–2x/day during flare
- **Aquaphor or plain Vaseline** — over hydrocortisone, lock in moisture

### From your existing stack, EXTRA dose during flare OK:
- Quercetin w/ Bromelain — add 1 cap mid-afternoon (3 caps total flare-day)
- DAO (Umbrellux) — 1 cap before any meal even slightly suspect
- Digest Gold — 1 cap with every meal
- L-Glutamine — extra 5g mid-afternoon (heals gut faster)
- NAC — keep as scheduled

### Skip during flare day only:
- B-Complex (1 day skip OK — B6 can mildly worsen acute mast cell activation)
- Berberine, oregano oil if currently on (they kill bugs but stress liver during flare)

## 🧊 FLARE DAY NON-FOOD ACTIONS

- **Cold pack on neck** 10 min on / 20 off — calms histamine headache
- **Cold pack on hand** 10 min — reduces mast cell activation in skin
- **Hot Epsom salt bath** for hands (1/2 cup salt in warm water, 15 min) — magnesium absorbed transdermally calms eczema
- **Sleep early** — histamine processing peaks at night
- **No exercise during flare** — exercise releases histamine
- **Lights low, hydrate hard, no screens late**
- **Do NOT pop any blisters on the hand** — extends flare 2 weeks + infection risk

---

## 🚨 GO TO ER / URGENT CARE IF:

- Black/tarry stool, blood in stool, vomiting blood/coffee grounds (GI bleed)
- Severe stomach pain that doesn't ease within 2 hr of food + Pepcid
- Hand blisters merge into one large area, develop yellow crust, fever (infection)
- Difficulty breathing, throat tightening, swelling of face/lips (anaphylaxis)
- Confusion, severe dizziness, fainting

## 📏 RULES OF THUMB

1. **Fresh-cooked, eaten same hour, freeze any leftovers IMMEDIATELY.** Never use the fridge for cooked meat.
2. **If you didn't cook it yourself in the last 4 hours, it's a histamine risk.**
3. **When in doubt, white rice + butter + salt is always safe.**
4. **Tylenol > Advil. Always. For you, forever.**
5. **Coat stomach BEFORE any pill (including supplements when flaring).**
6. **One trigger meal = 3-day flare. Plan accordingly.**
7. **Strawberries / tomato / citrus / chocolate are LIBERATORS — they trigger histamine release even if they're not high in histamine themselves. Treat as equally dangerous.**
