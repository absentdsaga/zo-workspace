### Timing rules at a glance

| With food (helps absorption / prevents stomach upset) | Empty stomach (absorb better alone) |
|---|---|
| Zinc · B-Complex · Quercetin · D3+K2 · NAC · Berberine · Oregano oil | L-Glutamine · Magnesium · DAO |

**Important individual rules:**
- **Zinc** — take with food OR at a different time than calcium/iron (they block each other).
- **L-Glutamine** — always empty stomach, at least 20 min before food or 2+ hrs after. Water only (no juice, no protein).
- **NAC** — with food to prevent nausea.
- **Quercetin** — ALWAYS with food (fat helps absorption, poor on empty stomach).
- **D3 + K2** — with a meal that has fat (eggs, avocado, olive oil, nuts, butter). Fat-soluble.
- **Magnesium glycinate** — empty stomach is fine and absorbs better. If you get any nausea, take with a small snack.
- **B-Complex** — morning only. Taking late = insomnia.
- **DAO** — must be empty stomach, 15 min before the meal. Food blocks the enzyme.

## How magnesium glycinate helps YOUR specific issues

1. **Headaches** — Magnesium deficiency is one of the most documented causes of chronic headaches. Neurologists prescribe it as first-line prevention. You said you have a headache right now — this is likely a big part of it.

2. **DAO enzyme production** — Your body cannot produce DAO (the histamine-clearing enzyme) without magnesium as a cofactor. Low magnesium = low DAO = histamine buildup = more eczema, reflux, headaches.

3. **Reflux** — Magnesium relaxes smooth muscle (including the LES, the valve that keeps stomach acid down). Chronic low magnesium = tighter, less functional sphincter = more reflux.

4. **Gut motility** — Food sitting too long in the small intestine = SIBO fuel. Magnesium keeps the migrating motor complex moving.

5. **Mast cell calming** — Magnesium depletion directly triggers mast cell degranulation (histamine release). Restoring mag = fewer mast cell flares.

6. **Inflammation** — Low magnesium raises CRP and inflammatory cytokines across the board.

You don't need to feel "tired" or have "trouble sleeping" to benefit. These mechanisms run below conscious symptoms.
