# Histamine & Mast Cell Food Lists

Dioni's symptoms (immediate post-meal headaches, reflux, throat mucus, lethargy) fit histamine intolerance or MCAS overlay.

## High histamine — AVOID

- Aged cheeses (parmesan, cheddar, gouda, blue)
- Cured/smoked meats (salami, prosciutto, bacon, ham)
- Fermented foods (sauerkraut, kimchi, kombucha, miso, soy sauce, fish sauce)
- Vinegar and vinegar-containing foods (pickles, mustard, ketchup, mayo)
- Alcohol (all — especially red wine, champagne, beer)
- Leftovers >24h (histamine builds as protein ages)
- Canned fish (tuna, sardines, anchovies)
- Aged fish of any kind

## Histamine liberators (trigger release even if low histamine)

- Tomatoes, strawberries, citrus (pineapple esp.)
- Chocolate
- Egg whites (raw)
- Shellfish
- Nuts (esp. walnuts, cashews)
- Food additives: benzoates, sulfites, nitrates, MSG, artificial colors
- Spinach, eggplant

## DAO blockers (prevent histamine breakdown)

- Alcohol (biggest blocker)
- Black tea, green tea (mild)
- Energy drinks
- Some medications: NSAIDs, metoclopramide, amitriptyline

## Low histamine — SAFE

- Fresh meat (not aged, not leftover)
- Freshly caught fish (cod, salmon — frozen at sea is OK)
- Eggs (cooked)
- Rice, potato, sweet potato
- Most fresh vegetables (not the liberators above)
- Apples, pears, blueberries, melons
- Fresh herbs
- Olive oil, coconut oil

## Practical rule

**Cook fresh, eat immediately, freeze leftovers right away (not refrigerate).** Histamine builds in the fridge, not the freezer.
