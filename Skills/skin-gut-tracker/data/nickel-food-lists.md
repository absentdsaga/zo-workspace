# Nickel Food Lists

Based on published low-nickel diet protocols (Jensen 2006, Mislankar 2013, Antico 2011, Sharma 2007).

## HIGH NICKEL — strict avoid during 6-week trial

**Grains:** whole wheat, whole wheat bread, whole grain anything, oats, oatmeal, rye, millet, buckwheat, spelt, multigrain bread, granola, muesli, bran, wheat germ

**Legumes (all):** soybeans, tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk, soy sauce, lentils (all colors), chickpeas, hummus, peanuts, peanut butter, black beans, kidney beans, pinto beans, navy beans, lima beans, white beans, split peas

**Nuts & seeds:** almonds, almond butter, hazelnuts, cashews, pistachios, walnuts, pecans, sunflower seeds, sesame seeds, tahini, flaxseed, chia seeds, pumpkin seeds

**Chocolate & cocoa:** all dark chocolate, milk chocolate with high cocoa, cocoa powder, Nutella, chocolate milk, hot chocolate, chocolate ice cream

**Vegetables (high):** spinach, kale, brussels sprouts, broccoli, green beans, asparagus, peas, green leafy lettuces (esp. romaine), canned mushrooms, onions (high-ish), canned tomatoes/tomato sauce/paste

**Shellfish:** mussels, oysters, clams, shrimp, crab (lower), lobster (lower)

**Canned foods of any kind** — nickel leaches from the can lining. Canned tomatoes especially bad.

**Other:** licorice, baking powder (most brands — use baking soda or aluminum-free nickel-free like Bob's Red Mill), multivitamins, supplements with nickel traces, margarine, hydrogenated fats

**Cookware:** new stainless steel leaches nickel. Avoid cooking acidic foods (tomato, citrus, vinegar) in stainless. Prefer glass, enameled cast iron, ceramic, well-seasoned cast iron.

**Water:** first-draw morning tap water from galvanized pipes. Run tap 1 minute before drinking/cooking.

## MEDIUM NICKEL — limit

Whole-meal bread in small amounts, corn, some mushrooms, cauliflower, cabbage, bananas (borderline, but OK for most), figs, prunes, raisins, beer, red wine, black tea (especially if brewed in metal kettle).

## LOW NICKEL — safe base

**Proteins:** beef, pork, chicken, turkey, lamb, eggs, salmon, cod, tilapia, tuna (fresh not canned), sardines (fresh)

**Grains:** white rice, jasmine rice, basmati rice, rice noodles, rice flour, corn tortillas (in moderation, corn is MRT-moderate for you), sourdough white bread (not whole grain)

**Dairy:** butter, aged hard cheeses (histamine caveat), goat cheese, cream, most yogurt (MRT-moderate for you)

**Vegetables:** cucumber (MRT watch), carrots, zucchini, bell peppers (solanine watch), cauliflower, cabbage, white potato, sweet potato, pumpkin, butternut squash, celery, lettuce (iceberg, butter)

**Fruits:** apples, bananas, blueberries, strawberries, grapes, melon (cantaloupe/honeydew/watermelon), oranges, peaches, plums, cherries

**Fats:** olive oil, butter, avocado (MRT watch), coconut oil

**Drinks:** filtered water, coffee (moderate caffeine watch — MRT 1.4), herbal teas (not rooibos — moderate), fresh juices

## Specific to Dioni — combined safe list

Intersecting MRT-low + nickel-low + histamine-low:

- **Proteins:** beef, pork, eggs, salmon (fresh), cod, turkey
- **Carbs:** white rice, sweet potato, white potato (if not flaring)
- **Veg:** zucchini, carrot, cauliflower, butternut squash, cabbage
- **Fruit:** apple, blueberry, banana, melon, orange (fresh only)
- **Fats:** olive oil, butter
- **Drinks:** filtered water, small amount of coffee

This is the "safe base" to build meals from during the 6-week elimination.
