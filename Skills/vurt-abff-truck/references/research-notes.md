## 2. LED truck ad effectiveness (best practices)
- Effective glance window is **~2 seconds per pass** for pedestrians and ~3–8 seconds at stoplights (rear face).[^6]
- QR codes on DOOH drive median scan rates of **0.3–1.2% of impressions** when the QR is ≥ 15% of panel area and the call to action is single-verbed ("Scan," "Watch," "RSVP").[^7]
- Highest-converting LED truck creatives share three traits: (1) brand mark visible for 100% of runtime, (2) one idea per frame, (3) no gradients (LED muddies them).[^8]
- Audio on side panel — majority of pedestrians hear only 2–4 seconds. Don't build a spot that requires full audio comprehension; build a spot that works muted, with audio as bonus.[^9]

## 3. DOOH → mobile web/app conversion benchmarks
- DOOH-attributed mobile visits are typically measured via **UTM'd QR scans + location-matched device-ID exposure** (LPMG will not provide the device-ID exposure data; we rely on QR + landing page analytics).[^10]
- Median landing-page bounce on DOOH-driven traffic is high (65–80%) because scans happen in motion. Counter by auto-playing content above the fold and deferring any form to scroll-depth trigger.[^11]
- **Landing pages that start with silent auto-play video** convert 2.3× better than static hero images for entertainment/streaming offers (internal Meta/Google DOOH reports).[^12]
- SMS fallback ("text VURT to 12345") catches 0.1–0.4% of the audience the QR misses — small but worth it when cost is negligible.[^13]

## 4. Miami Beach festival ad saturation (why bold > clever)
- During ABFF week, every major venue block already has: curated OOH buys from Netflix/Amazon/HBO, ambient sponsor activations (Ally, L'Oréal pop-ups), truck convoys from other Miami-week trade show overlaps (Race Week, local promoters).
- Bold type + high contrast + one idea beats clever wordplay every time in this environment. A glance test at 40 ft is the right production standard.

## 8. Miami Beach ordinance risk — AUDIO IS OFF THE TABLE
- **§46-152 flatly prohibits sound trucks / loudspeakers / amplifiers on vehicles** emitting "loud and raucous noises" on public streets. Only carve-out is political campaigns.
- Special Events permit does **NOT** override the noise ordinance (confirmed on miamibeachfl.gov FAQ).
- Hard 100-ft plainly-audible cap between 11 PM–7 AM; daytime is judgement-based "loud and raucous."
- **Cruising ordinance (Ch. 106, Art. VII):** passing the same traffic-control point 2× within restricted window in Washington/Ocean/Collins zones = civil violation. A truck's whole premise is repeated passes — biggest operational exposure. Brief driver on route.
- Miami-Dade §19-15.12 bans vehicles/trailers parked continuously as advertising signs.
- LPMG markets "no permits needed" — contradicts MB code. Get insurance + indemnity in writing.
- Tourism & Culture Dept: 305.673.7577, Events@miamibeachfl.gov.

## 11. Geofence-to-retarget (2026 mechanics)
- Post-ATT: raw IDFA retargeting broken (25–30% iOS opt-in). What works: **addressable geofencing** — Simpli.fi/GroundTruth resolve polygon devices to **household IP** and retarget the home.
- Meta Custom Audiences via server-side CAPI from pixel events (once user hits landing page) is the cleanest Meta play.
- Vendors: GroundTruth (visit attribution strongest), Simpli.fi (household IP winner), StackAdapt (programmatic breadth, ~$5K/mo min), Blis.
- Budget: $7–$12 CPM event rate; practical min $5–10K media + $1–2K fees for 5-day ABFF window.
- Expect 15–35% incremental visit lift; 0.3–0.8% CTR on display.
- FL Digital Bill of Rights: treats precise geoloc as sensitive; threshold = $1B rev, so VURT isn't directly covered but the DSP is. Get vendor FDBR posture in writing.

## 12. SMS shortcode — 10DLC only
- **Shared shortcodes are banned** (carrier enforcement 2021+).
- **Dedicated shortcode = 8–12 weeks, $500–$1500/mo**. Won't hit 5/27.
- **10DLC (10-digit long code) = the answer.** Brand approval minutes–hours; campaign approval 10–15 days (up to 3 wks backlog). Start by 2026-04-25 → worst-case approval 2026-05-16 → 11-day buffer.
- Stack: **Twilio 10DLC + SimpleTexting** (skip Attentive/Postscript — Shopify-CRM assumptions).
- CTIA opt-in on creative must show: keyword, code, "Msg & data rates may apply," msg cap, HELP/STOP, privacy URL, terms URL.
- Double opt-in required even where TCPA doesn't strictly require it.
- June 30 2026: TCR will require PrivacyPolicyUrl + TermsAndConditionsUrl — include from day one.
