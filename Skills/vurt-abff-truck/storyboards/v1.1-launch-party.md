## VURT Opening Night · Friday May 29, 2026 · Gates Hotel South Beach

*v1.0 archived at `v1.0-launch-party.md`. Changes from v1.0: kinetic-type replaces "THE TV FOR WHAT TV WON'T SHOW" (reactive, off-voice) with VURT-native possessive beats; tagline alignment with VURT's own brand words — "Vertical Cinema. Built for the Culture."; hosts moved to main; talent-release warning killed per Dioni's fast-cut rule.*

---

### The idea
This is **VURT's coming-out moment in Miami** — the night Black vertical cinema introduces itself to the room that matters. The truck is not selling "a party." It's announcing that VURT exists, then framing the rooftop as where Miami meets it. The QR is the door. Ground floor = VURT experience, open to walk-ins + invitees. Rooftop = 500-cap invite-only.

**Hero message: "VURT IS HERE."** The party is the *answer* to what that means, not the subject of the ad.

### Why this works (grounded in OOH research)
- **OAAA rule: ≤7 words.** "VURT IS HERE" is 3. "Vertical Cinema. Built for the Culture." is 6.
- **Declarative confidence, no CTA.** Succession didn't say "watch now." Squid Game said "Let the new games begin." The brand carries the call. Our QR is the call.
- **Possessive, definite-article voice native to Black media.** BET: "Where Black Culture Lives." Essence: "Where Black Women Are Boundless." VURT: "Built for *the* Culture."
- **Quotable shape.** Six words, two beats, zero jargon. Built to be said at dinner.

### SIDE (16:9 · 1920×1080 video · SILENT — Miami Beach §46-152 bans vehicle audio)
**15-second spot. Pedestrian + sidewalk audience. All meaning carried by type, motion, cuts.**

| Beat | Time | Visual | Kinetic type |
|------|------|--------|--------------|
| 1 | 0.0–1.5s | Black → VURT logo stamp (hard cut, 6 frames) | Logo holds, one pulse |
| 2 | 1.5–2.5s | Black card | "VURT IS HERE" types in, full hold |
| 3 | 2.5–5.5s | Rapid cuts: 4–6 talent/show frames (0.5–0.6s each) from across the VURT slate — Chief Keef/Glo Navy, Karma in Heels, Come Back Dad, Kevin Hart title card, etc. — intercut with Miami-night texture | One word per cut, rhythm-locked: **"VERTICAL" · "CINEMA" · "OUR" · "CULTURE"** |
| 4 | 5.5–8.5s | Title card: "OPENING NIGHT · FRI 5.29" (Core Gold on VURT Black) | Type types in, 3-frame hold |
| 5 | 8.5–11.0s | Title card: "ROOFTOP · MIAMI BEACH" + small-caps host line below | Hard cut, hold |
| 6 | 11.0–14.0s | QR pulls center-stage with "SCAN TO REQUEST INVITE" under it | QR zooms in, type anchors below |
| 7 | 14.0–15.0s | VURT logo + `myvurt.com/abff` URL | Logo + URL hold for scan-back |

*"Kinetic type" means text that animates with the cuts — letter-by-letter or word-by-word punches in rhythm with the edit. It behaves like a cut itself. Keeps a silent 15s from feeling flat.*

*Why "VERTICAL · CINEMA · OUR · CULTURE" and not "THE TV FOR WHAT TV WON'T SHOW":* The old line used "TV" twice — dead frame; VURT is vertical cinema, not a TV competitor. It was reactive (defined VURT by what TV lacks), negative, and violated the "not trying too hard" brand rule. The new four-word pattern uses VURT's own brand vocabulary (Vertical Cinema, Culture) in a possessive rhythm (OUR) that's native to Black-media voice without performing it. Same cut count, stronger signal.

Design rules for both faces:
- **No audio.** Silent creative. Do not brief audio.
- **Bold sans only.** Skinny fonts vanish on LED.
- **All text legible at 40 ft.**
- **Logo visible in every second.**
- **No ABFF mention.** VURT is not an official sponsor.
- **Talent cuts = brand texture, not endorsement.** Fast-rotation cuts across many faces are fine without individual OOH releases. Consent only triggers when a single face becomes the hero of a frame.

### Stickiness loop (after the scan)

**Landing page (`myvurt.com/abff` before 5/29):**
- Headline: **"VURT is here."**
- Subhead: *"Vertical Cinema. Built for the Culture. Fri 5.29 is our opening night in Miami Beach."*
- Split CTA:
  - **[Request rooftop invite →]** (primary, Core Gold fill)
  - **[Ground floor walk-in info →]** (secondary, outline)

**Rooftop path (requested invite):**
1. Form: first name + phone + email + "how we know you" field.
2. Internal guestlist review (Dioni + team).
3. Instant auto-reply: "Request received. We'll confirm within 48 hours. — VURT"
4. On approval: "You're on the list for VURT Opening Night, Fri 5.29. Address + time sent Wed 5.27."
5. Wed 5.27 8 PM: venue + dress code + "bring 1" referral link.
6. Day-of 5.29 3 PM: arrival instructions + map pin + entrance.
7. Post-event 5.30 11 AM: "Here's what opening night looked like → [recap on myvurt.com]."

**Ground-floor path (walk-in):**
1. Info page: address + Fri 5.29 doors open time + "open to public, 21+ with ID."
2. Lightweight email capture: "Tell me when the next VURT thing hits Miami."
3. Day-of 5.29 3 PM email: reminder + "if the rooftop looks full, downstairs is where it's happening."
4. Post-event: add to general VURT newsletter.

Each touch re-routes to VURT web/app. That's the whole point.

### Variants (for A/B inside V1 rotation)

- **"500 cap"** — subline "500 ON THE ROOF · SCAN TO REQUEST" frames scarcity without saying "exclusive."
- **"Quiet"** — near-black panel with only "VURT IS HERE," "FRI 5.29," and a flickering QR. A24-pattern restraint. Stands out against LED spam. Highest aesthetic upside, lowest information density.
- **"Show-first"** — lead with one signature show still instead of "VURT IS HERE." Hero line becomes "OPENING NIGHT · FRI 5.29 · VURT." Works if a specific show is the hook more than the brand declaration.
- **"One host"** — rotate through three variants, each featuring one host alone (Tarik, Ted, Hilmon). Works if each investor is comfortable being the solo face on a truck.
