## What they share

1. **Female lead, unloading on a parent figure.** Both scenes are a daughter eviscerating family about love withheld.
2. **No setup needed.** The opening line drops you mid-detonation. "I got straight A's" and "Just shut up" both function as attack-hooks.
3. **Escalating specificity.** Neither speech stays abstract. Both name exact receipts: specific achievements, specific wounds.
4. **Lines the audience wants to borrow.** 28 saves on the Spence clip means people bookmarked it — to rewatch, to send, to caption their own grief. Saves reward relatability-with-words-I-couldn't-find-myself.
5. **One-to-one camera framing.** Tight on the speaker's face. Tatyana Ali tearing up → save magnet. Emotional tear quality is rare; the algorithm rewards it.

  - **Actionable:** When cutting, tag each scene as either "save bait" (raw, borrowable language about grief/withholding) or "like bait" (clean clapback with a punchline). Treat differently in caption strategy.

## The structural formula that worked

Both posts follow this skeleton — and every unused clip can be judged against it:

1. **Opening punch = stated grievance**, not setup. ("Just shut up." / "I got straight A's.")
2. **Anchor to a universal wound**: parent's favoritism, love withheld, performance → still invisible.
3. **Escalating list of specifics.** (receipts, receipts, receipts.)
4. **Close with an accusation or dare**, not a reflection. ("Because you didn't." / "He reveled in your disfavor.")
5. **Cut before the resolution.** Leave the wound open. Viewer rewinds to feel it again → save.
