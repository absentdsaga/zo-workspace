# Critique Rubric — Pre-Ship Gauntlet

Run this rubric on every VURT creative before declaring done. Five questions. Hard answers. No ship past a fail.

---

## The five questions

### 1. Cover the VURT logo. Is it still obviously VURT?

If you removed the wordmark and showed this to someone fluent in VURT, would they identify it? If no, the voice isn't strong enough. The work is generic. Rewrite until the line, layout, or image carries the brand on its own.

**Pass:** "BY INVITATION. ROOFTOP. 5.29." — even without the VURT logo, the typography, register, and specific scarcity is identifiable.
**Fail:** "Watch your favorite shows free, anytime, anywhere." — could be Tubi, ReelShort, Pluto, Crackle.

---

### 2. Read the line aloud. Does it sound like Dioni or like a press release?

Dioni's voice is short sentences, periods over em dashes, confidence over hype, specific over vague. If you read the line and it sounds like it could appear in an investor deck or a corporate announcement, rewrite.

**Pass:** "Built for the Culture." (six syllables, period, declarative)
**Fail:** "We are excited to bring vertical cinema to a multi-cultural audience." (corporate-third-person, generic, soft)

---

### 3. Strip every word that could appear in a Tubi, ReelShort, or Netflix ad. What's left?

Highlight every word in the draft. Strike through any word that could appear in a competitor's ad. What remains is the actual VURT voice. If most of the draft gets struck through, the work is generic.

**Pass:** "By invitation. Rooftop. 5.29." — none of these words land for a generic streaming ad. Only VURT runs invite-only Miami rooftops with industry hosts.
**Fail:** "Watch free vertical drama anytime on VURT." — every word is generic except the brand name.

---

### 4. What did you leave OUT that took courage?

Name it explicitly. The chrome strip on Lemon. The "Don't" in Don't Buy This Jacket. The empty bucket in FCK. If your concept includes everything you could fit, you didn't make a choice. The discipline is in what's missing.

**Pass:** C1-E billboard left out the cast headshots, the show stills, the launch sizzle, the QR code, the app-store badges, the ABFF logo, and the genre tags. What remains is hero phrase + proof line + URL.
**Fail:** A draft that includes the tagline, the cast photo, the show grid, the QR, the app store badges, the air date, the network bug, and the social handles. Nothing was cut.

---

### 5. Would a fan screenshot, tattoo, or quote this back?

Is there a single line, image, or moment that someone could lift out and share — with no caption, no context — and have it land? If not, the work is renting attention, not earning it.

**Pass:** "I told you not to come back." (Karma in Heels dialogue carved out of the show, used as headline) — fans of the show would screenshot.
**Fail:** A composition that "works as a whole" but has no single liftable moment. Mush.

---

## Verdict logic

- **5/5 pass** — ship.
- **4/5 pass with one soft issue** — ship, but note the soft issue for future iteration.
- **3/5 or worse** — back to sketch. Don't ship past a hard fail.

---

## What "ship past a hard fail" looks like (don't do this)

- Sending the V2-D Phone Hijack mockup to Dioni with "Vertical Cinema · Free" as the genre tag.
- Sending a billboard with cofounder names alone as the headline.
- Sending an OOH with a phone-frame on horizontal canvas.
- Sending creative with em dashes anywhere.
- Sending a concept that fails the cover-the-logo test.

Each of these has been done. Each has been called out. The rubric exists to stop the next one.

## Calibration: examples graded

### V2-D Phone Hijack mockup
Caption: "I told you not to come back." over a Karma in Heels phone-frame mockup. Tag underneath: "Karma in Heels · S1·E3 / Vertical Cinema · Free."

- Q1 (Cover the logo) — **soft pass** for the dialogue line; **fail** for the genre tag (could be any streaming platform).
- Q2 (Sounds like Dioni) — **pass** for the dialogue; **fail** for "Vertical Cinema · Free" (library-genre tag is exactly the framing he's rejected).
- Q3 (Strip generic words) — half the draft gets struck through. The genre tag is pure generic.
- Q4 (Courage cut) — **fail**. The genre tag is the opposite of cutting; it's adding chrome.
- Q5 (Fan screenshot) — the dialogue line passes; the surrounding chrome breaks the moment.

**Verdict: 1/5 hard pass, 4/5 fail-or-soft.** Do not ship.

**Fix:** Drop the genre tag. Show the dialogue and the show name only — pure in-world voice, no out-world chrome. OR, if the brief was actually for an out-world brand board, replace the dialogue with a brand line ("Vertical Cinema. Built for the Culture.") and choose a single hero image instead of a phone-frame mockup.

### C1-E "By Invitation" billboard
Hero: "BY INVITATION. ROOFTOP. 5.29." Proof line: "Hosted by Tarik Brooks · Ted Lucas · Hilmon Sorey · Eric Tomosunas." CTA: "myvurt.com/abff →"

- Q1 (Cover the logo) — **pass**. The specific scarcity and named-rooftop language is recognizably VURT-coded.
- Q2 (Sounds like Dioni) — **pass**. Period rhythm. Confidence. Specific.
- Q3 (Strip generic words) — almost nothing gets struck through. Every word is doing work.
- Q4 (Courage cut) — **pass**. No cast photos, no show stills, no QR, no app badges, no ABFF.
- Q5 (Fan screenshot) — **soft pass**. Industry attendees would screenshot to show they got the invite.

**Verdict: 5/5.** Ship.

### C2-G "Single Hero" billboard
Hero: "Vertical Cinema. Built for the Culture." Subhead: "ALWAYS FREE TO WATCH." Talent: single face full-bleed left half.

- Q1 (Cover the logo) — **pass**. The tagline is identifiable as VURT.
- Q2 (Sounds like Dioni) — **pass**.
- Q3 (Strip generic words) — "Always free to watch" is doing actual differentiation work (vs. ReelShort/DramaBox).
- Q4 (Courage cut) — **pass**. One face, not three. No show grid. No genre tags.
- Q5 (Fan screenshot) — **soft pass**. The single-hero treatment with VURT branding could become a recognizable visual pattern over rotation.

**Verdict: 5/5.** Ship.

---

## What this rubric is for

It's a pre-ship gate, not an inspiration tool. The four critic subagents are inspiration and analysis. This is the final filter before the work leaves your hands.
