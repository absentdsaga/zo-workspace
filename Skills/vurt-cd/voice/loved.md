# VURT Voice — Loved

Verbatim phrases Dioni has used or approved. These are the lines you steal from. Each entry: the line, the source, why it works.

---

## Brand-level

### "Vertical Cinema. Built for the Culture."
**Source:** VURT master tagline, used across all platforms.
**Why it works:** Six words. Two periods. First period defines the format with respect (not "short-form video" or "snackable mobile content"). Second period defines the audience without specifying which culture (multi-community without being "multi-cultural"). Earns its arrogance.

### "Built for the Culture."
**Source:** Used standalone on social, in deck headlines, in OOH.
**Why it works:** Three words. The capital-C "Culture" reads as plural — many cultures, all welcomed, none diluted. Owns the cultural-home positioning without a single demographic word.

### "Always free to watch."
**Source:** C2-G billboard concept (chosen over C2-A through C2-F).
**Why it works:** Distinguishes from ReelShort/DramaBox who paywall. "Always" is the load-bearing word — implies forever, not "introductory offer." "To watch" is the bargain — VURT only asks attention.

### "Stream free at myvurt.com"
**Source:** Caption protocol close, used on every clip caption.
**Why it works:** Action verb + price + URL. No "click the link in bio." No app-store nonsense. Direct.

## Event / FOMO

### "By invitation. Rooftop. 5.29."
**Source:** C1-E billboard (chosen over C1-A through C1-D).
**Why it works:** Specific scarcity. Named place. Dated deadline. Five words. Industry recognizes the Gates Hotel rooftop; tourists don't. The asymmetry IS the design.

### "Hosted by Tarik Brooks · Ted Lucas · Hilmon Sorey · Eric Tomosunas"
**Source:** C1-E proof line, ≤30% visual weight under hero.
**Why it works:** Names function as proof, not pitch. The frame word "Hosted by" tells the reader what to do with the names. Without the frame word, the names would be cognitive load.

### "Industry only. Fri 5/29."
**Source:** C1-E A/B test variant.
**Why it works:** Same mechanism, harsher gate. "Industry only" creates in-group / out-group instantly.

## Caption / scene-level

### "She wasn't supposed to find out like this."
**Source:** Caption protocol example.
**Why it works:** Specific receipt. Dramatic-irony framing — audience knows something the character doesn't. Stakes-as-surprise, not summary.

### "Only real ones know what's about to happen."
**Source:** Caption library.
**Why it works:** In-group identity without gatekeeping. "Real ones" is community language that signals "you're in if you get this," with no demographic specificity.

### "I told you not to come back."
**Source:** Karma in Heels S1·E3 dialogue, used as in-world headline.
**Why it works:** Verbatim from the show. Pure in-world voice — VURT disappears, the character speaks. This is why the V2-D Phone Hijack mockup's centerpiece line was right; it was the genre-tag underneath that was wrong.

### "What would you do in her seat?"
**Source:** Caption open-loop hook library.
**Why it works:** Question-form hook that the algo reads as engagement-bait without feeling like bait. Invites comment.

## Tonal moves

### Naming what's NOT being named
"This isn't an ad. It's an invitation."
"Don't come here for content. Come here for your people."
"You won't find this on the platforms you're used to."

### Periods as punctuation, not decoration
Three short sentences. Each ending in a period. Reads as confidence, not exhaustion.

### Capitalized hero phrases on dark fields
"BY INVITATION." / "ROOFTOP." / "ALWAYS FREE TO WATCH."
The capitalization is reserved for hero lines on OOH, never for body copy. Earns its weight by being rare.

### Numbers as image
"5.29" lands harder than "May 29th." The numerals are the image. Don't soften.

### Em-space-bullet rhythm: "X · Y · Z"
"Opening Night · Gates Hotel South Beach · Miami"
"Tarik Brooks · Ted Lucas · Hilmon Sorey · Eric Tomosunas"
Three-or-four item lists, separated by middot. Reads as cinematic credit-roll. Use this pattern; don't substitute commas.

## When in doubt

If you're not sure if a line is VURT, ask: **could this line appear, unchanged, in a Tubi, ReelShort, or Netflix ad?**

- If YES → not VURT. Rewrite.
- If NO → you're in the territory.

The line should feel like it could ONLY be VURT. That's the bar.
