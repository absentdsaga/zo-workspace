# VURT Voice — Forbidden

Verbatim phrases, framings, and visual choices Dioni has rejected. Each entry: what, why, what to use instead. **This file is the most-violated of all VURT voice docs. Read it every time.**

---

## Words / framings

### "Content"
**Why:** Reduces stories to commodity. VURT is not in the content business; it's in the culture business.
**Use instead:** Stories, episodes, series, originals, vertical cinema.

### "Influencer"
**Why:** Performative, transactional, anti-creator. VURT works with creators who own their work.
**Use instead:** Creator, talent, director, host.

### "Snackable"
**Why:** Demeaning to the work. Implies disposable. VURT episodes are vertical cinema, not snacks.
**Use instead:** "60 seconds to 5 minutes," "vertical cinema," "episodic."

### "Low budget" / "User generated"
**Why:** Reads as cheap and amateur. VURT shows are produced — the format is short, the craft isn't.
**Use instead:** Just don't frame around production scale. Frame around story or culture.

### "Viral"
**Why:** Marketing-speak. Cringe.
**Use instead:** Don't predict virality in copy. Let it happen.

### "AI"
**Why:** Hot-button, off-mission, attracts wrong attention.
**Use instead:** Just don't use it as positioning. If a tool is AI-assisted, that's an internal detail.

### "Black cinema" (as a category bucket on public-facing copy)
**Why:** Founders explicitly off-limits. Mark and Ted have said VURT should NOT be positioned like BET+. VURT hosts many cultures; this label boxes us into one.
**Use instead:** Specific community language ("hood drama," "family legacy," "romance world") if a culture is being named, OR no label at all.

### "Multi-cultural"
**Why:** Reads as "all cultures blended together." VURT celebrates DISTINCT communities, never merged. Flagged 2026-04-24.
**Use instead:** "Many cultures." "Built for the Culture" (where "the Culture" = many cultures plural, never one).

### "Black vertical cinema introduces itself"
**Why:** Performs cultural fluency. VURT doesn't announce. It is. Flagged 2026-04-24.
**Use instead:** Show the work. Don't narrate the work.

### "Vertical Cinema · Free" (as a library-genre tag line under a show)
**Why:** Library-genre tagging is exactly the framing VURT rejects. "Free" alone reads weak. Flagged on V2-D Phone Hijack billboard mockup, 2026-04-28.
**Use instead:** "ALWAYS FREE TO WATCH" as a value prop (treated as a brand statement, not a tag), and CULTURE tags ("ROMANCE WORLD," "FAITH HOUSE," "FAMILY TABLE") if tagging is appropriate to the format.

### "Drama / Romance / Comedy / Thriller" (Netflix-style genre tags on public-facing creative)
**Why:** VURT does not organize by genre. VURT organizes by culture. Flagged 2026-04-28 when a billboard triptych used these.
**Use instead:** Culture tags. See `Skills/vurt-billboard-miami/references/culture-tags.md`.

### "Watch anything, anytime"
**Why:** Generic streaming-app language. Could be Netflix, Tubi, Pluto. Says nothing.
**Use instead:** "Built for the Culture." "Always free to watch."

### Mentioning competitors by name in public copy
**Why:** Even to position against. Don't give them airtime. Tubi, ReelShort, DramaBox, Netflix, BET+, Allblk, Tubi, Quibi — never appear in public-facing VURT copy.
**Use instead:** Reference "the platform," "VURT," or the absence VURT fills.

### "ABFF" on creative
**Why:** VURT is NOT an ABFF sponsor. Legal/clearance risk + brand misalignment. Memory: project_vurt_abff_launch.
**Use instead:** Date-only context. "Fri 5.29 · Miami Beach."

## Punctuation / typography

### Em dashes (— or –)
**Why:** Dioni flagged this twice in ABFF truck iterations (v4, v12). Visual-noise punctuation that softens lines.
**Use instead:** Periods. Commas. Colons. Restructure the sentence.

### Pure white #FFFFFF on LED OOH
**Why:** Glares and blooms in daylight on Clear Channel digital boards.
**Use instead:** #EBEBEB (soft white).

### Multiple exclamation marks. Emoji spam.
**Why:** Cringe. Reads as begging for attention. The audience is too smart for this.
**Use instead:** One mark or none. One emoji or none.

### ALL CAPS for paragraphs
**Why:** Reads as shouting. Fine for short hero phrases ("BY INVITATION"), wrong for body copy.
**Use instead:** Title case for hero, sentence case for body.

## Visual / format

### Phone-frame device on horizontal canvas (840×400 / 16:9 / 2.1:1)
**Why:** Wastes 60% of horizontal real estate. ReelShort and DramaBox don't use phone-shapes because their player is already vertical. Researched and rejected — `Skills/vurt-billboard-miami/storyboards/c1-e-by-invitation.md` line 6.
**Use instead:** Full-bleed image, single-line hero type, single CTA. The horizontal canvas is the canvas — own it.

### Cast-grid / triptych on 840×400
**Why:** Mush at 10mm pixel pitch. Tubi/Netflix/Max never run grids — they run single show, single face, single tagline.
**Use instead:** One face, full-bleed left half, type column right.

### Cofounder names alone as billboard headline
**Why:** Vampire effect. Names alone without scaffolding read as cognitive load, not FOMO. Industry recognizes them; tourists don't. Names need a frame word ("Hosted by") and ≤30% visual weight.
**Use instead:** Headline does the FOMO ("BY INVITATION. ROOFTOP. 5.29."), names sit below as proof line.

### QR codes on driver-facing OOH
**Why:** Highway viewers cannot scan. Conversion approaches zero.
**Use instead:** A short URL ("myvurt.com/abff"). Only add QR if vendor confirms pedestrian placement.

### App-store badges (App Store / Google Play) in OOH or social
**Why:** VURT social ops drive to web (myvurt.com), not app stores. Memory: project_vurt_social_ops.
**Use instead:** "myvurt.com" as the only CTA.

### Show-stills grids
**Why:** Reads as content noise, not range. Range gets proven on rotation across slot plays, not within a single panel.
**Use instead:** Rotation strategy — same layout, different talent across slots.

## Mistakes I (Claude) have made repeatedly

These are the failures Dioni has called out. They go in this file because forgetting them means repeating them.

1. **Defaulting to library-genre tags.** "Vertical Cinema · Free." "Drama · Romance." Genre-mode is the failure mode.
2. **Performing cultural fluency instead of demonstrating it.** "Black vertical cinema introduces itself." Saying it instead of being it.
3. **Phone-frame everywhere.** Researched and rejected, but I reach for it because it looks like a "vertical streaming" cue. Wrong move.
4. **Cast-grid impulse.** I keep wanting to "show range." Range is shown in rotation, not in a panel.
5. **Em dashes.** I default to em dashes when restructuring the sentence is the right move.
6. **Cofounder names as the hero.** Names alone don't carry FOMO. They are proof, not pitch.
7. **App-store badges as CTA.** VURT drives to web. Always.
8. **Mediocre value-prop language ("Free!", "Watch anywhere!").** Weak. Use "ALWAYS FREE TO WATCH" as a brand statement, not a tag.
