# VURT Voice — Core Rules

The 10 commandments, VURT-adapted. Every concept gets graded against these before delivery.

---

## 1. Lead with the wound, not the win

VURT is a home for cultures that don't have one. That's a wound (people without a place) before it's a product. Every concept should start by naming the absence VURT fills — not the features VURT ships.

- ❌ "Stream short dramas in vertical format."
- ✅ "Your culture had nowhere to live online. Now it does."

**Self-check:** Where does the audience flinch in this concept? If they don't, you wrote a brochure.

---

## 2. Speak from inside the culture, not about it

Insider language signals "you belong here." Outsider language signals "we are explaining your culture to you."

- ❌ "Featuring Black, Latinx, and queer voices."
- ✅ "If you know, you know. We made a place."

**Self-check:** Could this line have been written by someone outside the culture and feel right? If yes, it's too explanatory.

---

## 3. Refuse to caption the obvious

If the image carries the moment, don't translate it into prose. The dialogue caption "I told you not to come back." needs no genre tag underneath it. Adding "Vertical Cinema · Free" tells the viewer what they already see and breaks the spell.

- ❌ "Karma in Heels · Vertical Cinema · Free"
- ✅ Just the line, and the show name, no library-genre tag.

**Self-check:** Strip every label, tag, and category descriptor. Does the work still land? If yes, leave them off.

---

## 4. Make the typography do the heavy lifting

VURT's wordmark + Inter Bold + #EBEBEB on #0B0B0B should be enough. Black field, sharp type, single dominant phrase. No image. No gradient. No glow. Every visual flex is earned, not borrowed.

**Self-check:** If you removed the photo, does the type carry the work? If no, the type isn't doing enough.

---

## 5. Build a voice consistent enough that one line does the whole job

A single sentence in VURT voice, on its own, with no logo, should be identifiable as VURT.

Working examples:
- "Built for the Culture."
- "Always free to watch."
- "By invitation. Rooftop. 5.29."
- "She wasn't supposed to find out like this."

**Self-check:** Cover the VURT logo. Is the line still obviously VURT? If no, you're writing for a competitor.

---

## 6. Specific scarcity, dated deadlines, named places

FOMO doesn't come from "limited spots" or "soon." It comes from named places, named people, named dates. The C1-E billboard works because "ROOFTOP. 5.29." is a specific room on a specific night.

- ❌ "Don't miss out!"
- ✅ "Rooftop. 5.29. Industry only."

**Self-check:** Could this date/place/person be swapped for any other and the work still hold? If yes, it's not specific enough.

---

## 7. Frame the brand as a worldview, not a product

VURT does not stream short dramas. VURT believes culture is the moat, community is the retention engine, and creators own their work. Every concept should ladder up to that worldview.

- ❌ "VURT — the home for vertical short-form drama."
- ✅ "Your culture had nowhere to live. We built it a home."

**Self-check:** Does the concept tell people what VURT IS, or what VURT BELIEVES? Belief is the move.

---

## 8. Tell the customer what NOT to do

VURT can use negative-pull because it has cultural authority. "Don't come here for content. Come here for your people." is the kind of line that earns trust — because it refuses to flatter.

**Self-check:** Where in the concept did you say no, refuse, decline, or push back? If everywhere is "yes," it reads as marketing.

---

## 9. Periods, not em dashes. Short sentences. Earned silence.

Dioni has flagged em dashes as a no-go twice. Use periods, commas, line breaks. Short sentences land harder. Silence (negative space, single-line headlines) carries weight.

- ❌ "Vertical cinema — built for the culture, free forever — only on VURT."
- ✅ "Vertical Cinema. Built for the Culture. Always free to watch."

**Self-check:** Read it aloud. Is the rhythm punchy or padded? Cut every "and," "but," "while" you can.

---

## 10. The medium is part of the message

Where the work runs is part of the work. A driver-facing 840×400 highway billboard plays a different game than a pedestrian Wynwood LED. A TikTok upper-third overlay plays a different game than a Facebook feed post. Concept BEFORE format is fine; concept that ignores format is malpractice.

**Self-check:** Did you account for the medium's pixel pitch, dwell time, and viewing context? If not, the work is decorative.
