# VURT Billboard Research — Compressed Findings

5 parallel research streams, 2026-04-28. Full agent transcripts in conversation history.

## 1. Vertical streaming OOH playbook (Reelshort/DramaBox/Tubi/Quibi)

**Headline:** Microdrama platforms don't run OOH at scale. 70%+ acquisition spend is paid social with vertical clips ripped from their own shows. There is no proven microdrama OOH playbook to copy — VURT is inventing it.

**Quibi cautionary tale:** $63M on OOH/awareness. Failure mode = demographic mismatch on culturally-flat creative. Don't repeat.

**Tubi pattern (closest analog):** One absurd single-frame visual + tagline + logo. No QR. No content grid. OOH amplifies social/broadcast moment, not primary acquisition.

**Established platforms (Netflix/Max/Disney):** Single show, single face, single tagline. Brand IS the URL. Show-stills grids never used. Sometimes QR-only as cultural stunt (Netflix has earned that).

**Pattern for unknown streaming brand:**
| Established | Unknown |
|---|---|
| Single show talent close-up | Must do double-duty: brand intro + value prop |
| Tagline only | Tagline + value prop |
| No URL/QR | URL mandatory |
| Content grid sometimes works | Content grid is noise |

## 2. FOMO mechanics on billboards

**What works (2-4 sec read):**
- Loss aversion / opportunity loss (Hodkinson 2016, prospect theory)
- Specific scarcity cues (date, count, gate) — not generic urgency adjectives
- Hard dated deadline ("May 29") beats soft urgency ("soon")
- Social proof of the in-room crowd (who's already in)
- Target-specificity / IYKYK creative — the *exclusion itself* is the connection mechanism (Wallach JCR / UAH 2026)

**Insider-recognition FOMO works IF:** non-insider still gets a coherent emotional read in 3 seconds. Names alone with no frame = vampire effect (Wharton). Names should be ≤30% of visual weight, prefaced by frame word ("Hosted by") so they parse as proof not headline.

**What DOESN'T work:**
- More than ~7 words headline (OAAA/Lamar/CCO consensus)
- Soft urgency without a date
- QR as primary CTA on drive-by panel
- "Don't miss out" / explicit FOMO language — reads desperate
- Logo soup
- Multiple competing CTAs

## 3. 840×400 DOOH design rules

**Spec maps to:** Standard Lamar/CCO programmatic DOOH, 12×25 to 14×48 ft highway-facing OR 6×12 ft pedestrian. 2.1:1 aspect. Driver-first design assumption.

**Type:** ≥80-100 px headline (15" at typical 12×25 ft = 25% of 400 px). 7 words max, 3-5 ideal. Sans-serif bold only. Stroke ≥6-8 px. Safe zone 5% inset.

**Color/contrast for Miami daylight LED:**
- 6,500-8,000 nits with auto-dim
- Black backgrounds work and are recommended
- Avoid pure #FFFFFF — use ~#EBEBEB
- Reduce saturated red and pure blue (bleed/halo)
- 10:1 contrast ratio minimum
- High contrast = +38% recall (OAAA)

**Image:type ratio:** 70% image / 20% type / 10% logo+CTA. Or type-only with extreme contrast. Never split focus.

**Failure modes:** >7 words; thin/serif/script fonts; logos <80px; logos >25% of frame; pure white BG; saturated reds/blues; phone numbers; long URLs; QRs on highway; centered logos; multiple CTAs; faces in groups (mush at 10mm pitch — single face is fine).

## 5. Culture/community tagging — top 3 for VURT

**Strongest signals 2026:**
- Romance dominates microdrama (ReelShort ~$400M / DramaBox ~$323M revenue, billionaire/contract/secret-heir DNA)
- Thriller has 40% higher retention than romance within microdrama
- Faith is the loud underserved signal — Great American +19% YoY, Pure Flix expanding, BET+ launched *Holy Hustle* Sept 2025
- Black 18-49 audience watches 7.5 hrs/wk MORE CTV than peers; 67% pay more attention to ads reflecting their culture (Nielsen 2026)
- Diaspora / Nollywood — currently funneled to YouTube ($10-15M/mo)

**Competitor language pattern:** Possessive culture-nouns (BET+ "Stream Black culture"; Crunchyroll "anime culture / fandom hub"; Allblk "where Black entertainment lives"; Great American "Faith, Family & Country"). Never "Drama / Comedy / Action."

**Definitive top 3 for VURT (Hip-Hop ruled out):**

**ROMANCE WORLD · FAITH HOUSE · FAMILY TABLE**

- **Romance** = library truth (Karma in Heels, Soul Ties, Champagne, Pretty Kitty, Love Letter) + microdrama growth engine
- **Faith** = strongest underserved signal + Reelshort/DramaBox can't play this lane (moat) + library proof (Church Boy, Soul Ties)
- **Family** = aligns with ABFF "Homecoming" theme + library proof (Welcome To The A, Baby Mama, Lil Duval, Trick Daddy) + reads Black-rooted without being ethnically reductive

Reads like Great American's "Faith, Family & Country" stack — values, not genres.

**Edgier alternative:** Romance / Faith / **Thriller** if creative wants "we have range" over "we're warm."
