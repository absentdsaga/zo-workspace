# TRICK DADDY POTS — Ad Scripts (6-second spec)

Spec: **6s, 1080x1920 vertical, audio-on, non-skippable, clickable end card via VAST.**

## What wins at 6s in celebrity cookware DTC (HexClad x Ramsay, Our Place x Selena, Caraway benchmark)

The category-winning beat structure:
- **0–1s:** Celebrity face, in-character, doing one signature gesture (laugh, stir, point). **Audio cue is critical** — sizzle, knife on board, signature catchphrase.
- **1–2.5s:** **Hand-off to product.** Celebrity's hand drops something into the pan, or grabs the lid. Hard cut to overhead cooking shot. Sizzle audio is non-negotiable.
- **2.5–4.5s:** **Money shot.** Non-stick release, ceramic glide, sear, or full-set-laid-flat reveal. This is the conversion frame.
- **4.5–6s:** **15-piece set + price anchor + brand lockup.** Price must be on screen. Caraway's whole creative system runs on visible price + visible savings.

**The mistake to avoid:** Wasting 3+ seconds on celebrity personality (joke, dance, catchphrase) before the product appears. **Solo Stove x Snoop got 19.5B impressions and zero sales lift this exact way.** At 6s, celebrity must hand off to product by **1.5 seconds** or the spot is just a cameo.

**Second mistake:** Not showing price. Celebrity sets read as expensive by default. Trick Daddy's $169.99 (vs $400+ for Caraway, $599 for HexClad sets) is the actual hook. Hide the price and you lose the spot.

## What's unique to Trick Daddy Pots (the wedge)

The show is the asset. Trick has a celebrity-cooking show with confirmed appearances by Mike Epps, Ms Pat, Trina, Rico Recklezz, Ice Cube, Lil Duval, Papa Duck. The pots in the show are the pots on the site at $169.99. **That's the entire ad.** Archive flash + product money shot + price stamp.

Trick is not available for a fresh shoot. No location access. The campaign is built from archival cooking-show footage + a half-morning product laydown the video guy can do solo.

## Spot #1 — "From the Show" (celebrity hand-off + non-stick money shot + price)

**Wedge:** Hero converting spot. Hits the cookware-winning pattern exactly: celebrity face at 0s, hand-off to product by 1s, money shot at 2.5s, set + price at 4.5s. Run as the always-on workhorse.

**Visual beats (6.0s total):**
- **0.0–1.0s:** **Archival Trick face from cooking show** — mid-laugh, mid-stir, signature gesture. Audio = his real laugh + sizzle bed underneath. (Pull the strongest 1-second window from the show — likely the Mike Epps or Ms Pat episode.)
- **1.0–2.0s:** **Hard cut.** Trick's hand drops a steak / onion / piece of fish into the hot pot, sizzle hits hard, oil shimmers. Pulled from show OR shot fresh in product laydown half-day.
- **2.0–4.0s:** **Money shot.** ECU non-stick release — food slides off the pan effortlessly, OR top-down sear glide. The "look how clean this surface is" frame that closes the cookware conversion.
- **4.0–5.0s:** **15-piece set laid out**, top-down, on dark surface (matches site aesthetic). Whole haul visible in one frame.
- **5.0–6.0s:** End card. `TRICK DADDY POTS · 15 PIECES · $169.99 · trickdaddypots.com`. Black background + steel-blue accent. Price legible.

**Audio:**
- 0.0–1.0s: His laugh + light sizzle bed.
- 1.0–2.0s: **Sizzle hits hard** — this is the audio money beat.
- 2.0–4.0s: Sizzle continues, soft beat enters underneath.
- 4.0–5.0s: Beat lifts (Miami bass, soft 808).
- 5.0–6.0s: Beat resolves. End clean.

**VO:** Trick — **"Same pots from the show."** (5 words, delivered over 0.5–1.5s in his own archival voice OR ElevenLabs v3 clone with signed consent.)

**On-screen text:**
- 5.0–6.0s: End card only. **`$169.99` is the largest text on the end card.** Shop Pay / Affirm badge bottom-right (real feature, never voiced).

**Production note:** The hand-off-to-pan beat at 1.0–2.0s can be archival (if the show has a clean drop-in frame) or fresh from the product laydown shoot. Fresh is better — full control over the food, the lighting, and the sizzle audio.

## Production & distribution notes

- **Trick on-camera time required: zero.** Optional 60-second phone VO session for Spots #1 and #3. If skipped, ElevenLabs v3 Instant Voice Clone trained on archival cooking-show audio (signed consent required — see `Documents/VURT-House-Ads-CONSENT-TEMPLATES.md`).
- **Product laydown shoot:** **half a morning.** One room with window light, dark wood or matte-black surface, phone camera. Whole 15-piece set + 1–2 pots for the flame/glide beats. Egg or fish for the slide money shot. The only IRL shoot day for the entire Trick campaign.
- **Archive rights:** confirm pull rights for cooking-show footage before ship. If show is Revolt-owned and Source Group doesn't have a clearance path, **Spot #2 ships regardless** (no archive). Spots #1 and #3 hold for rights resolution.
- **Price is the hook.** Every spot, every end card, $169.99 is the largest text. Voiced or not. **No spot ships without price visible at the close.**
- **Shop Pay Installments / Affirm badge** on Spot #2 and #3 end cards. `4× $42.50` reads as "less than fifty bucks a month" — that's the price-anchor wedge for cold-traffic conversion.
- **End card** is a fixed grid across all three: black background `#040404`, off-white wordmark, steel-blue accent line, price as the largest text. Same typography across all three.
- **VURT cross-promo:** on VURT series where Trick appears (per talent roster), prioritize these spots in the ad rotation — viewers already recognize him, CTR lift expected.
- **Ship order (1 week):** **Spot #2 first (Day 3)** — no archive risk, no talent dependency. Spot #1 second (Day 4 once VO + archive clearance lands). Spot #3 third (Day 5, only if guest frame clears at 1080x1920).
