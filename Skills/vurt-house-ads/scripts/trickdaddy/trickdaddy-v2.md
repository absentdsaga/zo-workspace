# TRICK DADDY POTS — Ad Scripts v2 (edit-only / archive-driven)

**Major constraint update:** Trick is NOT available for a shoot day. No location access to his home kitchen or Sunday's Eatery. The entire campaign is built from **existing archival footage** (primarily his celebrity-cooking show — confirmed episodes with Mike Epps, Ms Pat, Trina, Rico Recklezz, Ice Cube, Lil Duval, Papa Duck) plus **product laydowns** the video guy can knock out in a morning. If we can get a 5-minute remote phone VO session with Trick, we use that for custom lines. If not, we pull his voice from the cooking show audio.

Research-backed against `references/audience-insights.md` and `references/anti-slop-guide.md`.

**Creative direction:**
- The archive IS the asset. Every cookware DTC competitor is shooting fake "chef" b-roll in white studios. We have **Trick actually cooking, in his kitchen, with famous guests, on camera, with pans on a stove.** No one else can make that spot.
- Celebrity-guest angle becomes a feature, not a liability — "Mike Epps cooks in these pots. Trina cooks in these pots. Ice Cube cooks in these pots." Built-in social proof, no new shoot.
- Food shown must be food the audience cooks at home: smothered chicken, lobster pasta, curry chicken and rice, fried grouper, steak, bacon and eggs — all literally in the archive, no styling needed.
- Price framing: **$169.99 on-screen, Shop Pay Installments (4 interest-free payments of $42.50) shown as a logo below.** Confirmed live: trickdaddypots.com runs Shopify Payments, which auto-enables Shop Pay Installments for orders $50+ via Affirm. Never voice the math — Trick should never say "forty-two-fifty times four" on camera. The Shop Pay logo does the whole job silently; VO stays conversational. Price occupies 2–4 seconds late, not the opening.
- Whole 2026 home/kitchen trend (warm earthy neutrals, dark cottagecore, grandma's-kitchen nostalgia) lands dead center on real home-cooking footage without us chasing it.

Budgets: ~28 words for 15s, ~50 words for 30s (VO-driven means tighter word room since we're not cutting on camera performances we don't have).

CTA: `trickdaddypots.com` · `#TRICKIGOTMYPOTS`

Price lockup: `15 PIECES · $169.99` with Shop Pay Installments logo below (`4 payments of $42.50` as logo art, not spoken)

**Legal note:** Before cutting anything from the cooking show, confirm Trick (and/or his production company) controls the master rights and can clear guest appearances for commercial use. If a specific guest (e.g., Ice Cube) requires separate clearance, swap to a guest who is already clearable. Do this BEFORE the edit starts — not after.

## 30s #1 — "These Are The Pots" (hero — the kitchen the show was filmed in)

**Why it works:** Turns the entire existing cooking show into a product ad. Every cook-up the audience already watched on his channel was in these pots. That's the argument — not a claim we're making, a fact the archive proves.

**Visual beats (all archival + product laydowns):**
- 0.0–3s: Cold open — a pan hitting a flame, tight. Meat going in. Sizzle foregrounded. (Archive: any cook-up opener with strong sizzle.)
- 3–8s: Quick-cut montage of Trick cooking with a guest — could be Mike Epps stirring, Trina laughing at the stove, Ms Pat laying out patties. Three cuts, three different guests, same kitchen. Guest faces visible.
- 8–14s: Tight on the pan/pot itself doing the work — not on Trick, not on guests — pot with oil, lid coming off a dutch oven, a saucepan with rice. The star of the shot is the cookware.
- 14–22s: Trick on camera from the archive, plating or lifting a pan. Pick a beat where he's briefly talking about the food (not about a guest) — this is where his VO sits naturally.
- 22–27s: Product reveal — 15-piece set laid out on warm wood (custom laydown, video guy's shoot). Price lockup keys in.
- 27–30s: End card.

**Trick VO (pulled from show archive or remote phone session, 38 words):**
"Every episode, every plate — same pots. That's my kitchen. Mike cooks in 'em. Trina cooks in 'em. My grandma could've cooked in 'em. Fifteen pieces. One set. Get yours."

**On-screen text:**
- 3s: `FROM TRICK'S KITCHEN`
- 8s: Guest name cards flash fast as each guest appears (e.g., `MIKE EPPS`, `TRINA`, `MS PAT`) — 0.5s each, lower third
- 22s: `TRICK DADDY POTS · 15 PIECES · $169.99 · SHOP PAY 4 INSTALLMENTS`
- 27s: `trickdaddypots.com · #TRICKIGOTMYPOTS`

**Sound design:**
- 0–3s: Full diegetic — pan sizzle, burner.
- 3s: Miami bass bed enters (Trick catalog if clearable — "Nann," "Let's Go," or "Take It To Da House"; backup: licensed Miami bass production in the same tempo range).
- 8–22s: Music under, diegetic kitchen sounds on top.
- 22–30s: Music lifts on product reveal.

**Color / grade:** Match-grade the archival footage — warm, slightly desaturated. Color-match the custom product laydown so the cut at 22s feels seamless.

**Anti-slop check:** No claim we can't back with a frame in the archive. No "finally, a pan that…" No white studio. Guest name cards instead of a narrator announcing "celebrity chefs cook with Trick Daddy Pots" — show, don't say.

## 15s #3 — "Fifteen Pieces" (value — pots named, not counted)

**Why it works:** Bundle-as-hero is the strongest cookware ad format (verified across Our Place / Caraway / Beautiful). Previous v2 had a "one… two… three…" counting VO — which, honestly, is filler. Naming the pieces is actually informative, mirrors the product page, and tees up purchase intent for people trying to picture what's in the box.

**Visual beats (100% product laydown, shot by the video guy):**
- 0.0–2s: One pot on a warm wood surface. Hand enters, places it.
- 2–11s: Pieces fly in one by one. Each piece gets named in the VO as it lands. Full set built in real-time.
- 11–13s: Hero laydown — full 15-piece set. Big price lockup.
- 13–14s: End card.

**Trick VO (pulled from phone session OR composed from show dialogue, 25 words):**
"Saucepan. Three-quart stockpot. Seven-quart stockpot. Two skillets. Frying basket. Utensils. Mittens. Lids. Fifteen pieces. Everything you need. One sixty-nine."

*(Piece list confirmed from live product page: 1.5qt saucepan + lid, 3qt stockpot + lid, 7qt stockpot + lid, 8" skillet, 11" skillet, 4pc utensils, frying basket, 2 oven mitts = 15 pieces.)*

**On-screen text as each piece lands:**
- `SAUCEPAN` → `3QT STOCKPOT` → `7QT STOCKPOT` → `8" SKILLET` → `11" SKILLET` → `FRYING BASKET` → `UTENSILS` → `MITTENS` — one label per piece/group, each for 0.5s. Lids tuck in with their pots.
- 11s: `15 PIECES · $169.99 · SHOP PAY 4 INSTALLMENTS`
- 13s: `trickdaddypots.com`

**Sound:** Clean. A single kick pattern under the naming, Trick's voice on top. No music bed competing — we want to hear each piece named clearly.

**Why not "one, two, three…" counting:** Counting VO is filler — it doesn't tell the viewer anything the visual already says. Naming the pieces is informative, answers a real question ("what do I get for $169.99?"), and mirrors the product page — which is where the click lands anyway. Viewer hits the site already primed.

## Production notes — zero-shoot-with-Trick edition

- **The archive is the shoot.** Before anyone cuts, the video guy needs to inventory every episode of Trick's cooking show and pull a rough cut-list of: (a) pan-on-flame opens, (b) each guest's strongest 3-second beat, (c) plating/serving moments, (d) Trick-on-camera beats where he's talking about food (for VO candidates), (e) any table/family moments. That inventory is the creative brief — not a script.
- **Rights clearance is the critical path.** Before any edit starts, confirm in writing: (a) Trick/his production company owns the masters, (b) guest appearances can be used commercially for this campaign, or (c) which specific guests require individual clearance. If Ice Cube isn't clearable, we swap to Lil Duval or Mike Epps for that beat. Do this first — re-editing after a denial costs more than the clearance did.
- **One half-day product-laydown shoot** covers every product reveal across all 6 spots. Warm wood surface, natural light, 15-piece set + 1–2 pots in use. This is the ONLY original footage we shoot for this campaign.
- **VO: two options.**
  - **Option A (preferred):** 5-minute remote phone VO with Trick. He reads the 6 lines across the spots (plus 2–3 alt reads of each). No studio — his phone. Total ask on him: 15 minutes of his time. This gives us cleaner, purpose-recorded VO.
  - **Option B (fallback if even 15 min is too much):** Pull his voice from show audio — edit lines together from existing footage. Quality will be lower and edit will be harder, but it works. Flag this before the edit starts so the editor can budget accordingly.
- **Trick catalog music clearance.** This is the secondary critical path. Ideally Trick self-clears his own catalog. Budget 1–2 days for this. Backup: licensed Miami bass production in the same tempo range. Never stock library for 15s #4 (the no-dialogue spot).
- **Price lockup is non-negotiable:** `$169.99` big, Shop Pay Installments badge (`4 interest-free payments of $42.50`) below. Confirmed live — Shopify Payments is enabled on trickdaddypots.com, so Shop Pay Installments auto-applies at checkout. Never voice the math in VO — the badge does the work silently.
- **End card consistency:** wordmark top, `trickdaddypots.com` middle, `#TRICKIGOTMYPOTS` bottom. Same grid across every spot.
- **VURT cross-promo:** on VURT series where Trick appears (per talent roster), prioritize this creative in the ad rotation — viewers already recognize him, CTR lift expected.
- **Cut order of operations (1-week turnaround):**
  1. Day 1–2: Archive inventory + rights clearance confirm.
  2. Day 2: Product laydown shoot (half day).
  3. Day 2–3: Trick phone VO session.
  4. Day 3–5: Rough cuts of 30s #1, 30s #2, 15s #1, 15s #3 (highest-value spots).
  5. Day 5–6: Rough cuts of 15s #2, 15s #4 (ship only if they meet the bar).
  6. Day 6–7: Revisions, final mixes, delivery.
