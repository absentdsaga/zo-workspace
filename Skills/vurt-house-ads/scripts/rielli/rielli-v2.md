# RIELLI — Ad Scripts v2

**Priority brand per Ted Lucas.** Research-backed rewrite against `references/audience-insights.md` and `references/anti-slop-guide.md`.

Creative direction:
- Luxury swim winners never lead with price, logo, or face. We lead with fabric in motion.
- Arielle on-camera is our unreproducible moat — produced editorial (Oseree, Agua, Faithfull) cannot buy back "real founder, Miami, making one piece at a time."
- Quiet confidence register. Single hero piece per spot. No % off. No fake urgency. No "empower your confidence" energy.
- VURT 30s pre-roll with audio-on and clickable VAST end card. Drama-environment audience — emotional open outperforms feature open.

Budgets: ~32 words for 15s, ~58 words for 30s (a beat of silence at the top costs nothing and buys a lot).

CTA pattern: `riellibrand.com` — no promo code yet. Once Arielle provides a welcome offer (free shipping over threshold, gift with purchase, early access — NOT a % off), add below the URL in 18pt. Never above it.

## 30s #1 — "Only One" (hero — founder, VURT's first Rielli impression)

**Why it works:** Answers the exact objection this price point needs to answer — "why this over anything else?" In her voice, the "one-of-one" proposition stops being a tagline and becomes a commitment.

**Visual beats:**
- 0.0–2s: MACRO. Arielle's hands, thread between her fingers, pulling through silk. Scissor snip. No face yet. No text.
- 2–5s: She lifts the piece. Holds it up against a window. Miami atelier, late afternoon, real light.
- 5–11s: She walks out onto a flat roof / Miami water view — this piece now on a woman (could be Arielle herself or a model she hands it to). Piece in motion. Wind.
- 11–18s: Arielle back at the atelier bench, cutting a *different* piece. Different fabric. Different woman's measurements pinned above the bench. Real tags, real paper patterns.
- 18–24s: Tight on Arielle's face. She speaks. Eye contact.
- 24–28s: The second woman, on a different coast, wearing the second piece.
- 28–30s: End card.

**Arielle on camera (18–24s), 22 words:**
"Every piece I make, I make for one woman. When I cut it, it's hers. No one else will wear it."

**Optional VO under the opening 18s (spare, ~16 words):**
"Every Rielli piece starts with one cut. One pattern. One woman. Built for her."

**On-screen text:**
- 3s: `MIAMI ATELIER`
- 16s: `ONE OF ONE`
- 28s: `RIELLI · riellibrand.com`

**Sound design:** Open on diegetic atelier — scissors, fabric, sewing machine. At the roof reveal (5s), a warm undertow synth comes in low. No drop. No build. The music *joins* the room, it doesn't replace it. Avoid any track that sounds like a stock library cue.

**Color / grade:** Warm natural light, slight film grain. Shot on FX3 / A7S-level prosumer. No glossy studio look. Midday clean > golden-hour cliché.

**Anti-slop check:** No "luxury redefined." No "made for the modern woman." No drone pullback. No model laughing at nothing.

## 30s #2 — "Waitlist" (tension / information gap hook)

**Why it works:** Opens on a verified high-performing pattern (information-gap → scarcity) using a truth that's structurally true about Rielli — the atelier can only produce so many pieces. Converts curiosity in the first 3 seconds without shouting.

**Visual beats:**
- 0.0–3s: Black frame. Text types on: *"This piece has a three-month waitlist."* Under it, in smaller: *"We make one at a time."* No sound yet.
- 3–6s: SMASH. Close-up — the piece being cut from its pattern. Scissor sound foregrounded. Music in.
- 6–14s: Atelier montage — stitching, hand-tagging, a second woman's measurements being taken, the piece being folded into tissue paper and a Rielli box.
- 14–22s: The piece now on a body. Boat bow. Wind. Water. She turns.
- 22–27s: Arielle on camera, brief line.
- 27–30s: End card.

**Arielle on camera (22–27s), 17 words:**
"Every Rielli piece is cut to order. One woman, one piece. That's how I built the brand."

**On-screen text:**
- 6s: `MIAMI ATELIER`
- 20s: `HANDMADE · ONE AT A TIME`
- 28s: `RIELLI · riellibrand.com`

**Sound:** Typing sound on the text (real keys — like a notebook in a designer's studio, not a sci-fi terminal). Then silence-to-fabric. A light bossa / Italo-disco revival bed comes in at 6s, stays low the whole way through.

**Anti-slop check:** Scarcity line is literal (they actually pre-order one at a time), not manufactured. Arielle's close states the business model flatly — no "exclusivity," no "luxury," no pressure. The model is the message.

## 15s #1 — "Miami Atelier" (craft, no face, VO-optional)

**Why it works:** Can run with NO voice at all, which means it scales to silent autoplay environments AND the audio-on VURT slot. Sound design carries it.

**Visual beats:**
- 0.0–1.5s: HANDS. Fabric shears through printed silk, clean snip.
- 1.5–6s: Needle through fabric. Machine stitching. Fingers clipping a tag that reads `N°` + a handwritten number.
- 6–11s: The finished piece on a hanger, then in one cut — on a body. Golden-hour light without the cliché (warm, but soft — not orange).
- 11–14s: A second piece. *Different.* On a second woman.
- 14–15s: End card.

**VO (optional — 11 words):**
"Not mass produced. Made in Miami. One at a time."

**On-screen text:**
- 1.5s: `MIAMI ATELIER`
- 6s: `N° 01`
- 12s: `ONE OF ONE`
- 14s: `RIELLI · riellibrand.com`

**Sound:** Diegetic only until 11s, then soft piano or muted synth under the reveal. If running without VO, hold diegetic the whole way and let the silence work.

**Anti-slop check:** No "handcrafted luxury for the modern woman." The number card and tag reference does the heavy lift — show, don't say.

## 15s #2 — "One Of These" (hero product — pattern interrupt)

**Why it works:** Pattern-interrupt opener (46% 3-sec view rate vs 22% baseline per Stackmatix) anchored in a specific, undeniable brand claim. Works in VURT and on socials.

**Visual beats:**
- 0.0–2s: Camera INSIDE a swim piece — fabric filling the frame. Pulls out sharply to reveal: a single piece on a white surface, tag visible (`N°` + number).
- 2–6s: A hand places a *second* piece next to it. Different print, different cut, different tag number.
- 6–11s: Both pieces in motion — on two different women, intercut fast, two different settings.
- 11–14s: Back to the pair on a flat surface. Tags facing camera.
- 14–15s: End card.

**VO (14 words):**
"Only one of these exists. Only one of that one, too. That's Rielli."

**On-screen text:**
- 11s: `ONE OF ONE · ONE OF ONE`
- 14s: `RIELLI · riellibrand.com`

**Sound:** Low-BPM downtempo Afro-house or slowed-R&B bed. Avoid any track that sounds resort-generic.

## 15s #3 — "Worn" (credibility, no name-check)

**Why it works:** Press-credibility in luxury reads best when flashed as texture, not announced. We earn "globally worn" visually — you should be able to pause any frame and see a different location/woman.

**Visual beats:**
- 0.0–2s: Woman #1 on a boat bow, Bahamas. Wind. Tight on fabric moving on her body.
- 2–5s: Woman #2, Tulum. Different piece. Different body type.
- 5–8s: Woman #3, Miami private pool. Different piece.
- 8–11s: Brief cut — magazine page flipping past (SI Swim, Forbes 30U30 — real press Rielli has on file). No voiceover calling it out.
- 11–14s: Back to a single piece on a single hanger in the atelier — where it all started.
- 14–15s: End card.

**VO (12 words):**
"Made in Miami. Worn everywhere. Still one piece at a time."

**On-screen text:**
- 8s: `SPORTS ILLUSTRATED · FORBES 30 UNDER 30` (small, lower third, 1.5s only)
- 14s: `RIELLI · riellibrand.com`

**Sound:** One continuous track — Italo-disco revival or slowed R&B — carries the whole cut. No sound-effect stingers.

**Anti-slop check:** Press logos flashed as b-roll texture, not announced ("Forbes recognized" in a VO reads desperate at this price point). No Kardashian name-check in a 15s — let the viewer do the pattern-match work.

## 15s #4 — "The Hand That Cuts" (pure craft — no body, no model)

**Why it works:** Counter-programs every other swimwear ad on the internet, which all open on a body. This one never shows a body. Entire spot is fabric + hands + atelier. Makes the 15s about the maker, not the wearer.

**Visual beats:**
- 0.0–3s: EXTREME MACRO. A single stitch going through fabric. Pull back slowly.
- 3–8s: Arielle's hand (or a seamstress's) cutting, pinning, threading. Only hands. Face never in frame.
- 8–12s: The piece hanging on a hook. Camera pushes in. Tag number visible.
- 12–14s: Dissolve — the same piece, now on a hanger near a window, the Miami skyline soft in the background, no body.
- 14–15s: End card.

**VO (10 words):**
"Every Rielli piece is made by hand. In Miami."

**On-screen text:**
- 12s: `N°` + number (tag readable)
- 14s: `RIELLI · riellibrand.com`

**Sound:** All diegetic until the final beat. Single piano note or a held synth pad on the end-card hit.

## Production notes

- **Arielle is required for the two 30s.** If she's unavailable, we do not substitute with a VO actress on the 30s — we ship only the 15s and shoot her later. Founder authenticity is the whole category moat.
- **Phone-only shoot is viable.** For both 30s, Arielle's on-camera lines are 5–6 seconds each, shot tight on her face. That's textbook iPhone/Android front or rear camera territory — 4K, 24/30fps, window light, AirPods as a lav mic (or a $20 Rode clip-on). Video guy gets her raw files via AirDrop/Drive and assembles from there. This is actually the **creator-native register** the VURT audience reads as authentic; a full crew shoot would make the 30s feel more "ad" and less "founder." Default plan: **Arielle phone-shoots her own on-camera beats + hand/atelier macros, no crew.** Crew day only if she prefers it.
- **If we can't get into the atelier** (lease issue, access blocked, she works elsewhere): the spot still works from (a) her own apartment with good window light for the on-camera lines, (b) any clean workspace for the hand/macro b-roll — a kitchen counter with fabric + scissors + thread reads as atelier at this crop, and (c) AI-generated atelier wides (Veo 3.1 with "sunlit Miami design studio, sewing machine, pattern paper, warm afternoon light" — indistinguishable at 9:16 crop). Under no circumstance do we fake a cheap studio set — empty room beats fake set.
- **Body shots (non-Arielle wearers):** since we're not paying models, all body shots pull from (a) Rielli's existing product photography + campaign archive (she has Sports Illustrated and Forbes footage she owns rights to), (b) AI-generated bodies in Veo 3.1 / Kling 2.5 using her product photography as image-to-video reference — the model never needs a recognizable face, the piece is the hero. Face-forward model shots get cropped to neck-down or shot from behind. Zero model fees, zero AI likeness-rights issue.
- **One shoot day** is the max we should need at the atelier. Baseline plan is phone-only; if atelier access is available, upgrade to it for free.
- **Do not auto-cut a 30s down to a 15s** — every 15s in this spec is designed from zero with its own hook.
- **End card** is a fixed grid: wordmark top, `riellibrand.com` middle, offer (when available) bottom. 18pt offer — never larger than the URL.
- **Upscale path:** AI-generated footage → Magnific. Live atelier → Topaz. Assembly in Premiere with ElevenLabs v3 for any VO passes. Runway Act-Two *only* if Arielle's schedule forces an extension — flag me first.
- **FTC / press:** Only cite press Rielli has provided on their public press page. No borrowed logos. No implying endorsements we don't have.
