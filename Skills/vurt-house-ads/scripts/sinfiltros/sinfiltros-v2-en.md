# SINFILTROS NUTRITION — Ad Scripts v2 (English-primary, Spanglish code-switch)

Research-backed rewrite against `references/audience-insights.md` and `references/anti-slop-guide.md`.

Creative direction:
- Symptom-naming hooks dominate in women's wellness ads. Open inside the problem, not the solution.
- Valentina's 6.5M TikTok following is our single biggest unfair advantage. Her face + voice = social proof for free. Lead two of four spots with her.
- Spanglish code-switch beats straight English OR straight Spanish for US Latinas 22-32. Not translation — the Spanish lands inside the English sentence ("mi mamá said I was being dramatic — turns out my hormones were screaming").
- Palette: terracotta, warm cream, forest olive, black. NOT millennial pink (Bloom owns that and it's saturated).
- FTC-safe: structure/function language only. Never "cures," "treats," "eliminates." Always: *supports, helps with, feels like, takes the edge off.*

Budgets: ~30 words for 15s, ~60 words for 30s.

Products:
- **MOODMIES** — PMS support gummies — $26.99
- **GLOWMIES** — Hair, skin, nails gummies — $26.99
- **Bundle** — $49.99

CTA: `sinfiltrosnutrition.com`

**End-card disclaimer (small, always visible 2s):** *These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. Not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.*

## 30s #1 — "Mi Mamá Said I Was Being Dramatic" (founder, hero)

**Why it works:** Opens on a symptom + cultural detail no competitor can copy. Valentina's voice carries the whole spot. This is the ad that turns her 6.5M TikTok equity into VURT conversions.

**Visual beats:**
- 0.0–3s: Valentina at her kitchen counter, morning light. No makeup. She speaks directly to camera — the first line is the hook.
- 3–10s: Cuts of her going through her morning — coffee pour, Moodmies bottle cap twist, two gummies into her hand.
- 10–18s: Real-woman cuts — one at a desk, one on the couch with a heating pad, one laughing at dinner. Different bodies, different shades, different rooms. **Source: Valentina's existing organic UGC from her TikTok community (she has 6.5M, there's a deep well of rights-cleared user content on her page) OR her own team/friends filmed casually. No paid creators in this campaign.**
- 18–25s: Back to Valentina. Closer. She says the line that reframes the category.
- 25–28s: Product shot — Moodmies + Glowmies bottle pair, bundle box between them. Terracotta/olive surface.
- 28–30s: End card with disclaimer line.

**Valentina on camera (0–3s, hook — 15 words):**
"Mi mamá said I was being dramatic about my period. Turns out — my hormones were screaming."

**Valentina on camera (18–25s, close — 34 words):**
"I made Moodmies because nothing I was taking worked for *me*. And Glowmies for the part that takes longer — the hair, the skin, the nails. Takes four, six weeks. Worth it."

**On-screen text:**
- 3s: `MOODMIES · PMS SUPPORT`
- 14s: `GLOWMIES · HAIR · SKIN · NAILS`
- 25s: `BUNDLE · $49.99`
- 28s: `sinfiltrosnutrition.com`
- 28–30s (small, bottom): *These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA.*

**Sound:** Low-BPM cumbia or slowed reggaeton (think Kali Uchis "Moonlight" tempo, not "Despacito" tempo). Diegetic under Valentina's open — coffee pour, bottle cap — then the bed comes in at 3s.

**Anti-slop check:** No "I was skeptical at first." No "finally, a supplement that actually works." No before/after lighting trick. Her opening line is specifically culturally earned — a Latina told this story first because a Latina lived it first.

## 30s #2 — "Day 24" (symptom-first, VURT drama-environment-native)

**Why it works:** Freeze-frame telenovela cold open matches VURT's content environment — the viewer won't register it as an ad for 2-3 beats. Highest-converting supplement hook pattern (symptom naming) delivered in the register the audience is already watching.

**Visual beats:**
- 0.0–3s: TELENOVELA-STYLE CUT. Freeze-frame on a young Latina mid-argument with her partner in their kitchen. Slight zoom-in. Dramatic sting.
- 3–5s: Text types on the freeze: *"Day 24 of her cycle."*
- 5–10s: Unfreeze. She walks away, closes the bathroom door, leans against it. Pulls her phone out. Orders Moodmies.
- 10–17s: Day 3 of next month — same woman, same couple, different vibe. Sunlight. Coffee together. She pops a Moodmie, he doesn't even notice. Normal.
- 17–23s: Cuts — her at a desk, laughing with a friend on the couch, stretching at a window. Product in frame once (on a counter).
- 23–28s: Tight on the bundle. Terracotta surface. Hand reaches in and picks up the box.
- 28–30s: End card + disclaimer.

**VO — Valentina (32 words total, delivered across 5–22s):**
"Día veinticuatro looks like an argument you didn't mean to start. Moodmies won't fix your relationship — pero te ayudan con el PMS. And that's the part I can help with."

**On-screen text:**
- 3s: `Day 24 of her cycle.`
- 11s: `Day 3 next month.`
- 23s: `MOODMIES · $26.99 · BUNDLE $49.99`
- 28s: `sinfiltrosnutrition.com` + disclaimer small

**Sound:** Telenovela sting at 0.5s — keep it tongue-in-cheek, audience will clock the reference. Warm cumbia bed from 5s onward.

**Anti-slop check:** The drama is earned, not performed — VURT audience is watching *actual* relationship drama between ad breaks; we're matching register, not cosplaying. No POV bathroom mirror. No "this gummy saved my relationship" melodrama (that would be a disease-claim-adjacent compliance red flag *and* dishonest).

## Production notes

- **Phone-only shoot is the default.** Valentina's on-camera + VO is 3–5 min of screen time across all 4 of her spots. Shoot order of operations: (a) she phone-shoots 15s #4 alone — it's supposed to look organic, no crew needed. (b) Video guy shows up at her place for one morning — 2 hours max — and captures the rest on her iPhone / his phone in 4K with a $20 clip-on lav. Her kitchen + bathroom, morning light, no studio. This matches the register of her 6.5M-follower content and costs nothing to produce.
- **Full shoot day optional.** If Valentina prefers crew/camera support, one half-day with the video guy covers 30s #1, 30s #2, 15s #1, 15s #2, and all product laydowns. Phone-only is just as good here — don't add crew to feel "professional," the category winners are founder-phone-shot.
- **No paid creators this campaign.** Any "real women" cuts in the spots pull from: (a) Valentina's rights-cleared organic UGC already on her page, or (b) her own team/friends filmed casually on a phone for free. If we can't source either for a given shot, remove the shot — don't substitute with paid creators or stock.
- **Abuela shoot (15s #3)** — cast a real older Latina from Valentina's circle or family if possible, shoot at her actual kitchen. If not available, use a real abuela's archive footage with consent. Do not hire a model playing abuela — audience will clock it.
- **Spanish version** (`sinfiltros-v2-es.md`) drops the Spanglish code-switches for full Spanish on 30s #1 and 30s #2; keeps English on creator spot. Audience test in-market before Spanish version gets paid spend.
- **FTC discipline:** No "cures PMS." No "eliminates cramps." No "stops hair loss." Every claim lives in structure/function + personal-experience framing. Compliance review before air.
- **No millennial pink.** If any on-set stylist brings in pink as the hero color, redirect to terracotta, olive, warm cream, black.
