# Anti-Slop Guide — What We Don't Write

If a phrase could come from any ad in any category, we don't use it. These are banned outright.

## Banned phrases (any brand)

- "No filters. No extremes."
- "Luxury redefined."
- "Elevate your summer / morning / routine."
- "Made for the modern woman."
- "Finally, a [supplement / pan / swimsuit] that actually works."
- "I was skeptical at first, but…"
- "Effortlessly chic."
- "Game-changer."
- "Trust me on this."
- "Empower your confidence."
- "Made with love."
- "The only [x] you'll ever need."
- "Goodbye [problem]. Hello [solution]." (Olly owns this lockup — don't copy.)

## Banned visual tropes

- Golden-hour silhouette b-roll with no subject
- Drone pullback over turquoise water with no person in frame
- Model laughing at nothing while holding a coconut
- Slow-mo hair flip at sunset
- Gum/tablet fizzing into glass of water
- Bathroom-mirror selfie opener with no context
- Before/after where the lighting obviously changed
- Forced non-stick egg slide with no narrative
- All-white studio kitchen with no human
- Stop-motion gummy dances (Olly owns this)
- Strikethrough price as the opening frame (reads as scam)
- AI-hallucinated hands, smooth plastic skin, warped background blur

## Banned structural moves

- Opening with the product / logo / price
- Voiceover with nobody on camera for 30 straight seconds
- Four beats of product beauty shots before any human
- "Founder confessional" where the story is generic ("I couldn't find what I wanted so I made it" — ok for Rielli only if she has a specific line, not this abstract phrasing)
- Fake urgency ("only 3 left," "last chance") — especially corrosive to brands where scarcity is literally real (Rielli one-of-one)
- Disclaimer crammed onto the end card in unreadable type (FTC bar is "clear and conspicuous")

## AI-slop tells to avoid (specific to 2026)

- **Symmetric faces, glassy skin** — viewers are now trained to spot these. 70% of US consumers question authenticity of branded content.
- **Stock-library piano-house bed** — no taste, reads automated.
- **Robotic VO cadence** — TTS ad reads are the biggest tell in Q2 2026. If we can't shoot live VO, we use ElevenLabs v3 with a cloned voice and real direction.
- **Overused cliché openers that have been parody'd**: "POV: you…" is saturated. Use only when the POV specifically delivers information (e.g., POV: Day 24 of your cycle, crying at a dog food commercial — the POV names a symptom, that's earning its keep).

## What to do instead

- **Show hands.** Stitching. Cooking. Picking up a gummy bottle. Hands that could not be AI-generated.
- **Show the room.** Miami atelier. Sunday's Eatery line. Valentina's actual kitchen. Specificity is the moat.
- **Say one real thing.** One line that could only come from this brand. "These my pots." "Only one of these exists." "Mi mamá said I was being dramatic — turns out my hormones were screaming."
- **Let diegetic sound carry.** Scissors, burners, a bottle being uncapped. Silence-then-one-line beats wall-to-wall narration.
- **Price and offer late, not early.** Build feeling first. Close with the stack.

## The 3-word rule

If I can strike out any 3 consecutive words from a script and the meaning is unchanged, they need to be replaced or cut. Generic phrasing compounds into AI slop.
