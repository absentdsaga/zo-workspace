# Audience Insights — Source Group House Ads (VURT placement)

Research-backed audience briefs per brand. Every script written against this file.

VURT placement specs: 30s non-skippable pre/mid-roll, audio-on, clickable VAST end card. Audience skews urban multicultural 18-34, mobile, drawn to relationship drama and emotional storytelling. Telenovela-adjacent register.

---

## RIELLI — Luxury Swim Buyer

**Who she is (ranked by size of the addressable bucket, not demographic order):**
- 25-34, HH income $150K+, "soft luxury" buyer — she owns Reformation, Aritzia, Toteme, Alo, Djerf Avenue, maybe one Jacquemus bag. Not flashing logos. "If you know, you know."
- 35-54 secondary — multi-trip per year, resort wardrobe, buys a $300 suit without calling a friend first.

**Her purchase triggers, in order:**
1. A trip just booked (primary — luxury swim is a trip-planning purchase)
2. Instagram celeb sighting (Kardashian/SI/Vogue recognition drives it)
3. "Where's that from?" — friend in the same tax bracket wearing it
4. Press appearance (Forbes 30U30, SI Swim, Vogue)
5. Life reset — post-breakup, bachelorette, honeymoon, first pregnancy-free summer

**What she rejects:**
- % off (cheapens the category — luxury swim winners rarely discount in-ad)
- Fake urgency ("only 3 left") — reads as Temu. Rielli's scarcity is literally real; don't shout it
- Generic golden-hour drone b-roll with no subject
- Model laughing at nothing while holding a coconut

**What converts her:**
- Fabric/stitch/hand close-up in frame 1 — craftsmanship is the whole sell at her price
- Single hero piece, not a collection grid
- Real woman in motion (walking out of water, stepping into a boat)
- Founder voice — Arielle's presence is the unreproducible moat vs. Oseree/Agua/Faithfull produced editorial

**Emotional register:** Quiet confidence. Understated. The "not one of many" line is structurally right — it just needs her voice saying it, not a disembodied VO.

**Source anchors:** BoF, Crobox luxury consumer psych, Who What Wear 2026 swim report, Motion DTC hooks catalog.

## SINFILTROS — Latina Wellness Buyer

**Who she is:**
- 22-32, bilingual US Latina, college → early career → early motherhood
- Discovers on TikTok → asks group chat on WhatsApp → buys on DTC if founder story lands, Amazon if it doesn't
- Also owns: Rare Beauty, Glow Recipe, Sol de Janeiro, Fenty, Topicals, Olipop, Poppi, Liquid IV, Stanley, Owala
- Reggaeton-native (Kali Uchis, Young Miko, Tokischa, Bad Bunny). NOT generic Latin-pop stings.

**Her purchase triggers:**
1. TikTok creator she trusts mentioning it (primary)
2. A symptom moment — bad PMS week, skin flare, postpartum hair shed
3. Founder story that resonates (Valentina's post-bariatric arc does this)
4. Group chat screenshot from a friend

**Why gummy beats pill/powder for her specifically:**
- Pills code as prescription / sickness
- Gummies code as self-care / treat
- Fits habit: morning coffee, bathroom counter — ritual, not medicine

**Price tolerance:** $25-35 = impulse. $35-45 = considered. $45+ = needs strong founder story or clinical proof. SinFiltros at $26.99 sits in the impulse zone — lean into it, don't apologize for it.

**What converts her:**
- Symptom naming in the hook ("If your PMS makes you feel like a different person, watch this")
- Spanglish code-switching inside the dialogue, not as translation afterthought
- Abuela reference as legitimacy, not costume ("my abuela had a tea for everything — this is the 2026 version")
- Telenovela-coded micro-drama openers — a freeze-frame "¿otra vez?" reads instantly to the audience and matches VURT's drama environment
- Valentina's face — she has 6.5M followers. Her being in frame IS social proof.

**What she rejects:**
- "Finally, a supplement that actually works" (dead phrase)
- "I was skeptical at first, but…" (dead phrase)
- Millennial pink packaging era (saturated — Bloom owns pink, we're not in that lane)
- Before/after with obvious lighting change (reads as fake)
- Disease claims / medical authority cosplay (FTC risk + she sees through it)

**Compliance language that works AND converts:**
- "Supports me through my cycle"
- "My skin has never been this calm"
- "Feels like my PMS week isn't a whole other person anymore"
- "Takes the edge off"
- Always small end-card line: *These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. Not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.*

**Cultural palette:** Terracotta, warm cream, forest olive, black. Latina clean girl, NOT millennial pink.

**Source anchors:** HMC 2026, inBeat Hispanic marketing, Portada bilingual advertising, FTC Health Products Guidance, Evolut 500-ads supplement analysis, Bloom Nutrition case study (NetInfluencer, Inc).

## TRICK DADDY POTS — Culture-Led Cookware Buyer

**Who he/she is:**
- Millennial 28-43, HH income $60-120K, urban/suburban multicultural
- Cooks 4+ nights a week — home-cook identity is part of self-image
- Hosts Sunday dinner, holidays, birthdays. Takes pride in plate game.
- Grew up on Trick Daddy in rotation (he peaked 2001-2006 — this buyer was 10-20 then and has an emotional anchor)
- Follows Black food creators on IG/TikTok (Kiano Moju, Cheryl Day, Jocelyn Delk Adams, SweetPotatoSoul, Chef Pii)

**Purchase triggers:**
1. Post-Thanksgiving pan failure (November-December window is real)
2. Moving / new apartment
3. New Year kitchen upgrade
4. Tax refund (Feb-April — strong for this segment)
5. Registry / wedding season

**Category blindspot we're exploiting:** Every top cookware DTC ad (Caraway, HexClad, Our Place, Made In) is a bright-quiet-white-kitchen with ambient piano. Even Ayesha Curry's line stays family-safe. **Nobody is making a Liberty City Sunday dinner cookware ad.** That's the whole opening.

**What converts:**
- Real kitchen, real grease, real noise. Sunday's Eatery b-roll is texture no competitor has.
- Miami bass / 2 Live Crew / Trick's own catalog — music as language, not background
- Food shown = food Black audiences cook at home: mac with the crust pull, smothered turkey wings, candied yams, fried ribs, collards with the meat in. Not seared scallops.
- Observational footage > scripted monologue. Trick talks to camera in 5-8 second bursts, not 30s paragraphs.
- Bundle-as-hero framing ($400 value for $169.99) with installments (Affirm/Klarna/Shop Pay — $42.50 × 4)
- Sunday's Eatery as the credibility engine ("From the restaurant to your kitchen")

**What to avoid:**
- Forced non-stick egg-slide (reads as dropshipped Amazon pan)
- All-white studio kitchen with nobody in it
- "Finally, a pan that…" founder confessional
- Stock music bed
- "Restaurant-quality-at-home" as abstract claim (meaningless unless anchored to a real restaurant — anchor it to Sunday's)

**2026 aesthetic tailwind:** Warm earthy neutrals + dark cottagecore + grandma's-kitchen nostalgia. A real Sunday dinner table lands dead center on every 2026 home/kitchen trend without chasing it.

**On-camera Trick performance:** Ratio is ~15s kitchen action / 10s Trick on camera / 5s product + price card. His cadence is declarative Miami drawl — write him one-liners, not paragraphs.

**Variant strategy:** HexClad ships 150+ creatives per sprint. One shoot day at Sunday's Eatery with Trick should produce 20+ cutdowns.

**Source anchors:** HexClad Motion playbook, Caraway Shopify case study, Our Place/Selena Gomez iSpot library, Sunday's Eatery (Local 10, Infatuation Miami), Grand View cookware buyer data, 2026 home/kitchen trend reports.
