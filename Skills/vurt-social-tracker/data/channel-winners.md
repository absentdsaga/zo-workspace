### What's actually true

- **None of these are YT Premieres.** All 42 uploads = standard uploads (no `liveStreamingDetails`). "A VURT Premiere" is a title label — a team convention for "new / spotlight clip" — not the YT Premiere product.
- **None are full episodes.** All three breakouts are 56s–2:30 catalog clips. "Full episode premieres win" was wrong; **short catalog clips with named shows occasionally break out** is the accurate pattern.
- **The 166K / 74K / 40K are outliers, not a baseline.** Most recent uploads land 300–1,500. Favorite Son Premiere (Apr 21) is 1,127. 35 & Ticking Premiere (Apr 20) is 686.

### Current scheduling (per Dioni)

- Catalog clips (older, pulled from back-cat) posted first in the day.
- "Premiere"-labeled clips (newer / brand-new, spotlight-eligible) posted in the late afternoon.
- This cadence is fine — keep it. The correction is vocabulary, not workflow.

### YouTube directive (revised)

- Keep the catalog-AM / premiere-PM cadence.
- **Test YT's real Premiere feature** for new originals — scheduled with countdown + live chat. Cheap to run, clean A/B.
- **Don't claim "full-episode premieres win"** until we've actually uploaded one. That's a missing data point, not a known pattern.
- Named show + named talent in every title (the three breakouts all share this).
- Rich description with cast / director / genre keywords + `myvurt.com` (compounds into organic search per GA4).
