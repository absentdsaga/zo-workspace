# VURT Knowledge Base

## Company Overview
- VURT = vertical-first streaming platform for indie filmmakers
- Tagline: "Stories told OUR Way. Built for the Culture."
- Subtitle: "Home Of The Vertical Filmmakers And Creators"
- Website: myvurt.com
- App: iOS App Store, Google Play, and web
- Launched: March 17, 2026 (TechCrunch coverage by Lauren Forristal)
- 100+ episodes of original micro-series, full-length films, TV shows at launch

## Business Model
- AVOD (advertising-based video on demand)
- Non-exclusive licensing agreement
- 50/50 revenue split with creators
- Direct submission — no aggregator or distributor required
- 48-72 hour turnaround from approval to live on platform
- Advance Distribution Program planned — capital advances against content value pre-distribution
- Creator equity model — creators are stakeholders, not suppliers

### Episode Specs
- Episodes: 60 seconds to 5 minutes
- Designed for sequential viewing and bingeability
- Each episode functions standalone while advancing story
- Strong openings encouraged, strong cliffhangers required
- Vertical compositions should feel designed, not cropped

### Content Standards
- Narrative-driven with clear point of view
- Culturally fluent without over-explaining identity
- Clean audio, intentional framing, thoughtful pacing
- Visual consistency across episodes
- Clear subtitles or closed captions
- NO: low-effort/algorithm-driven content, influencer vlogs, reaction videos, shock-value content

## Brand Identity

### Personality
- Confident, not loud. Intentional, not stiff.
- Culturally fluent as lived experience, not costume.
- Cinematic by design — crafted with care and restraint.
- "VURT doesn't ask for permission. VURT knows exactly who it's for and why it exists."

### Voice & Tone
- Direct and clean. Short sentences. Clear intent. No fluff.
- No hype words, exaggerated promises, or influencer-style excitement.
- "If it reads like it's trying too hard, it's not VURT."
- Culture is present but never performed.

### Words We Use
Stories, Culture, Ownership, Episodes, Series, Vertical Cinema, Creators, Originals, Community

### Words We Avoid
Content, Influencer, Snackable, Low Budget, User Generated, Viral, AI

### Caption Style
- One strong idea per post
- Minimal punctuation
- No excessive hashtags
- Emojis rare and purposeful
- White space is okay
- "A good VURT caption feels like a statement, not an announcement."

### Gut Check Before Posting
1. Does this feel confident or loud?
2. Are we spotlighting the story or ourselves?
3. Would this still make sense a year from now?
4. Does this feel true to the culture or forced?

### Colors
- VURT Black: #0B0B0B (primary backgrounds)
- Gold Highlight: #F5D27A (subtle reflections, top edge gradients)
- Core Gold: #D4A63A (logo gradient center, premium accents)
- Deep Gold: #9C6B12 (shadow areas, cinematic weight)
- Warm White: #F2F2F2 (headlines, titles, body text on dark)
- Soft Gray: #9A9A9A (metadata, captions, secondary text)

### Typography
- Headline: Neue Haas Grotesk (fallback: Inter)
- Body/UI: Inter
- Accent: IBM Plex Mono (sparingly — episode numbers, timestamps, metadata)
- Rule: "If typography ever feels decorative, playful, or attention-seeking, it's not VURT."

### Logo
- Full VURT wordmark + Iconic "V" mark
- Always high-contrast backgrounds
- Never stretch, skew, add shadows/outlines/effects
- Never on busy backgrounds without solid/gradient overlay

### Social Media Philosophy
- "Front porch, not a billboard" — invite conversation, not announcements
- "Curated feed, not a repost dump" — quality and pacing matter
- "Creator amplifier, not a brand ego page" — the work comes first
- Posting priorities: story clips, creator spotlights, cultural commentary, platform updates (sparingly), community wins
- Rule: "If a post doesn't serve the story, the creator, or the community, it doesn't belong on the feed."

### North Star Message
"VURT was the only platform that TRULY understood authentic cinema and culturally rich storytelling in the vertical era."

### Creator Promise
- Distribution without dilution
- Vertical adaptation support (preserving tone, pacing, intention)
- Visibility without algorithm roulette (curated, not random)
- Respect for original vision
- Platform built for longevity, not burnout
- Commitment: "If a decision benefits the platform but harms the creator or the story, it's the wrong decision."
