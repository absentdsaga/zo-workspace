## The legal theory

Post-death collection of SSA retirement benefits is a well-worn federal prosecution pattern called **"deceased payee fraud"** (Retirement and Survivors Insurance — RSI). The classic fact pattern:

1. Beneficiary dies abroad or at home.
2. Family member controls the bank account that receives the direct deposit.
3. They delay reporting the death so the deposits keep arriving.
4. They either leave the funds in the account and draw them down, or transfer them out to their own accounts.

Federal statutes implicated:

- **42 U.S.C. § 408** — felony SS fraud (false statements, converting benefits of another). 5-year SOL; up to 5 years in prison.
- **18 U.S.C. § 641** — theft/conversion of US government money. 5-year SOL; up to 10 years if > $1,000.
- **18 U.S.C. § 1343** — wire fraud. 5 years ordinarily, but **10 years when conduct affects a financial institution** — the SSA → Bank One International → Scotiabank wires plausibly qualify. As of April 2026, wire fraud conduct from June–November 2021 is still within the 10-year window, but the clock is ticking — no delay.
- **31 U.S.C. § 3729 (False Claims Act)** — civil, up to 10 years.

Administrative clawback by SSA has **no statute of limitations** (SSR 70-54, 20 C.F.R. § 404.502/506). SSA can always demand the money back from whoever received it.

**Standing to file:** Anyone can file. Gisela's status as sole heiress is context that helps, not a gate. Even anonymous reports are accepted.

**Who recovers the money:** US Treasury recovers the SS overpayment (not the estate). Gisela's recovery lane for the misappropriated funds is (a) criminal restitution if DOJ prosecutes Amarilys, and (b) a DR civil/criminal action over the Scotiabank account drainage.

### 3. HHS OIG — Medicare fraud (parallel, separate)

Medicare goes to HHS OIG, not SSA OIG. File the same day:

What they want: SSN / HIC number, date of death, suspicion that claims were billed against his coverage after death. HHS-OIG has an active work-plan project exactly on this (W-00-24-31497 — "MCO Payments for Services After Beneficiaries' Deaths"), so the intake path is well-traveled.

## Realistic expectations

- **Time horizon:** Federal investigations are slow. Expect 6–18 months before anything visible happens on the US side. The DR bank investigation and the DR criminal denuncia move faster and in parallel.
- **What you'll get back in the short term:** a tip number, and possibly a call from an OIG agent asking for more detail and evidence.
- **What "success" looks like:** SSA establishes the overpayment against Amarilys (she gets a federal debt bill), DOJ may prosecute for § 408 / § 641 / § 1343, and the case becomes leverage in the DR civil/criminal forum.
- **What it does NOT do:** directly put money into Gisela's pocket. That happens through the DR side (recovering Scotiabank funds, civil claim for enriquecimiento sin causa, vehicle recovery).

## Relationship between the US and DR cases

They are independent and cumulative. The US prosecutes theft of US government money + the wire. The DR prosecutes estafa/abuso de confianza/lavado of the funds once they landed in Scotiabank and were used on DR soil. No double-jeopardy bar between two sovereigns. Coordinating: the DR fiscalía can oficie to SSA/FBU via MLAT if needed; the US case can use DR bank records obtained through the DR criminal case.
