# Testament Supremacy — Defensive Doctrine

This case pivots on one fact: **there is a valid testamento auténtico, Gisela is the sole heiress, and it was protocolized at a notario.** That changes the legal character of every adversary claim.

## The principle

Once a valid will exists and is unchallenged on formal grounds (capacity of testator, formal requirements, genuine signature), the testator's wishes are sovereign within the limits of forced heirship. Under DR law, because Delio had **no descendants and no surviving parents**, he had **full testamentary freedom** (Arts. 913–915 CC, read together with Art. 1002).

Therefore:

- Amarilys is not an heir. She is a **stranger to the succession** for any purpose beyond being a family member.
- Siblings have **no reserve** in DR law. They take under intestacy only when the decedent dies without a will. Delio did not.
- Any move Amarilys made that assumed she was an heir — the late death declaration as "declarante", the car transfer she allegedly authorized, the attempted acto de notoriedad — was done **without legal standing**. That doesn't retroactively give her rights; it strengthens the case that she acted fraudulently.

## How to respond to Amarilys's three recurring arguments

### "Delio owed Luis José money"
- Even if true, **debts don't make her an heir**. Alleged creditors of the estate file a claim against the heir (Gisela) with proof. Gisela can accept, dispute, or repudiate. Amarilys has no role.
- **Demand the proof:** Pagaré, contrato, sentencia, reconocimiento firmado por Delio. Without a written título, the claim fails under DR evidentiary practice for sums of meaningful value.

### "Delio was going to give me the casa two years ago"
- Real property transfers require **written form** (Art. 1589 CC — promesa de venta; Ley 108-05 for the registered transfer itself).
- Even if a written promesa existed, the will (dated later) **revokes inconsistent prior dispositions** per Art. 967 CC's "revocability of wills" doctrine — the will is the final expression.
- **No writing = no argument.** One-line response.

### "I should get a share because I'm his sister"
- Not under a will with no forced heirs. DR law does not award sibling shares when the decedent legally disinherits them by universal legatee designation (Gisela).
- If she claims the will is invalid: she must prove it. Burden is on her. She'd need to attack: (a) capacity of testator, (b) formal defects, (c) forgery, or (d) posterior revocation. She has alleged none of these with substance.

## What must NEVER be conceded in written communication

- That Amarilys is an "heir" or "co-heir" — she isn't.
- That there's a "negotiation" to be had. **There is nothing to negotiate.** There is a voluntary return of what she holds without authority, or there is litigation.
- That any oral promise or claim has weight.
- That the family's delay (2021 → 2025) compromises our position — it does not. All relevant actions are well within prescription.

## What CAN be conceded as operational kindness

- Family-relationship language ("tía") in non-legal messaging, while maintaining firm legal posture.
- Allowing her to exit gracefully (return documents, vehicle, drop the false claims) without pursuing denuncia penal, IF the abogado thinks it buys speed. This is **our discretion** — not a right of hers.
- Time for her to respond to formal requerimientos before escalating.

## The procedural truth

Even with an airtight will, DR succession still requires:
1. Declaratoria de Herederos (notarial o judicial) confirming who the heirs are per the will.
2. DGII registration and payment/exemption of impuesto sucesoral (3%).
3. Registro de Títulos update for real property.
4. Banking institutions' own heredero validation procedures.

Every one of those steps is **administrative, not adversarial**. Amarilys's only ways to slow us down are to inject adversarial filings (oposiciones, tercerías, penal complaints of her own). Each of those will fail on the merits but can cost time. Our defensive posture is:
- **Move fast** on each administrative step.
- **Make the record** — ensure every step is documented, signed, dated, stamped.
- **Do not engage with her narrative** in any filing or public record — simply execute against the testament.
