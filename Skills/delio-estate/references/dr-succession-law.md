# Dominican Republic Succession Law — Reference

Grounding for answers on this case. When citing law to Dioni or the abogado, use this file rather than improvising.

## Hierarchy of law applicable

1. **Constitución Dominicana** — property rights, due process.
2. **Código Civil Dominicano** — substantive law of successions (Arts. 718 et seq.) and contracts (Arts. 1101 et seq.).
3. **Ley 2569 de 1950 sobre Sucesiones y Donaciones** (as amended) — the fiscal side: 3% impuesto sucesoral, DGII registration, exemption thresholds.
4. **Ley 108-05 de Registro Inmobiliario** + Reglamento — process for titles, duplicados, oposiciones, anotaciones preventivas.
5. **Ley 659 sobre Actos del Estado Civil** — acta de defunción, actos de notoriedad, declaraciones tardías.
6. **Ley 140-15 del Notariado** — notarial acts, responsibility of notarios.
7. **Código Penal Dominicano** — falsedad (Arts. 145 et seq.), estafa (405), abuso de confianza (408), robo (379+).
8. **Código de Procedimiento Civil** — oposición, demandas, medidas conservatorias (embargo retentivo, Art. 48+).

## Testamentary succession (our case)

### Types of will in DR
- **Testamento auténtico** (most common, strongest) — made before notario + witnesses per Arts. 971–975 CC. Enforceable immediately on death. Generally what "testamento" means colloquially.
- **Testamento ológrafo** — fully handwritten, signed, dated by testator. Must be judicially "protocolized" after death to be enforceable.
- **Testamento místico** (sealed) — rare.

### Key articles
- **Art. 895 CC** — Succession opens at death at the last domicile.
- **Art. 967 CC** — Will is always revocable until death.
- **Art. 1002 CC** — Universal legatee (heredero universal) inherits the whole estate not otherwise disposed of.
- **Art. 1004 CC** — Universal legatee takes possession via *envío en posesión* when there are forced heirs; otherwise directly.
- **Art. 1006 CC** — If no forced heirs (reservatarios), universal legatee is seized ("saisine") by operation of law on death.

### Forced heirship
- Descendants are forced heirs. If there are none, **parents can be forced heirs** (Arts. 913–915 CC).
- **Our case:** Delio died without descendants; parents were already deceased (both predeceased him per acta). Therefore **no forced heirs survived Delio**. He had full testamentary freedom. Gisela as sole heir by will is ironclad.

## Intestate succession (what Amarilys would have needed)

Only applies when no valid will. Since there IS a valid will, this is moot for our case — but for completeness:
- Order without descendants, spouse, or parents: **siblings and their descendants per stirpes** (Arts. 745, 750 CC).
- Amarilys is a sibling — she WOULD have inherited her share in intestacy. But the will overrides this.

## Declaratoria / Determinación de Herederos

Required to register a succession with DGII and to move property titles.

- **Can be judicial** (instancia before Tribunal de Primera Instancia) or **via notario** (acta de notoriedad under Ley 140-15 / Ley 659).
- **When testament exists:** the testament is the basis; the declaratoria recognizes who the heirs are per the will, not invents them.
- **Who must be included:** heirs as identified by the will. With a universal legatee, she is the sole party needed, though the tribunal sometimes requires notice to intestate heirs who would have inherited but for the will, especially when there's a challenge.

## Registro de Títulos process for duplicado (our casa path)

Under Ley 108-05 and its Reglamento:
1. Declaración jurada de pérdida (notariada).
2. Certificación de la Policía Nacional por pérdida del título.
3. Publicación en periódico(s) de circulación nacional (usually 2-3 publications, 15 days apart, depending on practice).
4. Depósito de expediente at the Registro de Títulos correspondiente (Santiago for Puñal).
5. Registro revisa, publica su propio aviso, espera plazo de oposición (15 días hábiles).
6. Si no hay oposición válida: emite duplicado.
7. Si hay oposición: abre incidente; puede elevarse al Tribunal Superior de Tierras.

To update the name on the title to Gisela requires:
- Sentencia de Declaratoria de Herederos.
- Constancia DGII de registro de sucesión.
- These are deposited and the Registro emits el nuevo Certificado a nombre de la heredera.

## Fiscal (DGII) path

- **Ley 2569:** 3% sobre el caudal hereditario neto (net estate) as impuesto sucesoral.
- Plazo: **90 días** desde la apertura de la sucesión (desde el fallecimiento) para presentar la declaración jurada (Art. 26 Ley 2569). Extendible.
- Late filing: recargos, intereses. The family is 5 years past the original plazo but this is administratively normalizable — DGII routinely processes late sucesiones.
- Exención: if the estate is below the threshold (currently ~RD$500,000 in net value, but check current indexation) or if the heredera qualifies for specific exemptions.

## Protection tools available to us

- **Oposición al duplicado** (adversary could file) — 15-day window after Registro publishes its own aviso.
- **Anotación preventiva** (we file) — blocks transfer recording during dispute.
- **Embargo retentivo** — Art. 48+ CPC. Useful to freeze Scotiabank account or other funds.
- **Acción en nulidad** — against any falsified act (car transfer, fraudulent heredero declaration).
- **Denuncia penal** — Procuraduría Santiago. Crimes: falsedad (145+ CP), estafa (405 CP), abuso de confianza (408 CP), extorsión (400 CP).

## Prescriptions / statutes of limitation (key windows)

- **Acción en nulidad** por vicio del consentimiento: 5 años (Art. 1304 CC) from discovery.
- **Acción pauliana** (acreedores/herederos contra actos fraudulentos de tercero): 5 años.
- **Petición de herencia:** generally 30 years; against a possessor in bad faith, some doctrine argues imprescriptible.
- **Acción penal** por estafa/falsedad: varía, ~3–10 años según gravedad (Código Procesal Penal).

We are within all material windows for our case, with the oldest adversarial act being 2021 (vehicle) → 4.5 years elapsed.
