# Amarilys — Catalogued Fraudulent / Adversarial Moves

Each entry: **what happened / date / evidence location / legal characterization / defensive countermove.**

---

## 1. Fraudulent vehicle title transfer
- **What:** Nissan transferred out of Delio's name on **20 Sep 2021**, 3 months *before* the death certificate was even filed.
- **Evidence:** Matrícula photos in WhatsApp (24 Oct 2025, ~1:29 PM). `evidence/whatsapp/davie-chat.txt` lines 194–234.
- **Legal characterization:**
  - **Falsedad en escritura pública o privada** — Art. 145–147 Código Penal Dominicano (if a notary was used) / Art. 150 CP (private document).
  - **Estafa** — Art. 405 CP (using false document or false quality to obtain property).
  - **Nulidad absoluta** of the transfer — Art. 1108 CC (falta de consentimiento — deceased cannot consent) and Art. 1131 CC (causa ilícita).
- **Countermove:**
  - Acción en nulidad against the acto de venta/traspaso.
  - Solicitud a DGII / impuestos internos for full transfer paperwork (who signed, notary used, stated price).
  - If a notary is identified, report to the **Colegio Dominicano de Notarios** and possibly the Procuraduría for criminal falsedad.
  - Request to Policía Nacional / fiscalía to recover the vehicle or block further transfer (oposición).

## 2. Late "declaración tardía" of death (weaponized)
- **What:** Amarilys filed the death declaration on **10 Nov 2021**, five months after real death (13 Jun 2021). Per Davie, purpose was to legitimize US Social Security checks she was already cashing.
- **Evidence:** Acta de defunción page in `evidence/caso-herencia-delio.pdf` (pg 1).
- **Legal characterization:**
  - Declaración tardía is legal in DR if done in good faith, but **combined with prior unauthorized acts (car transfer, SS collection)** it's evidence of a plan to control the estate pre-declaration.
  - **US side:** cashing SS checks after the holder's death = **18 U.S.C. § 641** (theft of public money) and **18 U.S.C. § 1001** (false statements); also **42 U.S.C. § 408** (SSA fraud). This is a **US federal matter** regardless of DR civil process.
- **Countermove:**
  - Preserve the acta with its visible "DECLARACIÓN TARDÍA" marking.
  - If family wants to pursue: report to **SSA Office of the Inspector General (OIG)** at https://oig.ssa.gov/report — can be done online. Evidence: real death date, declaration date, likely proof she received funds.
  - Not mandatory for the DR succession — but preserves federal leverage if she escalates.

## 3. Attempted fraudulent *determinación de herederos*
- **What:** Handwritten list (photographed by Davie) of documents Amarilys wanted to gather for a *determinación de herederos*, specifically requesting **7 cédulas of community members who "sepan firmar"** and **"no tengan su apellido"** — i.e., strangers willing to serve as witnesses in an acto de notoriedad.
- **Evidence:** Handwritten note in `evidence/caso-herencia-delio.pdf` (pg 2). Context in WhatsApp: Davie (26 Oct 2025 7:13 PM) — *"Ella quería hacer una determinación de heredero para vender la casa sin autorización familiar."*
- **Legal characterization:**
  - Attempted **falsedad** — falsified *acto de notoriedad* (Art. 70 Ley 659 sobre Actos del Estado Civil; Arts. 145 et seq. CP).
  - **Tentativa de estafa** — she wanted to sell the casa after the fraud act.
  - Because testament exists, any notario who processed this with Amarilys as heir (she isn't one under the will) would be complicit.
- **Countermove:**
  - Send the handwritten note + Davie's WhatsApp messages (ideally in a notarized *acta de comprobación*) to the new abogado-notario for the expediente.
  - When filing our duplicate-title expediente at Registro de Títulos, preemptively cite this as grounds to reject any counter-filing from Amarilys's side.

## 5. False debt claim — "Luis José"
- **What:** Amarilys's abogado claims Delio owed money to Luis José ("hermano de cabo"). Used to justify carving money out of the estate.
- **Evidence:** WhatsApp 24 Oct 2025, 10:55–11:04 AM.
- **Legal characterization:**
  - **No value without written proof** — claims on an estate require *título* (contract, promissory note, sentencia judicial, reconocimiento de deuda). DR civil practice: "los muertos no pagan" — a dead person's debts only bind to the extent they can be proven and were not repudiated by the heir (Gisela).
  - If paraded publicly without proof, could be **injurias o calumnias** against Delio's estate.
- **Countermove:**
  - In any meeting/filing: demand the documentary proof of the alleged debt. No document → claim dies.
  - Gisela (as sole heiress) can formally repudiate any non-documented claim.

## 6. False promise claim — "Delio was going to give me the casa"
- **What:** Amarilys claims Delio intended to transfer the casa to her "2 years before death".
- **Evidence:** WhatsApp 24 Oct 2025, 10:56 AM.
- **Legal characterization:**
  - **Legally irrelevant.** DR law (like most civil systems) requires written form for real-property transfers — Art. 1589 CC (promesa de venta) and registro inmobiliario requirements under Ley 108-05. Oral intentions are not enforceable against a valid testament.
  - The testamento is authenticated and post-dates any such alleged "promise" (or at least is the final expression of intent per Art. 967 CC on revocability of wills).
- **Countermove:** None needed. Let her raise it in a filing; the abogado responds with "prueba escrita o caduco".

## Cross-cutting pattern

Amarilys's moves have a **consistent shape**: take immediate control of Delio's documents and assets right after death → use them to collect the pension → transfer the car → attempt to falsify an heir declaration → when caught, pivot to invented debts and extortionate demands. Each move in isolation could be explained away; stacked, they paint a clear picture of a coordinated fraud, which is exactly how a DR criminal court would analyze it if the family chose to escalate.
