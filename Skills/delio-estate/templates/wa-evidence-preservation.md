# Template — Acta Notarial de Comprobación (WhatsApp)

**Purpose:** Convert WhatsApp screenshots and export into court-admissible evidence via a DR notario's *acta de comprobación*. A raw screenshot is weak evidence; a notario physically witnessing the messages on the original device and stamping it is strong.

**Who executes:** Dioni's or Davie's device holder (whoever has the original conversation) brings the phone to a notario. Notario performs the acta.

---

## What the acta should cover

1. **Identification of the notario** and date/place of acta.
2. **Identification of the party presenting the device** (Dioni or Davie), with cédula.
3. **Identification of the device**: make, model, phone number, IMEI if possible.
4. **Identification of the application**: WhatsApp, version.
5. **Identification of the conversation**: contact name as saved + phone number of the other party (Davie's or, if Dioni's device, the contact "Davie Elvis rosarios s.a").
6. **Transcription / printing of the messages** the notario observes on screen, with their timestamps.
7. **Notario's constancia** that she/he personally observed each message on the device and that the reproduction attached is faithful.
8. **Annexos**: printed screenshots (each page stamped), exported *.txt* chat, and optionally photos of the device showing the conversation.

## Why this matters

Under DR evidentiary practice, a message exported from WhatsApp is reproducible on any computer and can be challenged as fabricated. The *acta de comprobación* transforms ephemeral digital content into a notarized instrument with a public-faith presumption (*fe pública notarial*), which is harder for the adversary to dispute without filing a falsificación counterclaim against the notario — something they won't do.

Cost of acta: typically RD$3,000–10,000 depending on length and notario. Cheap insurance.

## Practical checklist before the visit

- [ ] Pick the right device (where the conversation originates, not a forwarded copy).
- [ ] Turn on airplane mode during the acta so no message edits/deletes can occur mid-session.
- [ ] Have the phone charged.
- [ ] Bring cédula of the owner of the device.
- [ ] Bring a printed copy of the key messages (list above) so the notario knows what to capture.
- [ ] Request **at least 2 originales** of the acta (one for the expediente, one archive).
