## SUBJECT OF COMPLAINT

This is a report of potential Medicare fraud involving post-death use of a deceased beneficiary's coverage and possible post-death collection of related federal benefits.

## DECEASED BENEFICIARY

- Name: **Delio Antonio Vásquez Vásquez**
- SSN (HIC base): **584-52-1788**
- DOB: 13 April 1941
- Date of death: **13 June 2021**, Santiago, Dominican Republic
- Medicare enrollment confirmed by HHS/CMS letter sent to his DR mailing address regarding the transition to the new Medicare card (original letter in file).

## SUSPECTED PARTY

- Name: **Amarilys Altagracia Vásquez** (DR cédula 031-0235285-7), sister of the deceased.
- She obtained possession of Delio's Social Security card and other personal documents following his death and has told family members she was collecting his Social Security benefits.
- The death was not reported to any civil registry until **10 November 2021 — nearly 5 months after actual death** — consistent with a deliberate effort to keep federal benefits flowing and to preserve the appearance of an active beneficiary.

## REQUEST

We ask HHS OIG to verify:

1. Whether any Medicare Part A, Part B, or Part D claims were billed against HIC 584-52-1788 after 13 June 2021.
2. Whether Medicare Advantage (Part C) or Part D capitation payments continued to be made after the date of death (the HHS OIG Work Plan project W-00-24-31497 is directly relevant).
3. Whether any provider on record billed services claimed to have been rendered after 13 June 2021.
