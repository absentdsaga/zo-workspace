## NARRATIVE OF SUSPECTED FRAUD

Delio Antonio Vásquez was a US Social Security Title II retirement beneficiary residing in the Dominican Republic. SSA direct-deposited his monthly benefits through Bank One International Corporation (ABA 026009797 / SWIFT FNBCUS33) to Scotiabank Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic, onward-credit account 1058909, to his personal account number 302639. His 2018 SSA-1099 shows benefits of $15,888 that year (approximately $1,324 per month; with standard COLAs by 2021, likely ~$1,300–1,400 per month).

Delio died on **13 June 2021** at his home in Puñal, Santiago, Dominican Republic. His death was natural and was known immediately to his family and community.

His sister, **Amarilys Altagracia Vásquez (DR cédula 031-0235285-7)**, did not report the death to the Dominican civil registry until **10 November 2021 — five months after the actual death**. She personally filed the late acta de defunción and is listed on it as the declarante.

Family members report that Amarilys obtained possession of Delio's Social Security card and other personal documents following his death and has stated to relatives that she was collecting his Social Security payments. The ~5-month delay between the real death (13 Jun 2021) and the official death report (10 Nov 2021) is consistent with a deliberate effort to keep SSA deposits flowing to Scotiabank account 302639 while preventing SSA from being notified. During that window, approximately five monthly payments of ~$1,300–1,400 each (approximately $6,500–$7,000 total) would have been deposited. The family has not been able to obtain independent confirmation that payments ceased on 10 Nov 2021, or whether any further Title II payments were issued after that date. The Scotiabank account has not yet been audited for post-death activity.

Amarilys has engaged in a pattern of bad-faith conduct toward the estate more broadly, including:

- A fraudulent title transfer of Delio's Nissan vehicle recorded on **20 September 2021** — three months after his actual death and before the death was officially reported.
- Refusal to hand over the original Certificado de Título of the casa (real property).
- Attempts in October 2025 to stage a fraudulent *determinación de herederos* using strangers (neighbors who do not share the Vásquez surname) to swear up a false *acto de notoriedad*.
- Monetary demands of up to 8 million DOP to release the estate property.

These facts are independently corroborated by testamentary records (Delio's testamento auténtico naming Gisela as sole heiress, opened by the notario), the Dominican police certification of the casa title loss (filed December 2025), WhatsApp conversations with the cousin who lives adjacent to the estate, and bank correspondence from SSA showing the international direct-deposit instructions.

We request:

1. Verification of payment records on SSN 584-52-1788 from 13 June 2021 forward, including all IDD wires to Scotiabank 302639.
2. Investigation of representative-payee records (if any were filed) and any SSA-7161 / SSA-7162 Foreign Enforcement Questionnaires returned during the fraud window.
3. Establishment of the overpayment and identification of the responsible party (Amarilys Altagracia Vásquez).
4. Referral to the US Attorney's Office as appropriate (42 USC § 408, 18 USC § 641, 18 USC § 1343).
