## Section 2 — Trade-Secret Operational Mechanics (How These Things Run At Scale)

These are operational levers documented across multiple operators (Fixated, Lumina, Spade, Clipping Culture, Cluely, MrBeast/Vyro, Whop, N3on, etc.). Where Fixated-specific behavior is known it's flagged; otherwise these are **the playbook patterns** Dioni's contact would call "trade secrets."

### 2.1 — Bot stack

- **Sx Bot / Clipify** is the dominant off-the-shelf clipping Discord bot. Features documented at https://docs.sxbot.io/clipify/clipify-the-1-clipping-bot-for-discord:
  - Account-link/verify (TikTok, IG, YT, X) — required before submitting.
  - Submission → admin review queue → public/private logs.
  - Custom payout rates per platform (e.g., "$10 per 100,000 TikTok views, $15 per 150,000 Twitter/X views").
  - Per-campaign budget cap with auto-lock when budget hits.
  - Leaderboard with rankings.
  - Manual admin disbursement (no automatic on-chain payment).
  - Premium-only — there's no free tier. Sx Bot reports >$250K in payouts in a 2-month window (Clipify landing).
- **Custom-built bots** at scale: top operators (Clipping by Anthony Fujiwara, Lumina, Cluely, Vyro) build internal tooling rather than rely on Sx Bot — typically Python/Node bots that scrape TikTok/IG/YT view counts via API + fingerprint suspicious account patterns.
- **Vyro (MrBeast)** has an **hourly view-count refresh + hourly payout** model with built-in dashboard; Stripe + PayPal + crypto wallet payouts. Onboarding at app.vyro.com.
- **Whop's stack** (the platform Clipping Culture and others run on top of):
  - **Submission within 1 hour** of posting to the platform or payment is forfeited (clippa.net guide).
  - **Hourly view-count check**, instant payouts when threshold hit.
  - **24-hour payout delay** as fraud buffer (gives Whop window to claw back fake views).
  - **AI Content Reward review system** with statuses: Pending → Approved / Flagged / Rejected. Auto-approve at 48h if AI flags as legit.
  - **Anti-bot algorithm** detects view-botting post-approval, **lifetime bans** for confirmed botters.

### 2.2 — Content distribution mechanics ("vault" / "drops")

- **Campaign brief = the "drop."** Brief lives in a dedicated channel (e.g., `#campaign-briefs`). Content (raw footage / source video / brand asset pack / hook list / required hashtags / mandatory captions / on-screen text rules) is dropped as a thread.
- **Brief specifies:**
  - 30-second minimum length, vertical 9:16, captions required, no competitor promos, original audio rules, post-within-24–48-hours-of-source-publication (Whop standards via autoclip.dev).
  - Tag requirements: e.g., bbno$ "Diamonds Are Forever" required tagging the artist; "memes are not allowed" rule on certain campaigns (Variety music piece).
  - **Geographic / audience-source requirements:** "50–60% USA audience" with proof — "a screen recording of your analytics showing geographic breakdown" (clippa.net Whop guide). This is the "audience source verification" mechanic Dioni's brief flags.
- **Vault** = pre-cleared B-roll / footage library. Pinned Discord posts or attached Drive links so 1K+ clippers don't all have to be sent assets individually. Clipping Culture's published 7-step process (https://clippingculture.com/blog/how-does-clipping-work) treats this as Step 1 (Source Content Selection).
- **Hook isolation:** Clippers are instructed to identify a **1–2 second hook** at the front of clips. Quote (NPR/Delaware Public Media, Oct 29, 2025): "You have to identify a hook, and the hook is in the first one to two seconds of a video, something that really pops out at you." — Anthony Fujiwara.

### 2.3 — Tiered roles & progression

- **Whop tier system** is informal: top performers get pinged for invite-only campaigns (the "Elite Clippers" waitlist tier on Clipping Culture's Whop landing).
- **Fixated** uses an **explicit two-tier server model** (open ~4K + closed ~100 white-glove). Most agencies copy this.
- **Lumina Clippers** publishes a "gig vs infrastructure" framing — beginner clippers as freelance, top performers promoted to "Captain" / "Chief Clipping Officer" role with stacked campaigns and consistent payouts.
  - Quote (luminaclippers, Apr 28, 2026, https://x.com/luminaclippers/status/2049115977552441529):
    > "the clipping industry is about to split into two groups. clippers who treat it like a gig … make $200/month, burn out. clippers who treat it like infrastructure … make $5k/month on autopilot."
- **Captain layer (typical structure across operators):**
  - 1 captain per ~50–100 clippers.
  - Captains review submissions, escalate good clips, get **20% commission on squad earnings** (per X-search synthesis of operator threads).
  - Promoted from clipper ranks based on consistent CPM performance + view thresholds.
  - **Chief Clipping Officer (CCO)** — emerging exec role. Ollie Forsyth listed CCO at #4 on his "essential new media roles" tweet (Apr 29, 2026). Clippster_ai (an internal CCO promotion) celebrated achieving the title (Apr 29, 2026).
- **Power law principle (Roy Lee / Cluely thread, Jul 2025, https://x.com/im_roy_lee/status/1945753984339431789):**
  > "Power law exists in everything, including clippers. 1% of your clippers will generate 99% of your views."
  - Implication: lean payout structure pays NOTHING per video, only on view-bonus, so under-performers cost zero.

### 2.4 — Watermark / submission proof systems

- **Standard proof-of-post requirement:** Clipper submits the live URL to Discord channel (or via bot `/submit` command), which the bot scrapes for view count.
- **Watermark / branded overlay:** Some campaigns require an on-screen watermark (handle, app icon, or campaign-specific text) so clips are recognizably part of the campaign. This is enforced via review.
- **Watermark removal as fraud:** Whop's anti-bot detects watermark-stripped versions or duplicate posts. Clippa.net guide notes: clippers who rip out tool watermarks (e.g., free-tier Opus Clip watermark) by exporting XML to DaVinci Resolve and resizing footage. Mainstream agencies treat this as standard clipper hygiene, not fraud.
- **Account-warmup protocol** (clippa.net Whop guide — this is the most operationally specific public doc on getting clipper accounts to NOT shadowban):
  - Days 1–3: scroll/like/comment/follow only (no posts).
  - Day 4: first post.
  - Days 5–7: max one post/day.
  - New accounts that post immediately face shadowbanning ("zero views no matter how good they are").
  - Implication: agencies that scale fast either pay a kickback to existing-account holders or build their own warmed-account farms (often offshore, India/Nigeria — see N3on below).

### 2.5 — Payout cadence + escrow tricks

- **Whop:** hourly view check + 24-hour payout delay (fraud buffer) + instant on-platform payout. 10% Whop take.
- **Vyro:** hourly view check, hourly wallet update, withdraw anytime via Stripe / PayPal / crypto.
- **Clipping (Fujiwara):** $50 per 100,000 views originally, evolved to $300–$1,500 per million views. Subscription fee from clients of $2,500–$10,000/mo (sometimes more) ON TOP of the per-view spend. ~$7.7M sales in 2025, much in crypto (Bloomberg via Spokesman, Oct 28, 2025).
- **Clipping Culture:** $0.10 to $3 CPM, with **per-video cap** of $100–$200 max (the cap is the escrow trick — even if a clip hits 50M views, payout is capped). Top earners $10K–$20K/mo, viral spikes >$100K/mo.
- **N3on / Adin Ross network (paid via Kick):** $40 per 100,000 views standard, $50 when boosting incentives. **303 clippers paid $1.4M over 5 weeks.** ~half the network is in **India and Nigeria** (Dexerto, citing Business Insider docs).
- **Cluely:** $0/video + per-thousand-views bonus only. Zero risk of paying for non-performers.
- **Escrow trick** (Variety music piece, 2026): "Agencies often act as an intermediary between clippers and artist teams or labels, **skimming off some of the budget upfront.**" — i.e., upfront retainer + per-view bounty layered together; agency captures spread.

### 2.6 — Anti-fraud beyond Whop's defaults

- **Geographic verification by analytics screenshot:** clipper must screen-record their own TikTok/IG analytics page showing audience % by country, posted to a verification thread. Minimum US thresholds (50–60%) are enforced (clippa.net).
- **Account-age + verification:** linked socials + minimum follower / posting-history floor for top tiers.
- **Lifetime bans on confirmed botting** (Whop's stated rule).
- **Manual sample review:** in-house team double-checks 1–2x daily on top brand-safe campaigns (Clipping Culture's published process).
- **Watermark-traceable footage:** seeding raw footage with subtle frame-level watermarks lets ops know which clipper an unauthorized re-cut came from. Not publicly documented at any of these orgs but is a common technique.
- **Critical critique from inside the industry** — RJO of Clip Central (`@RJO121445`, Mar 2026, https://x.com/RJO121445/status/2032902045632442781):
  > "A major problem with all of the clipping agencies on Discord and Whop is that they aren't selling placements they're selling communities. While they may claim it's 'CPM based,' the accounts are often far from vetted, and someone like 'Timmy9812' can join from a second world country and end up hurting your brand's reputation or deliver low quality results."
  - Counter-positioning: Clip Central books "top 1% of clippers like traditional talent" — i.e., turn the white-glove tier into the only product.
- **Critique from Jaquory Lunsford (Overlap_AI), Apr 30, 2026 (https://x.com/JaquoryLunsford/status/2049916771206066680):**
  > "A lot of clipping agencies are simply arbitraging cheap CPMs, relying on offshore editors, and in some cases manufacturing engagement just to hit campaign minimums."

### 2.7 — Onboarding gates (filter out spam farms)

- **Discord age-of-server / age-of-account requirement** (built into Discord verification levels).
- **Submission-channel gating** behind a `#rules-acknowledged` reaction-role flow.
- **Mandatory linked socials** with min-follower threshold on the higher tiers (typically 1K–5K followers as floor, but Spade Clipping bio explicitly says "no followers needed" for entry tier).
- **Education / course gate:** Clipping Culture and similar offer an "educational course for new creators" before campaign access (NetInfluencer, Apr 2026).
- **Whop application gate:** browse → request to join → seller approves. Whop takes 10% commission.

### 2.8 — Raids / coordinated-drop systems

- **Coordinated drop:** brief gets posted, clippers told to post within X-hour window (often 24–48 hours of source content publishing) so multiple clips hit the algorithm in a tight window.
- **Pre-launch teaser → launch-day mega-raid → sustain raids** — stagger posting across the 9 AM–9 PM UTC window to simulate organic buzz, vary captions, never identical clips.
- Documented case studies:
  - **Adin Ross campaign** (Bloomberg / Several / DigitalMusicNews, Oct 2025): **520 clippers, 11,000 videos, 430M views.** That's ~21 clips per clipper.
  - **John Summit "Lights Go Out"** (Variety, 2026): **$1,050 budget over 8 days → 32.4M views, 1.4M likes across 29 clips.** Top clip alone: 5.3M views. Effective CPM ~$0.03.
  - **bbno$ campaign by Clipping Culture**: $9,000 budget → **190M views**. Near 1B views over 2 months for the artist total.
  - **Cluely (Roy Lee)**: ~700 clippers, 100M views in weeks. Per Roy Lee tweet: "every single cluely-related piece of content gets crossposted across thousands of accounts."
  - **N3on / Adin Ross IRL stream w/ Iggy Azalea** (Dexerto): "No one actually watched the stream. They just saw the clips, and they're like, 'Wow, N3on and Iggy had a great time on this yacht.'" — N3on. Single clips reached **up to 50M views**, while live stream peaked at ~40K.

### 2.9 — Captain / sub-mod hierarchies

- Patterns synthesized across operator threads (Lumina, Clip Central, Cluely, Omar @Omar_organic):
  - **Founder / agency owner** → **CCO / ops director** → **captains (1 per 50–100 clippers)** → **clippers** → **subcontractors** (clippers hire their own editors offshore).
  - Faisal Ali (Pakistan, age 23) example from Bloomberg: subcontracted 2 editors and split his $600 MrBeast payout. This bottom-of-stack subcontracting layer is invisible to the agency but very common.
  - Kanoah Cunningham (24, NPR/Bloomberg): leads team of 8 editors, earns up to **$30,000/mo** as a captain-tier operator under Clipping.
- **Payment to captains:** typically ~20% commission on squad earnings (synthesized from operator chatter — not officially published).
- **Chief Clipping Officer** as a formalized title is being adopted (Ollie Forsyth essential-roles thread; Clippster_ai promotion announcement).

### 2.10 — Content-supply problem (the bottleneck)

Roy Lee / Cluely thread, July 2025 — this is the one quote nobody talks about but matters:
> "make sure you have enough content to clip. i go on at least 2 podcasts weekly. everyone at cluely creates content. our clippers clip everything. **the biggest reason not to have 400 clippers is because you don't have 400 clips a day to post.** make more content."
> — https://x.com/im_roy_lee/status/1945753991180365893

Implication for VURT: a 4K-clipper Discord is useless without 200+ clips/day of source footage. VURT's vertical-drama format actually solves this — every episode is a bottomless source.
