## Follow-up if they engage

The structural question Clip Ship will ask: "What does success look like for VURT?" Answer:

- **Volume KPI:** clipper-driven views attributable to VURT-tagged clips on TikTok/Reels/Shorts
- **Quality KPI:** average watch-through % on clipper output (we want ≥30%, that's the floor at which we pay CPM)
- **Brand KPI:** % of clipper output that includes myvurt.com or @myvurt on-screen for ≥2s
- **Conversion proxy:** UTM-tagged web clicks per clip (won't be huge — short-form-to-web CTR is 0.5–2%, we know that)

What we DON'T measure success on: signups, app installs, paywall conversions. We're AVOD. Views are the revenue event.

## What to NOT say

- Don't lead with "first vertical-drama clipper program" framing in the email — Clip Ship already does Netflix and music. They don't need our novelty pitch; they need to know we have a real IP catalog and budget.
- Don't ask for a discount upfront. Get a number first, then negotiate exclusivity for rate.
- Don't compare to Clipping Culture. Different positioning — Clip Ship is upmarket.
