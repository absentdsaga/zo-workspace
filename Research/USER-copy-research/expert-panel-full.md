## QUESTION: What's the core promise that makes builders want to pay?

### Solana Mobile Expert:
> "The promise isn't testing. It's **getting real users before your competitors do**. 
> - Genesis holders = your early adopter pool
> - First-mover advantage in their heads
> - "They tried it before anyone else" = fundraising line"

### Top Sales Copywriter:
> "Eugene Schwartz would say: what's the **deepest desire**? 
> It's not 'get feedback.' It's **'launch with confidence.'**
> 
> Every builder has shipped something and hoped. The pain is the hope. The promise is certainty.
> 
> Headline: 'Ship with confidence. Not hope.'
> 
> That's 10x stronger than 'Talk to your users before they talk about you.'"

### Buyer Psychology Expert:
> "Two psychological drivers at play:
> 1. **Loss aversion** — they fear a bad launch more than they want a good one
> 2. **Competitor anxiety** — 'what if someone else launches first?'
> 
> The headline should tap both. 'Ship with confidence' hits loss aversion. 'Before they talk about you' taps competitor anxiety.
> 
> But I'd lead with the POSITIVE outcome, not the fear. Confidence > fear."

### Product Positioning Expert:
> "You're not a testing service. You're a **launch platform**.
> 
> Positioning: 'The pre-launch platform for mobile-first crypto apps.'
> 
> Testing is the feature. Traction is the product."

### Web3 Native Marketer:
> "Keep it real. Builders here can smell marketing from a mile away.
> 
> Don't oversell. Say what you do: 'Real users test your app. You get the truth.'
> 
> Simple. Direct. No fluff."

### B2B SaaS Copywriter:
> "The 8-second rule: headline must work in isolation.
> 
> Current best:
> - 'Ship with confidence. Not hope.' ← strong, outcome-focused
> - 'Real users. Real feedback. Real launch.' ← tripling works
> - 'Test with Genesis holders. Launch with proof.' ← ecosystem-first

## QUESTION: What value props matter most to these buyers?

### Buyer Psychology Expert:
> "Ranked by psychological weight:
> 1. **Traction evidence** — for investors (dApp Store reviews)
> 2. **Risk reduction** — don't launch broken
> 3. **Competitive edge** — first in front of real users
> 4. **Convenience** — we handle everything

> The money reason is #1. Every builder raising needs to show 'people actually use this.'"

### Top Sales Copywriter:
> "The 'So What?' test on each value prop:
> - 'Bug reports' → So what? → 'You fix what's broken'
> - 'dApp Store reviews' → So what? → 'Proof of traction for investors'
> - 'Real devices' → So what? → 'What works on simulator fails on real phones'

> Lead with So What #2 (investors). Money talks."

### Solana Mobile Expert:
> "On Solana specifically:
> - dApp Store reviews ARE visible
> - First 3 reviews matter a lot for social proof
> - Genesis holders = recognized as 'real users' by the community
> - Builders know this. They care about this."

## QUESTION: How do we handle pricing without underselling?

### B2B SaaS Copywriter:
> "Never show prices below your floor. If $5K is minimum, $5K is the lowest number on the page.
> 
> Use anchoring:
> - Show $15K package as 'Most Popular'
> - $5K exists as 'Starter'
> - Nobody picks Starter when they see what's included in Most Popular"

### Buyer Psychology Expert:
> "Price is a signal of quality. Too low = 'they must not be good.'
> 
> $5K-15K range is fine for B2B. But anchor at $15K so $5K feels 'accessible' not 'cheap.'"

### Top Sales Copywriter:
> "Don't show prices publicly at all — use 'Contact us' for serious deals.
> 
> Or: Show $15K as anchor, let them negotiate down to $10K. They feel like they won.
> 
> Never give away your floor price in copy."

## QUESTION: Social proof — what works for this audience?

### Web3 Native Marketer:
> "Community trust > corporate logos.
> 
> What works:
> - "Tested by builders like you"
> - Specific names: '@miloonchain tested with us', '@dubsdotapp tested with us'
> - Numbers: '11+ dApps tested'
> - Screenshots of actual feedback/reports (if client approves)"

### Solana Mobile Expert:
> "Builders in this space know each other. Namedropping works.
> 
> 'We tested for MiloOnChain. We tested for Dubs.' >> 'Trusted by leading projects'
> 
> The latter sounds generic. The former sounds real."

### Buyer Psychology Expert:
> "Social proof hierarchy:
> 1. Named peers (highest trust)
> 2. Numbers (social validation)
> 3. Credentials (but only if recognized)
> 
> '11+ dApps' is good. 'MiloOnChain' is better."
