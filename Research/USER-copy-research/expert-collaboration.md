## EXPERT 1: Solana Mobile Expert

**What do Saga/Seeker builders actually need? What keeps them up at night?**

> The real pain isn't testing — it's **distribution**. The dApp Store is tiny. Every review is visible. Your first 10 reviews define your trajectory. Builders know this. They're scared to launch and get buried under apps with better reviews.
>
> Second pain: **wallet UX**. Mobile wallet flows are different from desktop. Builders need real Saga/Seeker users to test actual wallet interactions, not emulators. Simulator testing misses real-world friction.
>
> Third: **investor evidence**. Raising in this market requires traction proof. dApp Store reviews are verifiable on-chain — that's social proof investors actually trust.
>
> **What resonates:** "real devices," "Genesis holders," "dApp Store reviews." These are specific. They know exactly what they're getting.
>
> **What they hate:** generic beta testing. They've tried Discord betas. They got noise, not signal. They want verified users, not randos who download and review-bomb.

## EXPERT 3: B2B SaaS Copywriter

**What's the headline that converts? What's the pricing strategy?**

> **Headline:**
> > "Launch with confidence. Not excuses."
> 
> Or:
> > "Get your first 50 users before your first review."
>
> Both lead with outcome, not feature.
>
> **Hero structure:**
> - Headline: Outcome
> - Subhead: Proof + specific promise
> - CTA: Single, clear action
>
> **Pricing:** $5K is right. Show it on the page. "Campaign — $5,000 USDC" anchors the value. Don't hide it in a form. Show it proudly.
>
> **Conversion killers to avoid:**
> - "Submit your app" — sounds like homework
> - "Learn more" — no commitment
> - Too many words — builders scan, they don't read

## EXPERT 4: Web3 Native Marketer

**Does this sound like it's from the community? What triggers skepticism?**

> **Voice check:** Does it sound like a builder talking to builders? Yes if:
> - "Genesis Token holders" — community term
> - "dApp Store" — their store
> - "Saga builders" — how they refer to themselves
>
> **Avoid:**
> - "platform" — too corporate
> - "verified testers" — sounds like a feature list

> **On-chain proof works:**
> - "11 dApps tested" — specific, real
> - "Genesis holders" — on-chain verifiable
> - "dApp Store reviews" — public, visible
>
> **The @dubsdotapp tweet is gold.** Real builder endorsement. Keep that.
